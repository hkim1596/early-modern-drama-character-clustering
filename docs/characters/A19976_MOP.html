ſgod mend mee Ile let fall or take vp any thing I haue to doe him good. O are you aviſd of that, twold make a horſe breake his bridle to heare how the youth of the village will commend me, oh the pre tie little pincking nyes of Mopſa ſaies one, oh the fine flat lippes of Mopſa ſaies another, and then doe I bridle my head like a malt-horſe thus, ſet mine armes a kembo thus, wrethe my necke and my bodie thus, winke with one eye thus, & ſpread my peacocks tayle as broad as the proudeſt minx of em all. Godſme, my mo thers ſtealing of a nap. Why what ſhould I do ma dam, my mother tells me I muſt not ſay as I think. And what ſhould I doe then? Do not you flout me Dorus, & you do not, prouide a prieſt and Ile marry you, and my father and mother ſhal neuer know one. And Ile be the woman, who ſo euer ſay nay toote, little dreames my mother of what wee haue done. Tis wel ſhe was ſo quiet, for ſhe eate peaſe poridge to break faſt, & theyle make me break wind in my ſleepe like a horſe, and ſee as the deuil wil hate ſhe wakes, and here comes my father, no words and ye loue me. Nay now you flout me? Trickes, no as god mend me, and I should not haue a huſ band till I got him with tricks, I should lead apes in hell: but faith tell me, doſt thou loue me Dorus? If thou doſt not hit the marke, then thou'rt a very bung ler: but where is my Father? I but you ieaſt, I doubt you will not marrie me. As I am a Virgin I will. As I hope to be a wife I will. As I did the loſſe of my Maydenhead. Many a deere day agoe, yet I told Nobody on't but my Mother and our Horſekeeper, and they ſay I am nere the worſe mayd for that, and I can keepe my owne counſell, as I hope I ſhal; but will you meet me ſoone? Ile goe afore and ſtay, but doe not deceiue me, and you doe, Ile ſhew my Fathers Horſekeeper all as God mend me. whoſe there Manaſſes. plain I might be madam Mopſa in your mouth, good man &c. whers Dorus. not come, a peſcod on him, but als one I thought at firſt he would make but a foole on me. But choſe him, ſince he hath buld me with an vrchin, ile goe fetch Raph our horſkeeper, let him that got the calfe keep the cow in a knaues name and he wil, ha you your booke heere. Nay and ye can dote wel inough your ſelfe, I care for neither of them both, but indeed I loue to haue a thing wel done, for ſaies my mother, a thinge once wel done, is twice done, and I am in her mind for that vp and downe, yes, whoſe there? Dorus. no by my troth not I? Did ye not ſee Dorus. I ſhall run franticke and I find not Dorus.

Plot — The Isle of Gulls
Three members of the audience ask the Prologue about the play they are to see, The Isle of Gulls. They prove to have irreconcilable tastes in drama: one wants satire, one bawdy, and one bombast, but all eventually agree to give the play a chance. Concerned about his daughters’ unsuitable suitors, Basilius retires with them for a year to an uninhabited, fortified island, leaving his brother to rule Arcadia in his stead. A proclamation challenges royal would-be suitors to win the princesses by stealth. This attracts Julio and Amintor to the island, alerted by Basilius’ unworthy favourite Dametas; and also Lysander and Demetrius, respectively disguised as Zelmane, an Amazon, and Dorus, a woodman. ‘Zelmane’ becomes Violetta’s companion, and Demetrius enters Dametas’ service. Dametas arranges an opportunity for Julio and Amintor to rape the princesses by isolating them during a hunt. They kidnap the girls, but ‘Zelmane’ and ‘Dorus’ rescue them. ‘Dorus’ is rewarded with an appointment as Hippolita’s attendant, but the incident also casts doubt on ‘Zelmane’s’ gender, so Lysander loses his access to Violetta. Basilius and Gynecia are both enamoured of Lysander (each believing him to be a different sex), and each persuades him to accept the other’s advances for sport. Meanwhile Dametas’ daughter Mopsa falls in love with ‘Dorus’. Amintor and Julio continue to have ill success: they pose as envoys from Basilius’ brother, appealing for him to deal with an outbreak of abuses, corruption, and crime, but they are denied their promised reward by the avaricious Dametas. ‘Dorus’ gets rid of Dametas and his family by sending each of them on a wild-goose chase: Dametas to find buried treasure, his jealous wife Miso to Dametas’ supposed mistress, and Mopsa to the chapel in Adonis’ bower where a priest will marry them. ‘Zelmane’ similarly disposes of Basilius and Gynecia by arranging assignations with each at the same chapel, with the priest Manasses standing in for the Amazon. This frees Lysander and Demetrius to elope with the princesses; but their plans are overheard by Amintor and Julio, whom they then employ to smuggle the girls off the island.
