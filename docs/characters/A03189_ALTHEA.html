Full speech

They are of our line, Of which no branch did euer perish yet, By Cankers, blastings, or dry barrennesse. But Meleager let me turne to thee, Whose birth the Fates themselues did calculate, 'Twas thus: the very instant thou wast borne, The sisters, that draw, spinne, and clip our liues, Entred my chamber with a fatall brand, Which hurling in the fire, thus said: One day, one date, Betide this brand and childe, euen be their fate. So parted they, the brand begins to burne: And as it wasted, so didst thou consume; Which I perceiuing, leap't vnto the flame, And quenching that, stayd thy consumption. The brand I (as a iewell) haue reseru'd, And keepe it in a casket, lock't as safe As in thy bosome thou maintainst thy heart. Speake, how slaine? By Meleagers, how? vpon what quarrell? Could the proud boy ground such a damned act. My sorrowes turne to rage, my teares to fire, My praiers to curses, vowes into reuenge. Althea what distraction's this within thee? A sister or a mother wilt thou bee? Since both I cannot, (for these Princes slaine) Sister I chuse, a mothers name disdaine: The fatall brand in which the murderers life Securely lies, I'le hurle into the fire And as it flames, so shall the slaue expire. Mischeife I'le heape on mischeife, bad on ill, Wrong pay with wrongs, and slaughter these that kill. And since the Gods would all our glories thrall, I will with them haue chiefe hand in our fall. But hee's my sonne: oh pardon me deere brothers, Being a mother if I spare his life, Though it bee fit his sinne be plaug'd with death, And that his life lie in yon fatall brand, 'T will not come fitly from a mothers hand. Is this the hope of all my ten months paine, Must he by th'hand of him that nurst him now be slaine? Would he had perisht in his cradle, when I gaue him twice life: in his birth, and then When I the brand snatcht from the rauenous flame, And for this double good, hast thou with shame And iniury repaide me? I will now A sister be, no mother, for I vow Reuenge and death; Furies, assist my hand Whilst in red flames I cast his vitall brand. Assist my rage you sterne Eumenides, To you this blacke deed will I consecrate. Pitty away, hence thou consanguine loue, Maternall zeale, peccentall piety. All cares, loues, duties, offices, affections, That grow 'tweene sonnes and mothers, leaue this place; Let none but furies, murders, paracides, Be my assistants in this dam'd attempt: All that's good and honest, I confine, Blacke is my purpose; Hell my thoughts are thine. Drinke, quaffe, be blith; oh how this festiue ioy Stirs vp my fury to reuenge and death, Thus, thus, (you Gods aboue, abiect your eies From this vnnaturall act) the murderer dies. What hast thou done Althea? stay thy fury, And bring not these strange torments on thine owne Thou hast too much already, backe my hand, And saue his life as thou conferust this brand. Shall mirth and ioy crowne his degenerate head? Whilst his cold Vnkles on the earth lie spread? No, wretehed youth whilst this hand can destroy, I'le cut thee off in midst of all thy ioy. Burne, perish, wast, fire, sparkle, and consume And all thy vitall spirits flie with this fume. So, now'tis done, The brand consum'd, his vitall threed quite spun. She shall not, Iason no, She shall not. Do you wonder Lords of Greece, To see this Prince lye dead? why that's no nouell, All men must dye, thou, he, and euery one, Yea I my selfe must: but Il'e tell you that Shall stiffe your haire, your eyes start from heads, Print fixt amazement in your wondring fronts, Yea and astonish all: This was my sonne, Borne with sick throws, nurst from my tender brest Brought vp with femine care, cherisht with loue: His youth, my pride; his honour all my wishes, So deere, that little lesse he was then life. But will you know the wonder ('lasse) too true, Him (all my sonnes) this my inrag'd hand slue, This hand, that Dians quenchlesse rage to fill, Shall with the slaine sonnes sword the mother kill.

Plot summary

Plot — The Brazen Age (The Age of Brass)
Hercules and the shape-changing Achelous fight for the hand of Dejanira; Achelous transforms himself into a series of monsters, but Hercules retains the upper hand, and Achelous concedes defeat after one of his bull’s horns is pulled off (later to become the cornucopia). Hercules takes Dejanira home, and en route they meet Nessus, the only survivor of Hercules’ massacre of the centaurs. Feigning friendship, the vindictive Nessus offers to carry Dejanira across the river on his back; but when he gets to the other side, he attempts to abduct her. Hercules shoots him and, dying, he tells Dejanira that his blood is a love-charm, which she should use on Hercules if he ever seems interested in another woman; in fact, it is poisonous. Hercules travels to Egypt, where King Busiris sacrifices foreigners, and kills him on his own altar, but refuses to accept his crown. During his absence, Diana, offended that King Oeneus has sacrificed to all the gods but her, sends a boar to ravage the Calydonian countryside. The lords of Greece gather to hunt the boar. One of them is Adonis, and Venus falls in love with him; he insists on hunting, the boar kills him, and she transforms him into a flower. Atalanta joins the hunt, and Meleager falls in love with her; when the boar is killed, he is awarded its head, but he offends his companions by presenting it to her. In the ensuing fight, Meleager kills his uncles. His mother Althea is torn between her duty to her brothers and to her son. Meleager’s life is in her power, for, when he was born, the Fates visited her, pointed out a brand in the fire, and foretold that the child would live until the brand was burnt up; she rescued it and has kept it safe ever since. Now she burns the brand, and he dies; in remorse, she kills herself with his sword. Hercules joins Jason and the Argonauts on their quest to retrieve the golden fleece from Colchis. On the way they stop off at Troy, where King Laomedon faces an unwelcome obligation to sacrifice his daughter Hesione: he accepted a loan from Neptune’s priests to finance building the city walls, but failed to repay it when the work was complete, and retribution came in the form of a sea monster who must be appeased with the monthly sacrifice of a young Trojan woman; this month the fatal lot has fallen on Hesione.
