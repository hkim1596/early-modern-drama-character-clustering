Once I encountred him, and thus I ſaid: Thou Maiden youth, be vanquiſht by a Maide. But with a proud Maieſticall high ſcorne He anſwer'd thus: Yong Talbot was not borne To be the pillage of a Giglot Wench: So ruſhing in the bowels of the French, He left me proudly, as vnworthy fight, Heere's a ſilly ſtately ſtile indeede: The Turke that two and fiftie Kingdomes hath, Writes not ſo tedious a Stile as this. Him that thou magnifi'ſt with all theſe Titles, Stinking and fly-blowne lyes heere at our feete. The Regent conquers, and the Frenchmen flye. Now helpe ye charming Spelles and Periapts, And ye choiſe ſpirits that admoniſh me, And giue me ſignes of future accidents. You ſpeedy helpers, that are ſubſtitutes Vnder the Lordly Monarch of the North, Appeare, and ayde me in this enterprize. This ſpeedy and quicke appearance argues proofe Of your accuſtom'd diligence to me. Now ye Familiar Spirits, that are cull'd Out of the powerfull Regions vnder earth, Helpe me this once, that France may get the field. Oh hold me not with ſilenee ouer-long: Where I was wont to feed you with my blood, Ile lop member off, and giue it you, In earneſt of a further benefit: So you do condiſcend to helpe me now. No hope to haue redreſſe? My body ſhall Pay recompence, if you will graunt my ſuite. Cannot my body, nor blood-ſacrifice, Intreate you to your wonted furtherance? Then take my ſoule; my body, ſoule, and all, Before that England giue the French the foyle. See, they forſake me. Now the time is come, That France muſt vale her lofty plumed Creſt, And let her head fall into Englands lappe. My ancient Incantations are too weake, And hell too ſtrong for me to buckle with: Now France, thy glory droopcth to the duſt. Chang'd to a worſer ſhape thou canſt not be: A plaguing miſcheefe light on Charles, and thee, And may ye both be ſodainly ſurpriz'd By bloudy hands, in ſleeping on your beds. I prethee giue me leaue to curſe awhile. Firſt let me tell you whom you haue condemn'd; Not me, begotten of a Shepheard Swaine, But iſſued from the Progeny of Kings. Vertuous and Holy, choſen from aboue, By inſpiration of Celeſtiall Grace, To worke exceeding myracles on earth. I neuer had to do with wicked Spirits. But you that are polluted with your luſtes, Stain'd with the guiltleſſe blood of Innocents, Corrupt and tainted with a thouſand Vices: Becauſe you want the grace that others haue, You iudge it ſtraight a thing impoſsible To compaſſe Wonders, but by helpe of diuels. No miſconceyued, Ione of Aire hath beene A Virgin from her tender infancie, Chaſte, and immaculate in very thought, Whoſe Maiden-blood thus rigorouſly effus'd, Will cry for Vengeance, at the Gates of Heauen. Will nothing turne your vnrelenting hearts? Then Ione diſcouet thine infirmity, That wartanteth by Law, to be thy priuiledge. I am with childe ye bloody Homicides: Murther not then the Fruite within my Wombe, Although ye hale me to a violent death. You are deceyu'd, my childe is none of his, It was Alanſon that inioy'd my loue. Oh giue me leaue, I haue deluded you, 'Twas neyther Charles, nor yet the Duke I nam'd, But Reignier King of Naples that preuayl'd.

Plot summary

Plot — 1 Henry the Sixth
King Henry Vis dead, and England's French lands are lost. After a military setback, the French accept the offered aid of Joan Pucelle , who claims supernatural powers from the Virgin Mary; but they also release Talbot in an exchange of prisoners. With Joan 's help the French recapture the occupied town of Orleans, but the English take it back in a night attack led by Talbot. The Countess of Auvergne invites Talbot to visit her. intending treachery, but he second-guesses her and brings soldiers with him. In England, feuds brew between Gloucester and Winchester, and between Richard Plantagenet and the Duke of Somerset, whose supporters show their allegiance by picking different coloured roses. Richard , who has been deprived of his titles through the attainder of his father, learns from the imprisoned and dying Mortimer that he has a claim to the throne . King Henry restores him to his inheritance, creating him Duke of York. Joan takes Rouen by a ruse , but Talbot recaptures it the same day; she then persuades England's ally Burgundy to join the French. After King Henry's coronation in Paris, York is left in command of Prance, but personal tension between him and Somerset means that troop reinforcements arrive late; in consequence, Talbot is defeated, surviving only long enough to learn that his son has perished in battle. Joan conjures devils, but they will not help her: she is captured by York and burned at the stake. The French and English agree a peace which will entail King Henry making a diplomatic marriage, but this is pre-empted when Suffolk captures Margaret and (having fallen in love with her but being alr eady married) plans to install her as Queen of England . Henry agrees to marry her, and Suffolk goes to France to mili the arrangements.
