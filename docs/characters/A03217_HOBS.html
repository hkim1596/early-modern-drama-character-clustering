Hyday, bones a mee, here's lazy knaues. Past eight a clocke, and neither ware sorted, Not shop swept. Besides, thou canst not keepe open shop here, because thou art a forrainer, by the lawes of the Realme. I but sirra, Ile haue none of my English Prentises Frenchefied; bones a me knaue, Ile haue thee deale with no such broken Commodities. What sayst thou knaue? Bones of me, a Haberdasher of small wares. No, what is she then Iohn? Bones a me knaue I cannot speake a word of French. Madam, I crie you mercie for this wrong Done to your Ladyship, I did suspect you For a bad liuer, but I see you cleare, For which mistake, I doe remaine your seruant. How, would you my gray Mare see? If it like your Ladyship, I came by water, and neither of Mares backe, nor horse backe. No indeed Ladie, my name is not Frauneis, your ser uant and Iohn Hobson. No points; yes indeed Ladie, I haue points at my hose, though I goe vntrust. I haue no points in my parlour indeede, but I haue a hundred pounds worth in my shop. A search, and this a bawdie house! why Iohn, Bones a me knaue, How comes this to passe? How, must you haue money of me? Ile know where fore first, by your leaues. How, must I goe to prison for doing amisse? Bones a me Iohn, is not this a Ladie? Is not this Gentlewoman a dealer? And hath she not a good commoditie? Hath she not ware? And may not then a chapman deale with her? Bones a me Iohn, what Iohn? why honest Iohn? No more good Iohn. Swee honest Iohn, why bones a me knaue Iohn. Nay, bones a me Iohn, friends, sweet Iohn all friends; I doe confesse tha'st o'reacht thy master. Ca me, ca thee, conceale this from my wife, And Ile keepe all thy knauerie from thine vncle. Nay, bones a me, Ile take you at your word, Besides I hope these honest Gentlemen Will saue my credite. Tis Logicke to me sir, I vnderstand you not. God a mercie Gentlemen, God a mercie Iohn, but bones a me knaue, where are their lodgings? I hope they'le bring me to no more bawdie houses, I would not be taken napping againe for two and one: But Gentlemen Ile accept of curtesie, and then Iohn You shall with me to England, weele shew Fraunce Our backs. And you will needs deale for your selfe, Afore your time, you shall doo't in England. VVill you walke Gentlemen? Good morrow Master Doctor; my good Ladie! Bones a me woman, thou look'st sad to day, Thou hast not drunke a cup of sacke this morning. Gods blessing of your heart, need must be fed, Let vs that haue it giue the hungrie bread. My new elected master of the Hospitall, What hasty newes with you? Where? bones a me why? speake, why? Bones man, tis not so. A horse, a horse, cart-horse, mault horse, anything To saue the knaues life, I protest, I sweare This was the first time that I heard the knaue Hath beene in any trouble, bones of mee, T'was done without my knowledge.
