Reach at it, and if thine arme be too ſhort, Mine ſhall lengthen it. Art not thou a Prince, Vnckle to the King and his Protector? Then what ſhouldſt thou lacke that might content thy mind? What drempt my lord? Good Humphrey tel it me, And ile interpret it, and when thats done, Ile tell thee then what I did dreame to night. Tuſh my Lord, this ſignifies nought but this, That he that breakes a ſticke of Gloſters groue, Shall for th'offence make forfeit of his head. But now my lord, ile tell you what I dreampt, Me thought I was in the Cathedrall Church At Weſtminſter, and ſeated in the chaire Where the Kings and Queenes are crownde, and at my feete Henry and Margaret with a crowne of gold, Stood ready to ſet it on my princely head. How now my Lord! what, angry with your Nell For telling but her dreame? the next I haue Ile keepe to my ſelfe and not be rated thus. Ile come after you, for I cannot go before, But ere it be long ile go before them all, Deſpight of all that ſeeke to croſſe me thus, Who is within there? VVhat ſir Iohn Hum, what newes with you?
