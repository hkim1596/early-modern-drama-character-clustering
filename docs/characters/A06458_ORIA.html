Faith brother I must needs goe yonder. I know the Lady Honoria will be glad to see mee. I, but they say one shall see fine fights at the Court. Why, and are not these fine sights? Apt? To what? I would goe, if it were but only to shew you, that I could be there, and be mou'd with none of these trickes. An houre since Farewell Brother. My Lord, I was bold to craue the helpe of your house against the storme. Ha, ha, ha. I thanke you good Brother: and if you doubt of of the cause of my comming, I can Satisfie you. He satisfie you brother, for I see you are iealous of mee. Yes brother, and not before. I'me very wel my Lord, you do mistake me, if you thinke I affect greater company then your selfe. Your Lordship may descant vpon your owne be hauior as please you, but I protest, so sweet and courtly it appeares in my eye, that I meane not to leaue you yet. A rough carriage is best in a man, Ile dine with you my Lord. I haue heard of your Lordships nothing, Ile put that to the venture. Ile keepe no state my Lord, neither doe I mourne, Ile dine with you. I would your Lordship did but see how well This furie doth become you, it doth shew So neere the life, as it were naturall. I must not leaue this fellow, I will torment him to madnes, To teach his passions against kind to moue, The more he hates, the more Ile seeme to loue. Deare Lord: y'are too wild. I doe confesse I am to easie, too much woman, Not coy enough to take affection, Yet I can frowne and nip a passion Euen in the bud: I can say Men please their present heates; Then please to leaue vs. I can hold off, and by my Chimmick power Draw Sonnets, from the melting louers braine. Aymees, and Elegies : yet to you my Lord My Loue, my better selfe, I put these off, Doing that office, not befits our sex, Entreat a man to loue; Are ye not yet relenting, ha'ye bloud and Spirit In those veines, ye are no Image, though yee bee as hard. As marble, sure ye haue no lyuer, if ye had, 'T would send a liuely and desiring heate To euery member; is not this miserable, A thing so truely formd, shapt out by Symetry, Has all the organs that belong to man, And working to, yet to shew all these Like dead motions mouing, vpon wyers, Then good my Lord, leaue off what you haue beene, And freely be what you were first entended for: a man. Then ile hate you. Let me with your faire patience, sit beside you? I cannot thinke your better knowledge can vse a woman so vnciuilly. Tis but your outside that you shew: I know your Neuer was guiltie of so great a weaknes, Or could the tongues of all men ioynd togeather Possesse me with a thought of your dislike My weaknes were aboue a womans, to fall off From my affection, for one cracke of thunder, O wo'd you could loue my Lord. I had rather sing at dores for bread, then sing to this fellow, but for hate: if this should be told in the Court, that I begin to woe Lords, what a troop of the vntrust no bilitie should I haue at my lodging tomorrow morning. Come sleepe, and with thy sweet deceiuing, Lock me in delight a while, Let some pleasing Dreames beguile All my fancies that from thence, Song. I may feele an influence, All my powers of care bereauing. Though but a shaddow, but a sliding, Let me know some little Ioy, We that suffer long anoy Are contented with a thought Through an idle fancie wrought O let my ioyes, haue some abiding. God keepe your Lordship. Ile be the Clarke and crie Amen, Your Lordships euer assured enemie Oriana. Plot summary

Plot — The Woman Hater (The Hungry Courtier)
Oriana is on her way to visit the Duke’s court when she is caught in a hail-storm, and takes refuge in the house of Gondarino. Gondarino is a confirmed misogynist, and makes her duly unwelcome. The Duke, on his way to visit Oriana at home, is also caught in the storm and he too shelters in Gondarino’s house. Gondarino suspects that he is unwittingly being made the host of an illicit assignation (and indeed the Duke is attracted to Oriana). Oriana takes umbrage at Gondarino’s attitude to women, and pretends to pursue him amorously in an effort to torment him out of it. He sends her to stay in a bawdy-house, and tells the Duke that she is unchaste. Her brother Valore disputes this, and Gondarino is suspected of slander. To defend himself, he shows her to the Duke in her new lodgings, but his story is discredited when it emerges that he sent her there himself. The Duke sends the courtier Arrigo to kill Oriana, but he offers to spare her in return for sex. She refuses, and thereby proves her chastity: the Duke was testing her, and she now accepts his proposal
