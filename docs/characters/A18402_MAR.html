Lord what straite keepers of poore maides are you, You are so chast you are the worse agayne: And I trustie seruant. A mightie venture you shall be cronicled in Abra hams asses, Catalog of cockscombes for your resolution. A prettie morrall work it in the samplar of your hart, I thinke so vnlesse you haue alone in your belly. Nay if you be so fine you will not name it now, VVe are all alone, you are much too nice. Do then Elimine. VVhy woman I dare. Ile warrant my selfe if I list, but come let it alone, Let vs to Irus for our fortunes. VVhat skill for places, do we not all call sisters, A what you call it can you not name it. Oh he is a Count that is an Earle. I but my husband is the kings officer. And me thinkes husband is worth ten o … Lord. VVell Madam then and please your Ladishippe, VVhat gownes and head tyres will your honor weare. Oh braue God willing I will haue the like. Well if my husband will not, let him not loke for one good looke of me. Ile aske my husband when I am with child, And then I know I shall be sped I fayth. But euery pleasure hath a payne they say My husband lies each other nyght abrode, VVell time I hope and change of companie, VVill teach vs somewhat to beare out the absence, Come on sweete husband fot my time drawes neare.

Plot summary

Plot — 146
Jehovah sends John the Baptist to proclaim the coming of Christ in accordance with ancient prophecy. Having baptised Christ, John turns his attention to King Herod. The Pharisees, who feel threatened by John’s preaching, instigate an inquiry; but Herod is satisfied that he is not an evil man, and consents to see him. However, the Pharisees have an ally in Herod’s wife, Herodias: John has spoken against their happy but illicit marriage, and she fears being sent back to her despised first husband. Using her sexual allure, she persuades Herod to imprison John, but Herod will not put him to death. From his prison cell, John sends his disciples to find out whether Jesus is the promised saviour; they report back that he is. Herodias visits him, but cannot persuade him to recant. Herod celebrates his birthday with a feast. Tryphera’s dancing pleases him so much that he swears to give her whatever she desires. Herodias puts her up to asking for John’s severed head on a charger. Herod obliges; nobody tries to dissuade him. Jehovah declares that good will come of John’s death. Both Herodias and Herod feel guilty; Herod gives John’s body to his disciples for burial.
