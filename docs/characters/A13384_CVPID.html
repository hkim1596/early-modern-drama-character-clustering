W Ho would beleeue that in this human forme, And vnder theſe meane Shepherds weedes, A god head? nor yet of the lower ranke, But the moſt mighty 'mong the gods; whoſe powre were hid Makes oft the bloudy Sword of angry Mars Fall from his hand; ſterne Neptune hurle away His powerfull trident; and great Ioue lay by His thunderbolt: and thus attyr'de, I hope My mother Venus ſhall haue much adoe To find her Cupid. For the troth to tell, Sh'has made me play the run-away with her: Becauſe (forſooth) ſhee will ſole miſtreſſe bee, And to her pleaſure binde my ſhafts and mee; And (vaine ambitious woman as ſhee is) Would tye me to liue ſtil 'mongſt Crownes, & Scep ters, And to high Courts confine my power and me; And to my vnder-followers graunts to liue Here in theſe woods; and to aduance their powres, Ore ſilly Shepherds breſts; but I that am No childe, (though childiſh be my gate and lookes) Will for this once, doe as ſhall pleaſe me beſt: For not to her, but me allotted were The euer awfull brande, and goulden bowe: Therefore I purpoſe to conceale my ſelfe, And runne from her entreates; (for other powre Then to intreate, ſhee ſhall not haue ore me:) I heare ſhee haunts theſe groues, and promiſeth Vnto the Nymphes and Shepherds, which of them Will bring me to her, kiſſes for their paines, And more then kiſſes too; and cannot I To them ſhall hide me from her, liberall be Of kiſſes, and more too as well as ſhee? The Nymphes I know will like my kiſſes beſt, When I ſhall woe them that am god of loue: Therefore my mother doth but looze her paine, Here's none will bring her home her ſonne againe. Plot summary

Plot — Aminta
Resenting his mother’s efforts to control his activities and limit them to persons of high estate, Cupid absconds to live as a shepherd and make Silvia fall in love. Silvia was Aminta’s companion in boyhood. Three years ago he declared his love for her, and she has refused to see him ever since. Despite Daphne’s attempts to persuade her that she should take her amorous pleasures while she can, Silvia has no maternal desires: she loves only hunting, and hates Aminta because he loves her. Recognizing that Aminta needs to be more masterful, Daphne and Thyrsis arrange for him to encounter Silvia while she is bathing. But Silvia has another rejected admirer with similar ideas, a Satyr, and when Aminta arrives he finds Silvia stark naked, tied to a tree with her own hair, and about to be raped. He drives the Satyr off, but Silvia is haughtily ungracious and runs away. Daphne restrains Aminta’s suicidal impulses. Silvia borrows some clothes from Nerina and goes hunting. She encounters a wolf and chases it; Nerina follows, loses her, and then sees a wolf-pack licking blood off the ground, with Silvia’s bloodstained veil nearby. When Aminta hears of this, his suicidal impulses are renewed: with the help of Ergastus, he climbs a cliff and throws himself off. Silvia is not really dead — the wolves’ prey was only an animal — but the news of Aminta’s death prompts her to recognize her love for him, and seek to join him in the afterlife. It turns out that he is not dead: he fell into a bush, stunned himself, and recovers on feeling Silvia’s tears splashing onto his face.
