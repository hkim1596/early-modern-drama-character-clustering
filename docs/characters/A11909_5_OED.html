Plot summary

Plot — Oedipus (Seneca's Fifth Tragedy)
Plague ravages Thebes. Apollo’s oracle declares that the sickness will subside with the banishment of the murderer of King Laius. Oedipus, who has also received mysterious instructions to flee his mother’s bed, intends to comply with the gods’ command: he curses the murderer, wishing incest and parricide upon him. He consults Tiresias, who conjures up the ghost of Laius. The ghost names Oedipus as the murderer of his own father; but Oedipus’ parents are still alive, and he proposes to produce them in evidence. Oedipus imprisons his brother-in-law Creon, even though Creon insists that he has no ambition to depose the King. Ten years before, Oedipus killed a man who was trying to stop his chariot; now he realizes that this was King Laius. He is recalled to take the throne of Corinth after the death of his father; but he is reluctant to go for fear of fulfilling his own curse by marrying his mother. He then learns that he was an adopted child: he was the son of Laius, whom Phorbas the shepherd was commissioned to expose in infancy; but Phorbas gave him to a Corinthian instead. Oedipus realizes that he has indeed committed parricide and incest. Overcome with guilt, he blinds himself. The suicidal Jocasta has him hold a sword, and impales herself on it.
