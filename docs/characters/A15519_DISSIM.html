Nay no lesse than a farmer, a right honest man, but my to ng cannot stay me to tell what I am: Nay, who is it that knowes me not by my partie coloured head? They may well thinke that see me, my honestie is fled. Tush, a figge for honestie, tut let that go. Sith men, women, and children my name and doings do know. My name is Dissimulation, and no base mind I beare, For my outward effects my inward zeale do declare: For men do dissemble with their wiues, & their wiues with them again, So that in the hearts of them I alwaies remaine: The child dissembles with his father, the sister with her brother, the mayden with her mistris, and the yongman with his louer, There is Dissimulation betweene neighbor and neighbor, friend and friend, one with another. Betweene the seruant and his maister, betweene brother and brother, then why make you it strange that euer you knew me, Seeing so often I raunge throughout euery degree? But forget my basenes, ile towards London as fast as I can, to get entertainment of one of the three Ladies, like an honest man. Whither away good fellow? I pray thee declare. What if I did, would it be better for thee? Agreed, there is a bargen, but what shall I call thee? A name agreeing to thy nature, but stay here comes more com pany. Plot — The Three Ladies of London
Lady Love and Lady Conscience are concerned that Lady Lucre now rules in London, but Fame assures them that she and her adherents will meet their due punishment in the end. Four knaves and Simplicity, a penurious miller, go to London in the hope of employment by one of the ladies. Conscience rejects their applications, but Love agrees to take on Simplicity; the knaves find positions in Lucre’s household. Out of work because foreigners have taken all the jobs, Artifex also seeks a place with Lucre. Simplicity procures a testimonial from Conscience for his cousin Sincerity, who seeks a benefice; but he is unable to get anything from Simony because he has no bribe to offer. Dissimulation fobs him off with the parsonage of St Nihil — nothing - and an invitation to dine with Nemo — nobody — is likewise worthless (though Simplicity has _ difficulty understanding this). Simony employs Sir Peter Pleaseman, who can be either Protestant or Catholic as it suits his circumstances, as Lucre’s chaplain. Conscience is forced to sell her cottage to Lucre, but hopes to stay on as a sitting tenant. Usury threatens eviction; there is a proposal to pack the house with foreigners who will require less living space than English tenants. Eventually he relents and quadruples the rent instead. Love and Conscience are reduced to sending Usury their clothes in lieu of payment, but Dissimulation gives Simplicity money to redeem one of the gowns. He intended it to be Love’s, since he cares nothing for Conscience, but Simplicity returns Conscience’s clothes and delivers Love’s to Usury. Lucre enters an agreement with the Italian merchant Mercadore, whereby he will export essential materials and foodstuffs from England, and import foreign trinkets for gentlewomen. Mercadore goes to Turkey to procure knick-knacks, and encounters the Jew Gerontus. Two years ago, he borrowed a lot of money from Gerontus and avoided paying it back by skipping the country. Gerontus is happy to help in the supply of 1581 worthless tat to England, but eventually loses patience with Mercadore’s promises to pay his debt, and has him arrested. Mercadore proposes converting to Islam, which will legally void all his past obligations. Fearing that he might be blamed for a Christian’s apostasy, Gerontus cancels the debt, whereupon Mercadore’s Muslim convictions evaporate.
