But they haue not past you, they lye forzen in your face. Oh most sweet friend the World's so vicious, That had I with such familiaritie Frequented you since you were married, Possess'd and vs'd your fortunes as before, As in like maner you commanded mine, The deprau'd thoughts of men would haue proclaim'd Some scandalous rumors from this loue of ours, As saying, mine reflected on your Ladie, And what a wound had that beene to our soules? When only friend-ship should haue beene the ground To hurt her Honor, and your confident peace, Spight of mine owne approu'd integritie. Why sweet friend, That you are happie in her, shee is faire, Wittie and vertuous, and was rich to you, Can there be an addition to a wife? Doe not wrong So aire a wife (friend) and so vertuous, Whose good name is a theame vnto the World, Make not a wound with searching where was none, Misfortune still such projects doth pursue, He makes a false wife, that suspects a true; Yet since you so importune, giue me leaue To ruminate a while, and I will straight Follow and giue you an answer. Assure your selfe deere—Coxcombe, I will do't Or strangely be denied, all's as I wisht, This was my aime, although I haue seem'd strange. I know this fellow now to be an Asse; A most vnworthie husband though in view He beare himselfe thus faire, shee knowes this too, Therefore the stronger are my hopes to gaine her: And my deere friend that will haue your wife trid'e, I'le trie her first, then thrust her if I can, And as you said most wisely I hoped to be Both Touch-stone to your wise and Lapidarie. How will you passe the time, now fairest Mistresse. Indeed some wiues Are like dead bodies in their Husbands absence. Push, hee's an Asse, I know him, a starke Asse. Of a most barbarous condition, False-hearted to his friend, rough vnto you, A most desembling and perfideous fellow, I care not if he heard me, this I know, And will make good vpon him with my sword Or any for him, for he will not fight.
