Plot summary

Plot — 3 Henry the Sixth (The True Tragedy of Richard Duke of York and the Good King Henry the Sixth)
After defeating King Henry’s forces in battle, the Duke of York assumes the throne in parliament, and, following debate about the legitimacy of his title, Henry assigns the crown to York after his death. Outraged that her son has been disinherited, Queen Margaret plans to resume battle, and York’s sons persuade him to break his oath too. In the ensuing conflict, young Rutland is killed by a vindictive Clifford, and Queen Margaret captures York and torments him with a napkin stained with the boy’s blood; after a mock-coronation, she and Clifford kill him and place his head on the gates of York. York’s sons see a vision of three suns merging into one. During the battle, King Henry witnesses examples of families broken up by the internecine civil war. The Yorkists win, and Clifford is killed; his head replaces York’s on the city gates. York’s eldest son Edward becomes King, and the Earl of Warwick goes to negotiate a marriage alliance with the French royal family. Queen Margaret and the Prince also go to seek aid from France, while the deposed King Henry flees to Scotland, but later returns and is captured. Lady Grey sues to King Edward for the return of her husband’s lands, and he takes her for his Queen. Warwick’s diplomatic mission to France is thus rendered pointless, and, dishonoured, he switches to the Lancastrian side; he and Margaret are given soldiers by the insulted French King. King Edward’s marriage also alienates his brother Clarence, who joins Warwick. Edward is taken prisoner, but later rescued by Richard and allies. Restored to the throne, King Henry prophesies that the young Earl of Richmond may eventually become King. Edward returns with an army and seizes back the throne; Clarence reverts to the Yorkist side. In the ensuing battles, Warwick is killed, and the Lancastrians routed. Prince Edward, after behaving provocatively, is murdered; Richard then kills the imprisoned King Henry and determines to murder his way to the throne. Edward’s Queen gives birth to a son, and Queen Margaret, ransomed by her father, is sent to France.
