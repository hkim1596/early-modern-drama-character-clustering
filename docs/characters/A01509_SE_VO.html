¶Helpe, helpe, helpe. ¶What is the matter? ¶It was our chaunce in ending things, which we haue for to ende, Secretly to common here, where none should here our mynde, And lying close and secrete still, we shame to much to shew, But what we saw, we kéepe it close, till Ioachim may it know. ¶There is but one that liueth for aye, and séeth euery thing, He knoweth the fruite of euery trée, before the braunche doe spring, I leaue to him in this my néede, that which I haue to do, I am but one, thou knowest my God, these wicked men are two. ¶Me thinkes this matter very straunge, and soundes not lyke a truth My Mistresse vertue all the world haue noted from her youth. ¶Our Mistresse, yea she doth her lyfe, in such sorte still direct, As fyckle fame at no tyme durst, her honest lyfe suspect, I tell thée true Seruus my friend, I flatter not for méede, It shalbe found that they them selues haue wrought this wicked déede And cause that she would not consent, to serue their fylthy lust, They haue wrought this to scuse themselues, Seruus thou mayst me trust, ¶Come away, come away, in fayth Madame, you are a secrete whore Full long haue I mistrusted it, though I tooke you not before. ¶A whore, yea vyle and fylthye whore, fye on it fylthy acte, I thinke a thousand of her toyes, the vyldest whore doth lack. ¶Why God if I most synfull wight, might reason once with thée, Canst thou permit and suffer still, these wicked thus to lye? My sorrowes all and some I sende, them to thy heauenly throne, Receiue my sobbing teares oh Lord, and ease me that do mone. ¶Harde you not my fellow deare, I thought her hart had broke, And eke my soule did sorrow much, to here the wordes she spoke, But I know this assuredly, that if she guiltlesse bée, God will defende the innocent, from cruell destinie, ¶Guiltlesse man, yes assuredly, and marke what I doe tell, Thou shalt sée them dye shamefully, and she shall doe full well, Why God himselfe hath spoken this, the fruite showeth euery trée, And marke if synce thou kn west her fyrst, where is one such as shée ▪ She serueth God deuoutly still, she helpeth eke the pore, And to ayde them that want reléefe, she goeth from dore to dore, And now in this her greatest néede, she doth vpon God call, I promise thée I trust in God, he will not let her fall,

Plot summary

Plot — The Most Virtuous and Godly Susanna
Satan commissions Ill Report to bring about the downfall of Susanna, a paragon of virtue. Since pride, gluttony, envy, sloth, and covetousness have failed to achieve this, Ill Report decides to use lust. He selects the elders Voluptas and Sensualitas as his instruments of temptation, and they are tempted in turn when they see Susanna. Susanna’s husband Joachim leaves to inspect his realm, leaving the elders to administer justice in his absence. The elders spy on Susanna as she bathes in her orchard after dinner, and proposition her for sex. When she refuses, they slander her with whoredom. Meanwhile Joachim discovers that the elders are corrupt and hated by the people. The elders’ perjured testimony ensures that Susanna is sentenced to death, but God intervenes, sending Daniel to reveal the truth. The elders are stoned, and die penitent. Ill Report is hanged, and a frustrated Satan arrives to carry him to hell and tormenters for his failure. Susanna and her family count their blessings.
