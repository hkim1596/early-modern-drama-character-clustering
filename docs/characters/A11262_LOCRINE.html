Full speech

If Locrine do not follow your aduise, And beare himselfe in all things like a prince That seekes to amplifie the great renowne Left vnto him for an inheritage By those that were his ancestors, Let me be flung into the Ocean, And swallowed in the bowels of the earth. Or let the ruddie lightning of great Ioue, Descend vpon this my deuolted head. But for I see you all to be in doubt, who shall be matched with our royall sonne, Locrine receiue this present at my hand, A gift more rich then are the wealthie mines Found in the bowels of America, Thou shalt be spoused to faire Guendoline, Loue her, and take her, for she is thine owne, If so thy vnckle and herselfe do please. No longer let me wel enioy the crowne, Then I do peerlesse Guendoline. Vncle and princes of braue Britany, Since that our noble father is intombd, As best beseemd so braue a prince as he, If so you please, this day my loue and I, Within the temple of Concordia, Will solemnize our roiall marriage. And is this true, is Albanactus slaine? Hath cursed Humber with his stragling hoste With that his armie made of mungrell curres, Brought our redoubted brother to his end. O that I had the Thracian Orpheus harpe For to awake out of the infernall shade Those ougly diuels of black Ereb s, That might torment the damned traitors soule: O that I had Amphions instrument To quicken with his vitall notes and tunes The flintie ioynts of euerie stonie rocke, By which the Scithians might be punished, For by the lightening of almightie Ioue The Hunne shall die, had he ten thousand liues: And would to God he had ten thousand liues, That I might with the arme-strong Hercules Crop off so vile an Hidras hissing heads, But say me cousen, for I long to heare How Albanact came by vntimely death?

Plot summary

Plot — Locrine, the Eldest Son of King Brutus
Brutus, the conqueror of Britain, is dying. He gives his eldest son Locrine the crown and betrothes him to Gwendoline, and assigns his other sons, Camber and Albanact, the south and north respectively. His funeral is swiftly followed by Locrine’s royal wedding. Strumbo is in love with Dorothy, and persuades her to marry him. However, he is impressed into Albanact’s army when the Scythians invade northern Britain, and Dorothy is killed when the Scythians burn his house with her inside. The Scythians win the battle, and Albanact takes his own life rather than submit. To avenge his death, Locrine, Camber, and the peers give battle and defeat the Scythians. Humber’s wife Estrild is captured by Locrine’s soldiers, and Locrine is attracted to her. Strumbo is caught having sex with Margery. Initially he refuses to marry her, but is forced to agree after she beats him up. He encounters the defeated and starving Humber, but the ghost of Albanact stops him from feeding the Scythian. Humber eventually drowns himself. Locrine is unable to put away Gwendoline and install Estrild as his queen because Gwendoline’s father, Corineus, proves inconveniently long-lived despite being wounded in the battle with the Scythians. Locrine hides her in an underground pleasure- chamber and visits her in secret; they have a child, Sabren. After Corineus finally dies, Gwendoline goes to Cornwall to arrange the funeral, and Locrine takes the opportunity to bring Estrild out of hiding. Gwendoline and her brother raise an army, and defeat Locrine in battle; he and Estrild kill themselves. Sabren finds their bodies but is physically too weak to use the sword on herself. Gwendoline finds her and promises her a lingering, unusual death, so Sabren drowns” herself; the river is named after her, Severn. Gwendoline arranges to bury Locrine as befits a king - and to leave Estrild ignominiously unburied.
