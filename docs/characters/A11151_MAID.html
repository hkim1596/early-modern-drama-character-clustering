Full speech

Dainty Hypocrite. The witty man, the pretty man, the singing man, he has the dantiest dittie, so full of pith, so full of spirit, as they say. Old ends, I am sure they are new beginnings with me. By sixe 'th morning forsooth, hee to ke mee for Mary Ploodhound, having it seemes, never seene any on's be fore, and I being something amorously affected, as they say to his Welch ditties, answered to her name, lay with him all night, and married him this morning, so that as he tooke me for her, I tooke him as he vvas forsooth.

Plot — A Match at Midnight
Old Bloodhound is training his younger son Tim in the family pawnbroking business, since Alexander, the elder son, is a drunken wastrel. He intends to marry his daughter Mary to the informer Earlack, even though she prefers Ensign Young, while he himself will marry the young widow Wag. The widow has other suitors, including her servant Jarvis, but wants to get rid of them. She arranges a midnight assignation with Old Bloodhound, intending not to turn up. The various wedding plans unravel in the morning. Meanwhile, Tim has married Sue, not knowing her true profession until it is too late, and Mary has married Young, to the dismay of Randall, who finds that he has accidentally married a servant. Only Alexander seems on course for the wedding he wants, until the widow points out that Jarvis has a prior claim: when he tried to seduce her months ago, she demanded that he should prove himself by living in her household as her servant.
