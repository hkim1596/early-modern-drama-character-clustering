Eugenio, these are wonders past beliefe. Is your old father of so poore a iudgement To thinke it in the power of man to turne One person to another? But who's the man That workes these miracles? How deales Astrologie with transmutation? Eugenio, 'tis high time for vs t'awake. And as you loue our Flauia, and I Your sister faire Sulpitia ; let's doe something Worthy their beauties. Who falles into a sea, Swolne bigge with tempest, but he boldly beates The waues with armes and legges, to saue his life? So let vs striue 'gainst troublous stormes of loue, With our best power, least after we ascribe The losse to our dull negligence, not Fortune. She cannot out-loue me, nor you out-friend me, For th'sacred name whereof, I haue reiected Your fathers offers, importunities, Letters, Conditions, Seruants, Friends, and lastly His tender of Sulpitia in exchange For Flauia ▪ But though I loue your sister Like mine owne soule; yet did the lawes of friendship Master that strong affection; and denied him. ARmellina ? who ̄ do you draw your tongue vpon so sharply? This his graue lookes! In every lineament himselfe no liker. Had I not haply been advertiz'd, What could haue forc'd me thinke 'twere Trincalo ? Doubtlesse, th'Astrologer hath rais'd a ghost That walkes in th'reverend ghost of my dead father. Infinite power of Art! who would beleeue The planets influence could transforme a man To seuerall shapes! Pardon me, father; 'Twas my blind ignorance, not want of duty, That wrong'd you: all was intended for a Farmer, Whom an Astrologer, they sayd, transform'd. THe hopefull issue of thy counsell, Cricca, Brightens this eu'ning, and makes it more excell The clearest day, then a gray morning doth The blindest midnight: raising my amorous thoughts To such a pitch of ioy, that riches, honour, And other pleasures, to Sulpitia's loue, Appeare like Mole-hils to the Moone. Plot summary

Plot — Albumazar
The neighbours Pandolfo and Antonio, both widowers, have arranged to marry each other’s daughters; but before they can do so, Antonio is lost and presumed dead on an overseas voyage, and the plan goes into abeyance because his son Lelio disapproves of Pandolfo as a potential brother-in-law. Albumazar’s accomplice Furbo has gained access to Pandolfo’s household, posing as his daughter’s tutor, and has learned of his .- amorous aspirations, and also his credulity about. magic and astrology. Albumazar plans to trick him, and sets about demonstrating his occult power. Ronca shows Pandolfo some of the astrologer’s amazing inventions: a telescope and an ‘otacousticon’ (actually a pair of ass’s ears) which allow him to see and hear things at a distance. Albumazar then predicts Pandolfo’s marriage, and also a stabbing which his associates then enact in a brawl. Pandolfo asks the astrologer to raise Antonio’s ghost, but instead Albumazar undertakes to transform the tenant farmer Trincalo into Antonio’s likeness for one day, so that he can give Flavia in marriage to Pandolfo. Trincalo agrees because this will give him access to Antonio’s household, and therefore to the maid Armellina, whom he loves. Pandolfo is required to prepare a room for the metamorphosis, laid out with valuable possessions. When he begins to have second thoughts about paying Albumazar, his servant Cricca suggests that he should pretend to have been robbed of his valuables; but when they are indeed stolen, Albumazar pretends in turn that the transformed Trincalo has just locked them away for safe keeping. After undergoing the fake metamorphosis, Trincalo is told he may not look at himself in a mirror for fear of breaking the spell.
