Full speech

I hope your Highnesse Is pleas'd that I shall still hold my place with you, For I haue bene so long vs'd to prouide you Fresh bits of flesh since mine grew stale, that surely If cashir'd now, I shall proue a bad Cator In the Fishmarket of cold chastity, And for me, If I faile in my office to prouide you Fresh delicates, hang me. Faith I know none else, allmy bringing vp Aimdeat no other learning. The Phystian Helps euer at a dead lift; a fine calling, That can bothraise, and take downe, our vpon thee. Behold the man yon talke of. This lie beares a similitude of truele, Fie, you take the hangmans place. Would your highnesse Were pleas'd to informe me better of your purpose: I feele you Madam, The man that shall receiue this from my Lord, Shall haue a thousand crownes. besides His day of bastinadoing past ore, He shall not lose your grace, nor your good fauor. It must be a man Of credit in the Court, that is to be The foyle vnto your valour. If he be a Lord, 'Twill be the greater grace. Why then behold that valiant man and Lord, That for your sake will take a cudgelling, For be assurde when it is spread abroad That you haue dealt with me, the'ile giue you out For one of the nine Worthies. This man of war will aduance. Wind him backe as you fauor my eares, I loue no noyse in my head, my braines haue hitherto Bin imployde in silent businesses. Giue ground whilst I drill my wits to the encounter, Deuitry. I take it. Is there another parcell of you, if it be at pawne I will gladly redeeme it to make you wholy mine. Thankes for your good remembrance, You haue bin a souldier Deuitry, and borne armes. Much good may it do you Sir. It is time you put vp your sword, and run a way for meate sir, nay if I had not withdrawne ere now, I might haue kept the; fast with you: but since the way to thriue is neuer late, what is the neerest course to profit thinke you? True sence, bawdry. Ile shew you a new way of prostitution, fall backe, further yet, further, there is fifty crownes, do but as much to Protaldye the Queenes fauoret, they are doubled. Giue him but an affront as he comes to the presence, and in his drawing make way like a true bawde to his valour, the son's thy owne; if you take a scratch in the arme or so, euery drop of blood weighes downe a ducket. See, you shall not belong earning your wages, your worke's before your eyes. What opinion will the managing of this affaire Bring to my wisedome? my inuention tickles With apprehension on't: This is the man. Remember the condition as you looke for payment. Plague of the scruiners running hand, What a blow is this to my reputation? Where could I wish my selfe now? in the Ile of dogs, So I might scape scratching, for I see by her cats eyes I shall be claw'd fearefully.
