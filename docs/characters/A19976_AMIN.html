A poore ſouldier ant like your worſhip. Right worſhipfull. We thanke you good Dametas. You ſee how confidentlie wee preſume vpon your Letters promiſe, in furthering vs to attaine the louers prize. To ſtrike, how meane you. That was the father that begot the doubt in vs, you will appoint the hunt. As a Lawyer doth for his clyant for a ſecond ſee. Heeres no Iudas? Tis common in ſuch baſe fellowes, ſuch Court-ſpyders, that weaue their webbes of flatterie in the eares of greatneſſe, if they can once entangle them in their quaint trecherie, they poyſen em ſtraight. For my part Ile make that vſe of him that Phiſitions do of poyſon, vſe as much of him as ſerues for mine honeſt intent, & caſt downe the reſt, as vnfit for any neceſſary imployment. And let our diſcourſe goe ſo ſmoothly apparrelled, that it moue not the patience of the moſt tender eare. Now & Dametas be the mettle he was ſtampt for, a right villaine. Indeede, he that will proue falſe to his maker, wil be true to no man. Thats not for loue. So, hee hath put em faire to the ſtand, lets iſſue and ſurpriſe them, Offices ſo bought and ſould, that before the purchaſer can be ſayd to be placed in his Office, he is againe by his couetous Patrone diſplac't. Indeede ſince your Maieſtie left the Land, the whole bodie of the Common-wealth runnes cleane againſt the byas of true and priſtine gouernement. We are I faith: Inuention could not weaue, A quainter webbe, Suſpition to deceaue. What and wee ſhould acquaint the Ladies with our intents.
