Full speech

These fellowes have out-gone their Commission and rob'd them. I was a wise man to come abroad with out a sword. Doe you meane to murther a Gentleman? You have done a benefit I must acknowledge. Were you sent to rob or kill me? Forbeare the rest. This affords me matter: Re turne to that house, there leave your bootie and receive your reward; onely this I'le keepe. Blest Accident! 'tis surely the same. After a stricter view, my memory Vnlesse it erres, tells me I have seene your person Before this at my fathers. Have I found you? I shall search you deeper. It seemes you love my Sister? Cal't not presumption sir, you doe deserve her, In that you have indear'd me for my life Who am her Brother. And I commend you wit, Which I presume my Sisters love hath whetted. 'Twas a quaint Plott. Were th' Actors here againe, I'd pay their wages. Why sir, ridiculous fables May sometimes serve for imitation. Though twere a meere appointment in this rescue, To shew your love and valour. Alas sir, you are mov'd? But I must tell you sir, Seldome high spirits that pursue their honours, With earnest fllights, will stoop at weake respects: But prey upon th' opinion of those men That scan their actions; tearing their reputations Out of suspitions bowels. Hee's not a Gentle man Will not preserve his honour. You will not fight with me that am her Brother? I doe presume my Sister will reward you For these expressions. She is free to pay Her servants promis'd wages; be it kisses, Or any other dalliance. Not against him that loves my Sister. Never against your bosome, where a spirit So truely noble dwel's, that hath converted All my faign'd jealousies to usefull love. Pray reconcile all your distractions. Let not the least distrust abuse your confidence Of what I undertake. My Sister's yours, If the advise of me that am her Brother, And interested in her good or ill, Can be prevailing. So mine the like. Thus noble causes Put fire into the spirits of full men. Though sometimes seeming valour may arise Through lust or wine, from hatefull cowardise. Our mutuall loves. This jarres upon my heart-strings. You must not wrong my Father. But not to wrong my Father. I thanke you little one. An inferiour servant may serve, Mr. Dasher. And boyes too sometimes. Answer what Child? Yes Sir, hee can brooke any thing but wronging of his Father. Let not your love appeare so full of flattery. Bravely come off Sir. This Sack shall then have my Encomium. Which had the youthfull Father ever tasted He would have left his rites to Poetize; And chang'd his Yvy Chaplets into Bayes: Vnchain'd his spotted Linxes, and supply'd His Chariots loftier course with Pegasus. And with bewitching numbers charm'd the gods To be his Bacchanalls, that they might feast With this most heavenly Nectar. Pray Sir, let me prepare her. No more will I In the severer Person of a counsellour Instruct your care. But since by curious search I have inform'd my knowledge Even to satisfaction of his worth, Let me commend this noble gentleman Vnto your best desires; let him possesse them. My love's your warrant. Have not I searchd him throughly? have not I Found him deserving all that's due to man, Though malice were his judge. View but his person, Art could not shape a m re exact prep rtion: And through his Christall bosome read his heart Wherein such noble thoughts are character'd— Yes by my hopes of good, no otherwise Then I desire to have my selfe commended Vpon the like occasion. I doe interpret it; and have thought upon Another tryall. Let me intreat you Sister To conster my intentions right. Though I Commended him, 'twas onely to distinguish Your passion and your reason. Now I find The latter strongest, that you refuse the love Of one so much defective. Sister, I hope You are not earnest! Mother beshrew your heart, she was in a good eine. But come sir, wee l pursue it. Nay, blush not Sister; Though it be vertues colour. Say't agen. Here are more witnesses. From in ward joy that she affects this gentleman, Whose Vertues wonn Vpon my love to be his Oratour; And not respects infected with the mixture Of any worse condition.
