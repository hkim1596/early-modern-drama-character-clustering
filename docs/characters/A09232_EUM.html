Full speech

Tell me Time, tell me iust Time, When shall I Delia see? When shall I see the loadstar of my life? When shall my wandring course end with her sight? Or I but view my hope, my hearts delight. Father God speede, if you tell fortunes, I pray good father tell me mine. This man hath left me in a Laborinth, He biddeth me giue more than all, Till dead mens bones come at thy call: He biddeth me dreame of no rest, Till I repent that I do best. Hould thy hands good fellow. Why what was that Iack? Hould thy hands, I pray thee good fellow be not too hastie. Who's that? Forbeare I say, who is that which pin cheth mee? Alasse pretty Lad, I know not how to keepe my selfe, and much lesse a seruant, my pretty boy, my state is so bad. I thinke this boy be a spirit, How knowst thou all this? Well my Lad since thou art so impor nate, I am content to entertaine thee, not as a seruant; but a copartner in my iourney. But whither shall we goe for I haue not any money more than one bare three halfe-pence. I, go before, ile follow thee. No I promise thee not yet. Iack, why be it so then. Hostes I thanke you, I haue no great stomack. Why Iack what doost thou meane, thou knowest I haue not any money: therefore sweete Iack tell me what shall I doo. Why faith it is a follie, for I haue no money. Alas Iack my pursse is full of money. Thankes my fine eloquent hostesse. I am Iack, here is my hand. Come Hostesse receiue your money, and I thanke you for my good entertainment. Come Iack whether go we now? Go along Iack, ile follow thee, Iack, they say it is good to go crosse legged, and say his prayers backward: how saiest thou? How now Iack, what news? How now Iack, what is this? Why then Iack I will soone put out this light. Why with a stone ile breake the glasse, and then blowe it out. God speed faire maide sitting alone there is once. God speed faire maide; there is twise: God speed faire maide, that is thrise. Thou fairest flower of these westerne parts: Whose beautie so reflecteth in my sight, As doth a Christall mirror in the sonne: For thy sweet sake I haue crost the frosen Rhine, Leauing faire Po, I saild vp Danuby, As farre as Saba whose inhansing streames, Cuts twixt the Tartars and the Russians, These haue I crost for thee faire Delia: Then grant me that which I haue sude for long. Thankes gentle Madame: but heere comes Iack, thanke him, for he is the best friend that we haue. How now Iack, what hast thou there? Why Iack that is impossible, he was a young man. Why so thou shalt Iack. Before I will falsifie my faith vnto my friend, I will diuide hir, Iacke thou shalt haue halfe. Content your selues, my word is past to him, therefore prepare thy selfe Delya for thou must die. I very well Iacke. Iacke what art thou gone? Then farewell Iacke. Come brothers and my beauteous Delya, Erestus and thy deare Venelia: We will to Thessalie with ioyfull hearts.
