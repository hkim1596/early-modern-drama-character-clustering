Tis certaine the honest knaue Chamberleine that hath bin my Informer, my baud, euer since I knew Ware assures me of it, and more being a Londoner though altogether vnacquainted, I haue requested his company at supper. Be that my vndertaking: if I do not take a full reuenge of his wiues puritanicall oynesse. This honest knaue is call'd Innocence, ist not a good name for a Chamberlaine? he dwe't at Dunstable not long since, and hath brought me and the two Butchers Daughters there to interuiew twenty times & not so little I protest: how chance you left dunstable Sirra? True, but yet it comes scant of the Prophesy; Lincolne was, London is, and Yorke shall-be. I had a dellicate banquet once on that table. Here is your ring Mistris; a thousand times, —and would haue willingly lost my best of maintenance that I might haue found you haire so tractable. Haue you her sir? Hold downe her stomach good maister Fetherstone, while I fetch some. Exit. I will finde a remedy for this walking, if all the Docters in towne can sell it; a thousand pound to a penny she spoile not her face, or breake her neck, or catch a cold that shee may nere claw off againe, how doost wench? Is this he has al manner of languages, yet speakes none Gods so, now I thinke vpon't, a Iest is crept into my head, steale away, if you loue me. Exeunt: musition sings. Good Maister Mayberie, Philip, if you be kind Gentle men vphold the iest: your whole voiage is payd for. No matter how he talkes, but his Pericranion's perisht. Plot summary

Plot — Northward Ho
Luke Greenshield has failed to seduce Moll Mayberry in her husband’s absence; now he dines with Mayberry at Ware and describes how he slept with an unnamed wife, showing him her wedding ring. Mayberry grows jealous and hurries back to London to confront Moll, but is satisfied by her explanation that the ring was stolen. Seeking revenge, he has her play up to Greenshield’s advances on his next visit to the house. Greenshield arranges lodgings at one of Mayberry’s houses for himself and Kate, a young woman whom he introduces as his sister, but who is actually his wife. The servants are soon aware that they are sleeping together, and also that Kate has a habit of sleep- walking around the house and ending up in Featherstone’s bedroom; actually she is shamming with a view to visiting Featherstone, her lover. ===== PAGE 241 ===== 1605 Meanwhile, Philip is arrested for debt after spending lavishly on the prostitute Doll. His father Bellamont grudgingly pays up and he is released; the price of Philip’s freedom is to have to listen to some moralistic exhortation. In response, he conspires with Doll (who has a tendency to swindle her clients out of money and valuables by way of love-gifts). She poses as a wealthy lady who wants to hire Bellamont to write some verses; Philip then introduces her as the girlfriend whom Bellamont criticized. Doll is smitten with the old man and visits him at home; but when she arrives, he is engaged with Captain Jenkins, Doll’s client, who also wants to buy his poetical talents to write a love poem for her. Jenkins hides during Bellamont’s meeting with Doll, and hears her ease her conscience by confessing that she is a common whore; he proceeds to blacken her name among her other lovers. Doll blames Bellamont and swears vengeance. The characters now gravitate back northward to Ware. Featherstone plans an assignation there with Kate, using a ruse to get Greenshield out of London. Mayberry encourages Greenshield to think that Moll is also going there for sexual purposes, and they follow together, accompanied by Bellamont, Philip, and others. Bellamont proposes that they enliven the journey by playing practical jokes on one another, and he is the first victim when they stop off at Bedlam and the young men try to have him committed as a lunatic.
