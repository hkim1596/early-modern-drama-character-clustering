Come on sit, are you ready? To goe to this geere. Come Sir. No remedie. Indeed heers a remedie for that. I truely, come are you ready. I pray sir dispatch, the day goes away. Faith you haue reason, for I am like to break your yong neck. Alas sir, you are a foot too low to reach it, and I hope you will neuer grow so high while I am in the office. What, he that points to it with his finger. I know him not, but what of him? I, and many a faire yeere after, to trusse vp many an honester man then either thou or he. Faith I cannot tell, nor I care not greatly. Me thinks you should rather hearken to your soules health. Wel, thou art euen the meriest peece of mans flesh that ere gronde at my office doore. I, and that shall all they witnes that see you seale it with a theeues name. I mary sir, this is a good motion: my maisters, you see heers a good fellow. As how? stand you on that, then you shall off with this. O Lord sir, God blesse you sir, the man sir Petergade, Sir, he that was so full of merrie conceits. O Lord sir, he went the wrong way, the fellow had a faire commission to the contrary. Sir, heere is his pas port, I pray you sir, we haue done him wrong. you will stand between the gallowes and me. I thank your L. worship.
