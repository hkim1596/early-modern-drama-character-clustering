Full speech

I did not thinke, Rufaldo, pardon me, You could haue so preuail'd, but if she can Plant her affection on yee, Ile not be Backward to call you sonne. An old mans Darling, is a petty Queene Aboue all her desires. Rufaldo, with your patience, I would haue A word or two in priuate, you neede not Preiudicate me. Selina, thou knowest I am thy father. And you know whom you haue reiected. Aud you know what man he is, with whom You meane to tye that knot, nothing but Death is able to vndoe. Oh Selina, Felice thy poore sister thou recalst To sad remembrance, but heauen alas Knowes onely where she is. Thou with thy vncles tendernesse wast kept Alwayes ith Countrey, not vntill her losse At home with me, her fate taught me to giue A liberty to thee, her I restrain'd Poore wench in loue with Gasparo, till betwixt Obedience to a father, and the loue To him, she left vs both, father and friend, Now to auoyd the like affliction, I vowed thy freedome, and thou seest I doe Not encounter thy affection with the bonds A father might enforce vpon his child. But yet Selina, Take heed, be not too rash, I haue obseru'd You want no common iudgement, O doe not Precipitate thy selfe into a sorrow, Shall waste thee with repentance, let me tell you, There is a methode, when your passion's young To keepe it in obedience: you loue Rufaldo, Art thou not young? How will the Rose agree With a dead Hyacinth? or the Honey Wood-bind, Circling a withered Bryer? You can apply, can you submit your body To bed with Ice and Snow, your blood to mingle? Would you be deaf'd with coughing, teach your eye How to be rumaticke? Breathes he not out His body in diseases, and like dust Falling all into pieces, as if Nature Would make him his old graue: I say too much. O what are all the riches of the world, To an oppressed mind? which then must be Fed with despaire of change, or will gold Buy off th' imprisonment? nay, will it not Compose the chaines, that bind you to endure it? Well I haue said enough, keepe still your freedome, And loose it where you will, you shall not blame Me for your fate, nor grieue me with your shame. No more, heauens blessing and mine Light on thee, sha't haue Rufaldo. Rufaldo, heard you that? she sayes she would not Leaue you for a world of other men. As close as Cockles. 'Twere time I see we were at conference, To confirme all things for the marriage, You being agreed, I thinke we shall not differ In other circumstance, and 'twere sinne to let That keepe your ioyes asunder by delay: Please you weele haue some treaties. I am amaz'd, when was she seene? What should this meane, it is a strange absence, ath' wedding day too. My heart misgiues me, some fatall accident vpon my life, is hapned to her. Be dumbe, night-Rauen, she's lost, she's lost, The Fates sure make conspiracy to take My Daughters from me: one I lost because I would not giue her, and I haue repented Full iustice for it, and am I so vnhappy To lose Selina too? but Ile not sleepe, Vntill I finde her either aliue or dead. Rufaldo, you haue interest in this sorrow, Ioine in the inquisition. Oh my Girle Selina. No sir, I had watch'd three nights before, and be cause I would not winke at two or three drunkards as they went reeling home at twelue a clocke at night, the Deuill owed me a spight. Antonio not heard of yet? I hope 'tis so, and yet he staies too long. Here's Gorgon: Sirra, where haue you beene all this day? But sirra, your Master Antonio's gon. Goe your waies, sirra, and either bring me newes of him, or looke mee in the face no more, you'l finde we iest not. Away sirra. I cannot be heartily merry: well, let's leaue these two without any more ceremonies, 'tis late, all ioyes be multiplyed on my Sonne and Daughter: good night, I doe comfort my selfe with hope of Antonio's returne, and yet feares are great. Antonio slaine? haplesse Cornelio, My hopes were treasur'd vp in him, the staffe And comfort of my age, and is he gone? Why, was not he thy Brother? canst thou Thou hast so little share in yong Antonio, That thou darest speake of comfort? But cowards in despaire proue desperate, and most vnhappy. Didst not thou kill Antonio then? Stay, he'le confesse. Howsoeuer feare of death possesse him so, I see the murther in his eyes. His very hand doth shew a guiltinesse, looke how it trembles. I am Cornelio: is it with me thou woo'dst? Oh stay awhile. Doe I not dreame? Babulcus, pray' let vs haue your company, It doth concerne your freedome. Antonio liuing? Rufaldo, let vs make a merry day on't, If it be true. If? I doe sinne against Discretion to distrust it. O my starres, I doe acquit you all your iniuries, If you possesse me of Antonio. Neuer did man to blisse more willing goe. He has got a boy by this time. Master Bubulcus. Come, Master Ienkin, I now perceiue you lou'd my daughter, if you had acquainted me in time, I should not haue beene vnwilling to haue cald you sonne, but since 'tis too late, let your wisedome checke impatience: I know you are of a noble temper, howsoeuer passion may a little cloud your vertues, lets be all friends I pray. Oh, no more shooting at that but: harke, I heare the Shepheards musicke, and voyce too, lets sit downe I pray, Antonio keepe thy word. Wood-men Shepheards, come away, This is Pans great holy-day, Throw off cares, With your heauen aspiring aires Helpe vs to sing While valleyes with your Ecchoes ring. Nymphes that dwell within these groues, Leaue your Arbours, bring your loues, gather poesies, Crowne your golden haire with Roses, As you passe Foote like Fayries on the grasse. Ioy drowne our bowers, Philomell, Leaue of Tereus rape to tell, Let trees dance, As they at Thracian Lire did once, Mountaines play, This is the Shepheards holiday. Which is Antonio? Ha, what meanes this? Who's this? Amazement: thou Antonio? he Selina? But who is this? Stay children, stay: take heed, you doe not know What strength of ioy my fainting age can beare: You fall in too full showres, like swelling Nile These comforts will exceed the narrow bankes Of my poore frailty: rise, enioy your wishes, And my blessings be multiplyed vpon you. Ha! Rufaldo, here's Felice my lost Girle, Take her, take her, Gasparo. Selina, Art not thou Rufaldo's Wife? Nay, Rufaldo. Come, vpon recollection, you must make it a bar gaine: they haue, it seemes, bought and sold already, 'tis past recouery, he shall be worthy of her. Amen, and mine: Why I am rapt beyond my selfe with ioyes. Infortunio, Fate hath effected that I beg'd of Heauen in many prayers for you, oh my blisses.

Plot summary

Plot — The School of Compliment (Love Tricks)
Selina and Hilaria have each attracted the attention of several suitors. Selina’s brother Antonio is one of those interested in Hilaria; and Hilaria’s recently widowed father Rufaldo, who disapproves of Antonio and would rather she married the wealthy Bubulcus, would nevertheless like Selina for his second wife. Antonio has his friend and servant distract Rufaldo while he sneaks in to visit Hilaria; they do it by persuading Rufaldo that he has been miraculously rejuvenated. Selina’s father is happy to let her have her own way in ===== PAGE 140 ===== her love-life, because her sister Felice absconded after he interfered in hers. He proposes that she should be cautious about marrying Rufaldo, but she declares enthusiasm for the idea and asks for a hasty wedding; on the appointed day, however, she disappears and goes to live in the country disguised as a shepherd. Felice’s abandoned suitor Gasparo sets up a school of compliment to teach people refined behaviour. His clients include: Bubulcus, who wants to learn how to woo Hilaria and scorn Antonio; Delia, a chambermaid keen to brush up her etiquette: a judge who wants to learn how to address the jury; Medulla, a social-climbing widow; an old countryman who wants to turn his oafish son into a courtier; a quarrelsome gentleman; Ingeniolo, a justice’s clerk in love; and Jenkin, an amorous Welshman in search of poetry to turn a shepherdess’s head. The search for Selina sends several people off to the country, including her true love Infortunio. Antonio pretends to go too, and appears not to return; in fact he impersonates Selina and ‘marries’ Rufaldo. On the wedding night, the old man is horrified to learn that his new ‘wife’ will not sleep with him, and also has the physical strength to enforce ‘her’ will on him. Anxious not to lose ‘her’ altogether, he asks ‘her’ at least to live in his house and sleep with Hilaria; Antonio readily agrees. Bubulcus boasts to ‘Selina’ and Hilaria that he has killed the missing Antonio in a duel, stupidly forgetting that Selina is Antonio’s sister; ‘she’ fetches officers, and Bubulcus is arrested for murder. The real Selina has fallen in with a community of shepherds. Among them is her missing sister Felice, who uses herbal remedies to cure Infortunio of his love-melancholy for Selina.
