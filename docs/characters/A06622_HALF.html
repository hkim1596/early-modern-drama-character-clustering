I hope you shall neuer slip string, but hang steddie. Thou lyest, thou hast not a farthing to laie thy hands on, I am none of thine: but let mee bee wagging, my head is full of hammers, & they haue so maletted my wit, that I am almost a malcon tent. My master hath a fine scholer to his sonne, Prisius a fayre lasse to his daughter. They two loue one another deadly. The feathers haue put them vp, vtterly disli king the match, and haue appointed the one shall haue Memphios sonne, the other Stellios daughter, this workes lyke waxe, but how it will fadge in the end, the hen that sits next the cocke cannot tell. But they are not fooles that bee short, if I thought thou meantst so, Senties qui vir sim, Thou shouldst haue a crow to pull. At mine owne conceit and quicke censure. Sodainly me thought you two were asses, and that the least asse was the more asse. Yea, my yong master taught me to proue it by learning, and so I can out of Ouid by a verse. You must first for fashion sake confes your selues to be asses. Then stand you here and you there. Then this is the verse as I point it, Cum ma la per longas inualuêre moras. So you see the least asse is the more asse. Let me alone, Non enim mea pigra iuuentus, there's matter in this noddle. But looke where Prisius boy comes, as fit as a pud ding for a dogs mouth. I am the bodkin, looke well to your eares, I must boare them. Let vs consult at the Tauerne, where after to the health of Memphio, drinke we to the life of Stellio, I carouse to Prisius, & brinch you mas Spe rantus, we shall cast vs our accounts and discharge our stomackes, like men that can disgest any thing. Nay, I hope to see thee so strong, to shake three trees at once. In deede Inter pocula philosophundum, it is good to plea among pots. Why, sayest thou that thou hast left a print deeper in thy hand alreadie than a halfpenie canne leaue, vnles it should sing worse than an hot yron. By thee our Noses swell, With sparkling Carbuncle. Now Hoggishly ith' mire, Now must euerie one by wit make an ex cuse, and euerie excuse must be coosnage. Sharpe? Ile warrant twill serue for as good sauce to knauerie as As thy knauerie meat for his wit. When shall we meete. Away, away, if our masters take vs here the matter is mard. How? like wine, for my bodie being the rund let, and my mouth the vent, it wrought two daies ouer, till I had thought the hoopes of my head woulde haue flowen asunder. I care not, Omnem solum forti patria, I can liue in christendome as well as in Kent. He loues thee well that would runne after. Thou meanest, a browne nut is good to crack. In faith soure heart, hee that takes his pleasure on thee is verie pleasurable. Well then let fayre wordes coole that cholar, which foule speeches hath kindled, and because we are all in this case, and hope all to haue good fortune, sing a roundelay, and weele helpe, such as thou wast woont when thou beatest hempe. Well brought about. I for my part am resolute, in vtran que paratus, re die to die or to runne away. And I. Crosse your selues, looke how she lookes. Nay then let me come in with a dreame, short but sweet, that my mouth waters euer since I wakt: Me thought there sate vpon a shelfe three damaske prunes in veluet caps and prest satten gownes like indges, and that there were a whole handfull of curants to be araig ned of a riot, because they clu ̄ ged together in such clu sters, twelue raisons of the sunne were impannelled in a Iewry, and as a leafe of whole mase which was bailief was carrying the quest to consult, me thought ther came an angrie cooke, and gelded the Iewry of theyr stones, and swept both iudges, iurers, rebels and bailiefe into a porredge pot, whereat I being melancholy, fetcht a deepe sigh, that wakt my selfe and my bed fellow. Then aske my bed-felow, you know him, who dreamt that night that the king of diamonds was sicke. Wert thou once put to it? I Risio would faine see thee beg. What shall become of Accius and Silena? It was too good to be true, for we should laugh heartily, and without laughing my spleene would split, but whist here comes the man, Enter Accius. and yonder the maide, let vs stand aside. There was an auncient prouerbe knockt in the head. Excellently applyed. A braue fight is lyke to bee betweene a cocke with a long combe, and a hen with a long leg. And his combe longer than his wit. I marie sir. Whether will they, here be right coblers cuts. Because when their bodies were at worke a bout household stuffe, their mindes were busied about commonwealth matters. Ile warrant if this bee the creame, the milke is verie flat, let vs ioyne issue with them. Theres a glieke for you, let me haue my girde, on thy conscience tell me what tis a clocke? I am paid stroke dead in the neast, I am sure this soft youth who is not halfe so wise as you are faire, nor you altogether so faire as he is foolish, will not be so captious. A hard question, for it is oddes but one begate them both, hee that cut out the vpper leather, cut out the inner, & so with one awl stitcht two soles together. Tis Prisius daughter. O how fitly this comes of! I, tis an hard matter to haue a wit of the olde rent, euerie one rackes his commons so high. I and Silena to, if you tie them fast to your tongues. Asse for you all foure, Why was it a beare thou ridst on? No, it was for tyring. Ile enter into a statute Marchant to see it aun swered. But if thou wilt haue bondes, thou shalt haue a bushell full. I yeeld. And there was with them a priest. And the priest spake many kinde wordes. Nay I thinke not so. Now begins the game. Accessarie as it were. As good honfesse heere beeing trust, as at home with my hose about my heeles. Doe your worst, our knaueries will renenge it vpon your childrens children. If you mace vs, weele pepper you.
