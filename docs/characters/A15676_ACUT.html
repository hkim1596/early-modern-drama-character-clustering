Ha ha ha! Yes, I loue God and my neighbors. Prethee I hate none, but heauen hate me if I be in loue with any. Oh! how doe they then wrong my meditati on? my thoughts are with themſelues at a counſell, til with noiſe and thou with continuall talke, haſt driuen them to a nonplus. Partake then, and giue me the beleefe: thinkſt thou or knowſt thou any of this opinion, that, that moouing ma riſh element, that ſwels and ſwages as it pleaſe the moone, to be in bignes equall to that ſolide lump that brings vs vp? Good: there we haue a farther ſcope, and holde the ſea, can (as a looking glaſſe) anſwere with a meere ſmile any moouing ſhape vppon the earth. Oh, they are monſtrous madde, merrie wenches, and they are monſters Graccus, they call them Sea-maides or Mermaides ſinging ſweetelye, but none dares truſt them, and are verie like our Land-wenches, deuouring Serpents from the middle downeward. Thou haſt euen giuen me ſatisfaction: but haſt thou this by proofe? In truth! Plot — Every Woman in Her Humor
Misogyny has made the normally jovial Acutus.- melancholy. After scaring some foolishly fashionable characters, he joins them at the Hobby tavern, where Gaetica has taken lodgings with her dog. Disguised as a © soldier, Acutus picks a fight with Scilicet and Servulus, © then exposes their hypocrisy by returning disguised as first a friend and then an enemy of the ‘soldier’, and eliciting inconsistent responses. Scilicet attempts to woo Gaetica; she is more concerned about the disappearance of her man Bos with her dog, but becomes betrothed to him. , Lentulus is in love, and asks Cicero to woo Terentia for him. They go to dine with Flaminius, Terentia’s father, and Cicero is left alone with the ladies of the house. The upshot is that Terentia falls in love — but -- with Cicero himself rather than Lentulus. Rumours circulate that she is to marry Cicero, but Flaminius has not given his consent and takes offence. Lentulus makes Cicero and Terentia admit wronging him, and. _ he sentences Cicero to death — but only if Terentia will kill him. He woos and wins Terentia’s sister Flavia, and plans a double wedding. Acutus gives Philautus a sleeping potion, and he appears to die. He is rumoured to have drunk himself to death, but the reported numbers of alcohol victims escalate as the news circulates.
