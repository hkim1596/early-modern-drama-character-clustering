¶ He did sende me to you, and I may say to you, Chaue brought you hether the price of a good vat cowe: Haue here is foure olde Ryalles, iche would they were a score, Before yesterday cha did not see them this twentie yere and more. ¶ When ych put my hande in my coffer, ych might then feele them, But chaue bene blinde this thirtie yere, so that ych could not see them: Therefore chill conclare the cause of my comming hether. ¶ You can conclout the matter much better then I. ¶ Chaue you may see be brought vp amongst swine and kye. Chill nowe declare the cause of my comming, ¶ Because cham riche and haue something to take too, There came of late a yong sprignall mee to woo: Nay he is a holsome yong man, of twentie yere olde and three, It does me good to thinke on him by our blest lady. He made full much on me, and loued me god woote, And cha loue him againe euen at the heart roote: So that we plight our trothes eche to other. And so at peruenient time to marie together. ¶ You may see Sir, olde women haue much clitter clatter. But I pray you good Sir beare with my budenes, ¶ What soeuerich say my meaning is no lesse, But my husband, my husband was so well fauoured, That a yong drabbe of him was enamoured, Who sayde vnto him that it was a great shame For him to marie such an olde beldame: And thus this yong drabbe my husband entised, To whom she doeth hope right shortly to be maried. But chad rather the yong whore were quicke at the deuill Than she should haue my husband, my sweet heart and iewell. My loue in my youth was neuer so feruent As it is on my sweeteheart, nowe at this present: Shall I neuer busse my sweete heart agayne, Nothing in the worlde would make me so fayne. ¶ I wishe but one night with him for to lye, Ohe, he would make me looke yong by and by. ¶ Yea, but he is good in my bedde to keepe my backe warme, And nowe and then chill kisse him and clippe him in my arme: Therefore my Lord for your friend Moneys sake, That I may haue my husbande I pray you vndertake. ¶ Yea, but the yonger he is, the better he is for me, ¶ There will no such be gotte my deare heart roote, ¶ Nowe Gods deare blessing light on that soote face of yours, Chaue taried to long by two or three howres. Nowe chill desire from hence to depart.

Plot summary

Plot — All for Money
The play satirizes the domination of money in a sequence of discrete episodes. Theology, Science, and Art lament that they are now primarily studied out ofa desire for money. Money becomes ill and vomits up Pleasure, who in turn gives birth to Sin in the same manner, whose progeny in turn is Damnation. Satan is pleased that Money’s wide popularity will consign so many souls to hell, and asks Sin to assist in the process. The relative values of money and learning are assessed in an encounter between four characters who have or lack them in various permutations: learning is shown to be superior to money, and poverty in this world carries the promise of a heavenly reward in the next; the clinching point is that Learning with Money gives alms to the poor where Money without Learning refuses. Money feels overworked and Sin gives him an associate, All for Money, who presides over a corrupt court: he pardons theft and assault, infanticide, and bigamy, but hangs a poor thief who is unable to bribe him. He also advises two suitors to procure false witnesses to support their causes, and protects a corrupt and incompetent cleric. Judas and Dives, two souls damned for monetary sins, lament their situation; Damnation drives them off to hell. Godly Admonition warns the audience not to repent too late, and to give up cupidity.
