To take the aire here in Saint Georges field, Sir Robert Brackenburie, and to visit some Poore patients that cannot visit me … . And all the rest, oh were the king returnde, There might be hope, but ere his comming home, They may be tride, condemnd, and iudgde, and dead. Ile rack my credit, and wil lanch my crownes, To saue their liues, if they haue done no murther. Pray for me friends and I will pray for you, God send you better hap then I expect, Go to my lodging you, and if I perish, Take what is there in lieu of your true seruice. Euen what you wil (great Quéen) here do I lie, Humble and prostrate at your Highnesse feet, Inflict on me what may reuenge your wrong, Was neuer lambe abode more patiently, Then I will doe. Call all your griefes to mind, And do euen what you will, or how likes you, I will not stirre, I wil not shrike or crie, Be it torture poison, any punishment, Was neuer Done, or Turtle more submisse, Then I wil be vnto your chastisement.
