My Knight with a brace of Footmen, is come and brought vp his Ewe Mutton, to find a Ram at London, I muſt haſten it, or elſe picke a Famine, her Bloods mine, and that's the ſureſt. Well Knight, that choyſe ſpoy is onely kept for me. Turne not to me till thou mayſt lawfully, it but whets my ſtomacke, which is too ſharpe ſet already. Read that note carefully, keepe me from ſuſpition ſtill, nor know my zeale but in thy Heart: read and ſend but thy liking in three words, I'le be at hand to take it. How ſtrangely buſie is the Diuell and riches, Poore Soule kept in too hard, her Mothers Eye, is cruell toward her, being to him, 'twere a good mirth now to ſet him a worke to make her wedding Ring. I muſt about it. Rather then the gaine ſhould fall to a Stranger, 'twas honeſtie in me to enrich my Father. O nothing now, all that I wiſh is preſent. I would haue a wedding Ring made for a Gentlewoman, with all ſpeed that may be. Of ſome halfe ounce, ſtand faire and comely, with the Sparke of a Diamond. Sir 'twere pittie to loſe the leaſt grace. So is the Miſtris. Yes ſure I thinke I haue her meaſure about me, good faith 'tis downe, I cannot ſhow't you, I muſt pull too many things out to be certaine. Let me ſee, long, and ſlender, and neatly ioynted, Iuſt ſuch another Gentlewoman that's your Daughter Sir. I proteſt I neuer ſaw two Maids handed more alike I'le nere ſeeke farther, if you'le giue me leaue Sir. I, and I'le bide all loſſe Sir. Shall I make bold with your finger Gentlewoman? Plot — A Chaste Maid in Cheapside
Sir Walter Whorehound lives high on the expectation of an inheritance: unless Sir Oliver Kix produces an heir, his estate will pass to Sir Walter — and Sir Oliver is impotent. Sir Walter maintains Allwit and his wife, paying for everything, in return for exclusive sexual access to Mistress Allwit: he is the true father of the children presumed to be Allwit’s. But Sir Walter is ===== PAGE 327 ===== 1613 planning to discontinue the arrangement and get married: the goldsmith Yellowhammer has agreed to marry his son Tim to a Welsh gentlewoman whom Sir Walter has introduced as his niece, and Sir Walter hopes himself to marry Tim’s sister Moll. Moll has other ideas: her sweetheart Touchwood plans to elope with her, and has Yellowhammer himself make the wedding ring, umaware of the bride’s identity. The elopement is foiled, however: Yellowhammer and Sir Walter intervene just as Touchwood is putting the ring onto Moll’s finger, and Moll is taken home and locked up. Touchwood’s elder brother is poor but virile, and cannot afford to cohabit with his wife for fear that she may produce a legitimate child he will not have the means to support. He has fathered a prolific number of bastards, and refuses to acknowledge one of them when a former paramour, a country wench, brings it to him. She then disposes of the baby by duping two corrupt promoters set to prevent the illicit trade in meat during Lent: she hides the child in a basket and allows them to confiscate it as contraband. Lady Kix’s maid suggests that Touchwood Seniors excessive fertility might solve Sir Oliver’s problems in begetting an heir; Sir Oliver agrees to pay him £400 for the job. Mistress Allwit gives birth to another of Sir Walter’s children. Tim Yellowhammer arrives home from Cambridge and is sent to see his mother at the christening party; he is priggish about being in female company, contrary to university rules, and the women are drunkenly embarrassing. Encouraged to woo his fiancée, Tim finds it heavy going: he tries to converse with her in Latin, she with him in Welsh. Allwit learns of Sir Walter’s plans for Moll, and tries to forestall them by telling Yellowhammer that his prospective son-in-law is a notorious whoremonger, but Yellowhammer is unconcerned by this. Moll disappears from home: she and Touchwood Junior have eloped again, with the help of the London watermen; but her mother drags her home by the hair.
