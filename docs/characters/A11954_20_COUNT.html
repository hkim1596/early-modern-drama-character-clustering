Full speech

Porter, remember what I gaue in charge, And when you haue done ſo, bring the Keyes to me. The Plot is layd, if all things fall out right, I ſhall as famous be by this exploit, As Scythian Tomyris by Cyrus death. Great is the rumour of this dreadfull Knight, And his atchieuements of no leſſe account: Faine would mine eyes be witneſſe with mine eares, To giue their cenſure of theſe rare reports. And he is welcome: what? is this the man? Is this the Scourge of France? Is this the Talbot, ſo much fear'd abroad? That with his Name the Mothers ſtill their Babes? I ſee Report is fabulous and falſe. I thought I ſhould haue ſeene ſome Hercules, A ſecond Hector, for his grim aſpect, And large proportion of his ſtrong knit Limbes. Alas, this is a Child, a ſilly Dwarfe: It cannot be, this weake and writhled ſhrimpe Should ſtrike ſuch terror to his Enemies. What meanes he now? Goe aske him, whither he goes? If thou be he, then art thou Priſoner. To me, blood-thirſtie Lord: And for that cauſe I trayn'd thee to my Houſe. Long time thy ſhadow hath been thrall to me, For in my Gallery thy Picture hangs: But now the ſubſtance ſhall endure the like, And I will chayne theſe Legges and Armes of thine, That haſt by Tyrannie theſe many yeeres Waſted our Countrey, ſlaine our Citizens, And ſent our Sonnes and Husbands captiuate. Laugheſt thou Wretch? Thy mirth ſhall turne to moane. Why? art not thou the man? Then haue I ſubſtance too. This is a Riddling Merchant for the nonce, He will be here, and yet he is not here: How can theſe contrarieties agree? Victorious Talbot, pardon my abuſe, I finde thou art no leſſe then Fame hath bruited, And more then may be gathered by thy ſhape. Let my preſumption not prouoke thy wrath, For I am ſorry, that with reuerence I did not entertaine thee as thou art. With all my heart, and thinke me honored, To feaſt ſo great a Warrior in my Houſe.

Plot summary

Plot — 1 Henry the Sixth
King Henry Vis dead, and England's French lands are lost. After a military setback, the French accept the offered aid of Joan Pucelle , who claims supernatural powers from the Virgin Mary; but they also release Talbot in an exchange of prisoners. With Joan 's help the French recapture the occupied town of Orleans, but the English take it back in a night attack led by Talbot. The Countess of Auvergne invites Talbot to visit her. intending treachery, but he second-guesses her and brings soldiers with him. In England, feuds brew between Gloucester and Winchester, and between Richard Plantagenet and the Duke of Somerset, whose supporters show their allegiance by picking different coloured roses. Richard , who has been deprived of his titles through the attainder of his father, learns from the imprisoned and dying Mortimer that he has a claim to the throne . King Henry restores him to his inheritance, creating him Duke of York. Joan takes Rouen by a ruse , but Talbot recaptures it the same day; she then persuades England's ally Burgundy to join the French. After King Henry's coronation in Paris, York is left in command of Prance, but personal tension between him and Somerset means that troop reinforcements arrive late; in consequence, Talbot is defeated, surviving only long enough to learn that his son has perished in battle. Joan conjures devils, but they will not help her: she is captured by York and burned at the stake. The French and English agree a peace which will entail King Henry making a diplomatic marriage, but this is pre-empted when Suffolk captures Margaret and (having fallen in love with her but being alr eady married) plans to install her as Queen of England . Henry agrees to marry her, and Suffolk goes to France to mili the arrangements.
