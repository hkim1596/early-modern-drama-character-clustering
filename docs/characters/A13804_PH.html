Thou dost well to meditate on these three things at once for th ile bee found out altogither, ad gra cas calendas, but let them passe and cary the conceite, I told you this morning to the partie you wot of. In my immagination tis Capritious, t'will take I warrant thee. O I thinke thou meanest him that made 19. sonnets of his mistris Busk-point; Some netling stuffe yfaith; let him write thus ▪ Most heart commanding fac't Gentlewoman, uen as the stone in India called Basaliscus, hurts all that lookes on it: and as the Serpent in Arabia called Smaragdu delighteth the sight, so does thy celestiall orbe assimilating eyes, both please, and in pleasing wound my loue-darted heart. Had you staid a little longer 'twould haue bin starke rotte ̄ . Iupiter, Iupiter, I had thought these Flyes had bit none but my selfe, do Critticks tickle you yfaith? By your leaue Memory you are not alone troubled, Chronologers many of them are so so Phantasticke, as when they bring a Captaine to the Combate, lifting vp his reuengefull arme to dispart the head of his enemie, they'le hold vp his armes so long till they haue bestowed three or foure pages in describing the gold hilts of his threating Fauchion. So that in my Fancie the reader may well wonder his aduersary stabs him not, before he strikes, moreouer they are become most palpable flatterers alwaies begging at my gates for Inuention. But not till our backes breake, lud there was neuer any so haunted as I am, this da e there comes a Sophister to my house, knocks at my dore, his errand being ask'd, forsoth his an swere was to borrow a faire sute of conceites out of my war drop, to apparraile a shewe he had in hand, and what thinke you is the plot? Meane-while he's somewhat acquainted with you, for he's bould to bring your person vpon the stage. Yes you and you, and my selfe with all my Phantisticall tricks and humors ▪ but I trow I haue fitted him with Fooleries I trust heele neuer troble me againe. Thinke what you will of it, I thinke 'tis done, and I thinke it is acting by this time; ha ke ha ke, what drummings yonder, Ile laye my life they are com to present the shewe I spake off. Yes, yes stand still. What Pothecary durst be so bold as make such a con fection: ha what poison ist? But here you Madam, I pray you let your Pages tongue walke with vs a little, till you returne againe. Hot youths I protest, saw you those warlike preparations? I promise you a fearfull troupe of Souldiers. Who akes Tactus his parte? Ima gine theres old mouing amongst them: me thinks a handfull of nuttes would turne them all out of their Souldiers coates. In my foolish immagination Olfactus is very like the God desse of victory that neuer takes any part but the Conquerers. I coniecture hee intends to see he ight and after the ba taile to feede his Dogges, Hoggs and Vultures vpon the murdred cases. What blew thing's that, that's dappled so with Starres. In my conceit it were pritty, if hee thundred when hee speakes. How now Visus is your heauen at a stay? Or is it his Motus trepidationis that makes him stammer: I pray you Memory set him ag e againe. Nay rather it seemes hee's of Copernicus opinion, and that makes him stand still. You know Memory that was an extreame hotte day, & 'tis likely Terra sweat much, and so tooke cold presently after, that euer since she hath lost her voyce. I know this fellow of old, 'tis a Herauld, many a Cen taure, Chimera, Barnacle, Crocadile, Hippotame, and such like toyes, hath he stolne out of the shop of my Inuention, to shape new coates for his vpstart Gentlemen. Plot — Lingua, or The Combat of the Tongue and the Five Senses for Superiority
Lingua wants to be a sense, but feels that the five established senses are preventing her from pleading her cause with the judge Common Sense. Having failed to appeal to the senses’ various proclivities, she decides to cause discord between them. She is in possession of a crown and robe which were to have been the prize in a public-speaking competition, but which came to her, as the patroness of oratory, when the five contestants were all adjudged equal; now the inscription promising the crown to the greatest of the five will put the five senses at odds. Tactus comes upon the robe and crown: he puts them on, gets delusions of grandeur, and unsuccessfully tries to conceal his find from the other senses. When Lingua’s page Mendacio tells them that the objects were left by Mercury, they begin to quarrel over precedence and mobilize troops against one another. Lingua reports the discord to Common Sense, and Mendacio describes the various animals in the armies. Common Sense defuses the conflict by deciding to judge the senses’ relative worthiness: each sense will present his case in a show. While the shows are in preparation, he hears Lingua’s petition to become the sixth sense: she asserts her importance as a medium of communication; but the senses put in a counter-plea drawing attention to her misdeeds. The shows are performed. Visus presents Light and Colour, Heaven and Earth, while Auditus offers Comedy and Tragedy. Olfactus’ show features Odour and Tobacco, currently the most popular of scents, while Bacchus and Ceres turn out for Gustus. Tactus has problems: he had intended to present a show of women, but his performer has spent too long getting dressed and made up, and is late; he has to make do with putting his case verbally. Common Sense makes his judgement: Visus and Auditus, the senses which feed the soul, have precedence over those which only nourish the body, and Visus is awarded the crown; but Tactus is given the robe, and the others get court appointments. Lingua may not be a sense - there can be only five — but Common Sense decrees that women shall henceforth 1606 have a sixth, speaking. Newly appointed as court purveyor, Gustus invites the company to supper. Lingua chafes at the outcome — her rivals brought to amity with one another and herself made only half a sense - and plots more mischief.
