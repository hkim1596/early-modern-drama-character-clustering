Full speech

I much suspect that Ladies Chastity. I haue said. Now heare my protestations: I receiue This Ladies hand on these Conditions; If you, my Lord, her father, or her selfe, Know her selfe faulty, Oh confesse it here, Before the Ceremonies fasten on me: for if hereafter I find you once corrupted? by this right hand, My future hopes, my Fathers royalty, And all the honours due vnto our house, He haue as many liues and heads for it, As he hath Manners Castles, Liues and Towres; It shall be worthy to be bookt in Chronicles Of all strange tongues: And therefore beautious Lady, As you esteeme a Prince his name or honour, That youd be a Mecenas vnto vertue; If in the least of these you guilty be, Pull backe your hand. If chaste? she shall be dearer farre to me. Then my owne soule: I will respect her honour, Equall with that of my great Ancestours; All this I vow, as I am Prince and vertuous. Shee's mine: Set forwards then. Oh friend, was neuer man blest with a Bride So chast! I'me fearce my selfe, till this be knowne To my faire Forrest friend: Lett's mount away, The nights quite spent; and now begins the day. Good morrow Lady: Wher's your daughter pray? Pray may wee see her? You know the Princesse Iulia was suppos'd to bee Adulterate— Oh but faire friend, she was indeed bely'd! And I this morning rose from her chast bed: But wherefore sweete cast you that blushing smile? But you haue broak promise with me: For you told me That the same day and houre I took my Bride, You should Inioy a Princely Husband. And are you married then? Is hee oft fortunes? What haue you then, some tokens that were his? You amaze mee! Yet Rings may bee alike: If then your husband Bee of such state and fortunes, What dowre are you allotted. I gaue no more vnto my Iulia. But where is the security you haue For the performance of it? This is the Indenture that I gaue to Iulia; Preethee Lauretta, but resolue me true, How came you by this Charter? Rise, dee not weepe, Oh I am strangely rapt Into deepe strange confusion? I haue bethought already how to be are mee; This Charter and this Ring, faire Loue, keepe you; And when I send for you, you shall repaire Vnto the Court: This all I shall inioyne you. Come Mounsieur, now 'tis cast, Reuenge neere rules, so it be found at last. I doe protest, my Lords, I bosom'd with As true and chaste a Virgin, as ere lodg'd Within a Princes armes; All this I vow As I am Royall. Sweet, will you tel mee that? With which you did receiue a Ring the Duke My father gaue me. Nay, looke not you deiected beautious Bride, For this is done onely to honour you. Sure spare't for the Mothers sake; t'was sent to vs: Which of the trayne is wanting? Stroza, where is this Charter and the Ring? A strife, that nought saue combat can deside, The cause so full of doubts, and intricate. See, they are both arm'd, and euenly, without odds, Saue what the iustice of the cause can yeeld. Know Parma, thou hast kindled such a Flame, That all the Oceans billowes scarce can quench: Bee that onr quarrells ground.
