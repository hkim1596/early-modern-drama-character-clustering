Rise, you haue it sir. Speake your intents sir. As a subiect we giue you freedome. You displease vs, you are too bold. Sir, you wrong the Prince, I gaue you not this freedome, go to, be better temperde. Phylaster, tell me the iniuries you aime at in your riddles. Giue me your wrongs in priuat. Go to, be more your selfe, as you respect our fauour, youle stirre vs else: sir I must haue you know, that you are, and shalbe at our pleasure, what fashion we will put vpon you, smooth your selfe, ore or by the gods Hees possest. Away I doe not like this, I'le make you tamer, or i'le dispossesse you both of your life & spirit: for this time I pardon your wild speech, without so much as impri sonment. I feare their outrage, lest it should extend With dangering of Pharamonts life. How happie am I in thee Phylaster? Whose excellent vertues begets a world of loue, I am indebted to thee for a Kingdome. I here surrender vp all Soueraignetie, Raigne peacefully with thy espoused Bride, Ashume my Son to take what is thy due.
