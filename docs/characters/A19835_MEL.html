And we that neuer were accustomed To quarrell for our bounds, how do we see Montanus and Acrysius inter-striue How farre their seuerall Sheep-walkes should extend, And cannot be agreed do what we can: As if some vnderworking hand strake fire, To th'apt inkindling tinder of debate, And fostred their contention and their hate. Besides their various habits grow so strange, As that although their faces certaine are, Their bodies are vncertaine euery day, And alwayes diffring from themselues so far, As if they scorn'd to be the same they are. And all of vs are so transformd, that we Discerne not an Arcadian by th attyre, Our ancient Pastorall habits are dispisd, And all is strange, hearts, clothes, and all disguisd. Content Ergastus, and euen here will be A place conuenient for so fit a worke: For here our Nimphes, and heardsmen on this Greene, Do vsually resort, and in this Groue We may obserue them best, and be vnseene. Tis true. And what an instrument hath he there got, To be the Agent of his villany? How truely she negotiats, and doth plot, To vndermine fraile imbecillitie. How strong, these spirits, combine them in a knot, To circum ent plaine open honesty? And then to see how soone example will Disperse it selfe, being met with our desire: How soone, it will nkindle others ill, Like Naptha, that takes fire by sight of fire? So that vnlesse we runne with all the speed We can, to quench this new arising flame. Of vanitie, and lust, it will proceed T'vndoe vs, ere we shall perceiue the same: How farre already is the mischiefe runne, Before we scarse perceiu'd it was begun? And see that other vaine fantastick spirit, Who would corrupt out bodies too likewise, As this our mindes, and make our health to be As trouolesome as sicknesse, to deuise, That no part of vs euer should be free; Both forraging on our credulitie, Take still th'aduantage of our weakenesses; Both cloath their friuolous vncertainties In strange attyres, to make it seene the lesse. Well, come Ergastus, we haue seene ynow, And it is more then time, that we prepare Against this Hydra of confusion now, Which still presents new hideous heads of feare: And euery houre we see begets new broiles, And intricates our youth in desperate toyles. And therefore let th'aduantage of this day, Which is the great, and generall hunting day In Eremanthus, serue for this good deed And when we meete (as all of vs shall meete Here in this place anone, as is decreed) We will aduise our Shepheards to intermit That worke, and fall to this imports vs more, To chase out these wild mischiefes that doe lurke, And worse infest, then th' Eremanthian Boare, O ll Beasts else, which onely spoile our fields, Whilst these which are of more prodigious kindes, Bend all their forces to destroy our mindes. Plot summary

Plot — The Queen's Arcadia (Arcadia Reformed)
Arcadia is no longer the place of pastoral simplicity it once was: old shepherds have grown litigious, young ones are falling in love unwisely and unrequitedly, and the nymphs, who seem less beautiful than before, have abandoned the modest simplicity of traditional Arcadian dress in favour of quirky new fashions. The problems arise from foreign influences on this previously sequestered land. Colax has travelled abroad, and brought back with him the city woman Techne, and they are selfishly interfering in the love- lives of the nymphs and shepherds: they have made Silvia and Palaemon jealous of one another with mendacious tales of infidelity; and Colax has persuaded Dorinda to jilt Myrtillus in his favour, making her the latest in a string of sexual conquests. Meanwhile Lincus, a lawyer, and Doctor Alcon have both come from town to set up practices in Arcadia. Lincus has found the local amity bad for business: the Arcadians do not covet one another’s property and so never go to law. For Alcon, however, the Arcadians’ fellow-feeling is profitable, because when one falls ill, others follow suit, and they all want the same medicine from him — which is just as well, because he has only one to offer, and that is a placebo. Both are keen to drum up trade, so Lincus has set Montanus and Acrisius against one another, and Alcon has introduced the Arcadians to an unhealthy habit: tobacco-smoking. Carinus (who has recently jilted Amaryllis) and Amyntas are rivals for the love of Cloris, and argue about which of them has the greater command of her affections; but in fact Cloris loves neither of them, and has sworn chastity until she finds a worthy man. Colax now wants a change of girlfriend, and asks Techne to make Cloris love him. Techne offers to give Cloris a new outfit, and arranges for them to meet at a cave so she can try it on; but it is Colax who keeps the appointment, and attempts to rape her. Techne herself has fallen for Amyntas, and arranges for him to see Cloris meeting Colax. Amyntas becomes suicidal, so Techne sends Cloris to stop him; but she arrives too late, and he has already taken poison.
