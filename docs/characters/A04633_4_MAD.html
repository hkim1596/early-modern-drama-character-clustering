Full speech

Aboue with my Ladies women, reading verses. I did aske him if hee were Amphibion Broker. I faith Sir, and I will try. O! it shall be palpable, Make thee runne thorow a hoope, or a thombe-ring, The nose of a tabacco pipe, and draw Thy ductile bones out, like a knitting needle, To serue my subtill turnes. I haue heard you ha' offered Sir, to lock vp smoake, And cauke your windores, spar up all your doores, Thinking to keepe it a close prisoner wi'you, And wept, when it went out, Sir, at your chimney. Come, let's thither then, I ha' supt in Apollo! No, But with two Gentlewomen, call'd, the Graces ▪ This was truth, Sir. The same. He holds no man can be a Poet, That is not a good Cooke, to know the palats, And seuerall tastes o'the time. He drawes all Arts Out of the Kitchin, but the Art of Poetry, which he concludes the same with Cookery. Hee's a rare fellow, without question! but He holds some Paradoxes. And knowes the Clergies tast! Away then. We ieere all kind of persons We meete withall, of any rancke or quality, And if we cannot ieere them, we ieere our selues. They were the madder sort. I know. Why? of an Aduocate, he grew the Clyent. And shew'd the Law of nature Was there aboue the Common-Law. We call'd him a Coat-card O'the last order. Some readings haue it so, my manuscript Doth speake it, arlet. Or Arch-priest o' Canters, Nay, I'll not lose my argument, Lickfinger ; Before these Gentlemen, I affirme, The perfect, and true straine of poetry, Is rather to be giuen the quicke Celler, Then the fat Kitchin. It is granted. I haue almost done, I want but e'ne to finish. It is a Madrigall, I affect that kind Of Poem, much. I made it to the tune the Fidlers play'd, That we all lik'd so well. The Sunne is father of all mettalls, you know, Siluer, and gold. Now there, I want a line to finish, Sir. So takes she place! Who'ld lie in a roome, with a close-stoole, and garlick? And kennell with his dogges? that had a Prince Like this young Peny-boy, to soiourne with? Scatter your selfe amongst vs— The sacke, the sacke! Barbers are at hand. You haue now no money, A snarling Rascall, hence. A rare fellow! In troth A diuine Whimsey ! Hee's aliue, me thinks. Bate you rather. Nor Statute. He will turne the better ieerer. Let's do't in noyse. Charge, man o' warre. Your Phessants, & fat Swans ? But such as will not keepe your Capitol ? Or turne pure Iack-a-Lent after all this? On dew. But, by that time, he'll ha' no blood to reare 'hem. They barke, se defend ndo.
