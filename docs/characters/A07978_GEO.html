Yet never can the mixture Of gold with clay make any transformation Of that base matter into purer metall. You swell her praise too high; so meane a subject fits not these raptures. If she be So rare a piece, her low condition Makes me suspitious shee's some common wanton Lurks in that maske for safety. Nay rather hide her from me. She may raise motions; and if I should rivall thee, I must be serv'd: nothing was ere devis'd To fright libidinous nature from it's pronenesse, That can restraine me. She seems a handsome piece. That opportunity Would play the Bawd a little. They are arriv'd: let us prepare our selves. I hate women! Now by my love of pleasure, no delight Hath any relish on the wanton palat Of my desires; unlesse some mixture season it That is deriv'd from them. She takes me for an Eunuch. Sure my friend Hath overdone his part; and drawne the counterfeit Too neere the life of truth. Sweet Mistris heare The language of my heart that cannot glosse My plainer thoughts with superficiall words. I love you; my desires are throughly fir'd, And burne my bloud: which but your free enjoying Nothing can quench. I thinke you are; and one made up for pleasure, More then the dull converse of what's defective. Fie, that's a grosse construction; onely shame And common knowledge doth it, not the act Of a Wifes wantonnesse. A pledge for what should follow. No more of that. Nay, this way. Your will disposeth mine: we there may finde Handsome conveniences; and I'le renew My counterfeit of woman-hater: it May cast a mist before his jealous eyes Would watch us else. Exeunt. This is his Milke-maid sure. I still suspected 'Twas some disguis'd name to conceale a Mistris.
