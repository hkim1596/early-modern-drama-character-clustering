Sir, a duety well discharg'd is neuer follow'd By sad repentance, nor did your Highnesse euer Make payment of the debt you ow'd her, better Then in your late reproofes not of her, but Those crimes that made her worthy of reproofe. The most remarkeable point in which kings differ From priuate men, is, that they not alone Stand bound to be in themselues innocent, But that all such as are allide to them In neeren esse, or dependance, by their care Should be free from suspition of all crime; And you haue reap'd a double benefit From this last great act: first in the restraint Of her lost pleasures, you remoue th'example From oth of the like licentiousnesse, Then when 'tis knowne that your seueritie Extended to your mother, who dares hope for The least Indulgence or conniuence in The easiest slips that may proue dangerous To you or to the kingdome? Beasts of that nature when rebellious threats Begin to appeare only in their eies, Or any motion that may giue suspition Of the least violence, should be chaind vp; Their fanges and teeth, and all their meanes of hurt, Pard of, and knock'd out, and so made vnable To do ill; they would soone begin to loath it. I'le apply nothing, but had you grace done, Or would do yet, what your lesse forward zeale In words did only threaten, far lesse danger Would grow from acting it on her, then may Perhaps haue being from her apprehension Of what may once be practis'd: for beleeue it, Who confident of his owne power, presumes To spend threates on an enimy, that hath meanes To shun the worst they can effect, giues armor To keepe off his owne strength; nay more, disarmes himselfe, and lies vngarded gainst all harmes Or doubt, or malice may produce. Believing that Theodoret was not his brother and that Ordella is dead, Thierry offers to make amends by marrying Memberge, Theodoret's daughter. Brunhild is forced to admit her lies in order to prevent an incestuous marriage. She gives Thierry a poisoned handkerchief, which kills him; Martel brings Ordella to his deathbed. Brunhild admits complicity in his murder, and is sentenced to remain awake for the rest of her life; disturbed by the sight of Protaldi being tortured to death, she commits suicide.
