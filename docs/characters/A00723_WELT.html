I, but all your's haue, for you keepe none my Lord: Besides though it stands with your Honor to forget mens businesses; yet it stands not with your Honor, if you doe not doe a womans. Ti's vp my Lord, ope your eies, but I pray tell mee Is this antipathie twixt bright steele and you naturall, or how grew it? This Lord, and this knight in dogge-collers would make a fine brace of beagles. So does my Lord, something. Saue you my Lord. Why very fairelie my Lord, on honorable tearmes, Young Bould was iniur'd and did challenge him, Fought in the field, and the other gaue him satisfaction Vnder his hand, I was Boulds second, and can shew it heere. So doe not I. He lies that saies so, and ile make it good, And for I know my frend is out of towne, What Man soeuer wrongs him is my foe, I say he had full satisfaction, Nay that which wee may call submission: That the other sought peace first, and who denies this, Lord, Knight, or Gentleman, English, French, or Scot, I'le fight and proue it on him with my sword. Well and il'e be as good as my word. Agreed my Lord, and toward the Euening il'e car rie you to the Companie. S'hart call him foole againe. It may be shee's stolne out to see a play. Come to the Swaggerers. Pray welcome this Gentleman. Faith hee's a little faulty that way: somewhat of a bashfull and backward nature, yet I haue brought him amongst you, because he hath a great desire to be flesh'd. Sir, he cannot endure the sight of steele. Harke you my Lo: what will you say, if I make you beate all these out of the roome? Well drinke apace, if any braue you out braue him, Ile second you, they are a Companie of cowards beleeue me. Well said, speake to him man. Why how now my Lord, a Morralist? Why, Mounser Whore-bang? I am no play maker, and for pledging your healths, I loue none of the foure, you drank to so well. Shall I? Nay Ile beare him witnesse in 〈◊〉 truth, his soule lies fort my Lord. Rorer you lie. Why now thou art a worthy wight, indeed a Lord a Lorne. Fie no my Lord. Oh braue Lord, manie a rorer thus is made by wine: come it is one of their heads my Lord. Drawer, take your plate, for the reckoning there's some of their cloakes: I will be no shot-log to such. You see, we made bould with your lodging, indeed, I did assure my selfe, you were fast for this night. S'foote man, I carried him last night among the Rorers, to flesh him, and by this light he got drunke, and beate e'm all. Oh God Sir, I thinke in my conscience, he will eate steele shortlie, I know not how his conuersion will hold after this sleepe, but in an houre or two (last night) he was growne such a little dam-me, that I protest, I was afraid of the spirit, that I my selfe had rais'd in him: but this other matter of your expulsion thus mads me to the heart; Were you in bed with her? I'le e hang'd, if you were not busie to soone, you should haue let her slept first. But is't possible thou should'st haue no more wit, would'st thou come away vpon any tearmes, but sure ones, hauing night, her chamber and her selfe naked in thine armes? By that light, if I had a sonne of 4 whom I had help 't thus farre, that had seru'd me so, I would breech him. Haue done? done, done twice at least. Pish, Tarquin was a block-head, if he had had any wit and could haue spoke, Lucr ce had neuer been rauished, she would haue yeelded, I warrant thee, & so wil any woman God's pretious, it makes me mad, when I thinke on't: was there euer such an obsur'd trick? now will she a buse thee horriblie, say thou art a faint-hearted fellow, a milk-sop and I know not what, as indeede thou art. Z'oones, I would I had, I would haue so jumbl'd her honestie: would'st thou be held out at staues end with words? dost not thou know a widdow's a weake vessell, and is easily cast if you close.
