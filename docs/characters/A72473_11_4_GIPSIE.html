Full speech

YOur pardon Ladie, here you ſtand, If ſome ſhould judge you by your hand The greateſt fellon in the Land Detected: I cannot tell you by what Arts, But you have ſtolne ſo many hearts, As they would make you at all parts Suſpected. Your very face firſt, ſuch a one As being view'd it was alone, Too ſlipperie to be lookt upon; And threw men. But then your graces they were ſuch, As none could er'e behold too much; Both ev'ry taſte, and ev'ry touch So drew men. Still bleſt in all you thinke, or doe, Two of your Sons are Gipſies too, You ſhall our Queene be, and ſee who Importunes The heart of either yours, or you; And doth not wiſh both George, and Sue, And every Barne beſides, all new Good fortunes. I finde by this hand You have the command Of the very beſt mans houſe i'the land: Our Captaine, and wee, Ere long will ſee If you keepe a good table; Your Maſter's able. And here be bountifull lines that ſay You'le keepe no part of his bounty away. Thus written to Franke On your Venus banke; To prove a falſe ſteward you'le find much adoe, Being a true one by blood, and by office too. HOw right he doth confeſſe him in his face, His browe, his eye, and ev'ry marke of State; As if he were the iſſue of each Grace, And bore about him both his fame, and fate. LOoke, looke, is hee not faire, And freſh, fragrant too As Summer skie, or purged Aire, And lookes as Lillies doe, That were this morning blowne. Oh more! that more of him were knowne. INdeed hee's not Lord alone of all the State, But of the love of men, and of the Empires fate. The Muſes Arts, the Schooles commerce, our honours lawes, And Vertues hang on him, as on their working cauſe.

Plot summary

Plot — The Gypsies Metamorphosed
A troop of gypsies arrive with children and stolen meat, and proceed to read the palms and tell the fortunes of the King, the Prince, and various members of the aristocratic audience. Some rustics turn up, attracted by the sound of the Gypsies’ dancing. They decide to have a dance themselves with their wenches, and collect money to pay the musicians. The Gypsies steal their purses and other possessions, but then return the items to their pockets by legerdemain. The rustics are interested in the Gypsies’ place of origin, Devil’s Arse, and ask where the name came from. A song is sung by way of an answer, telling of how the first captain of the Gypsies, Cock-Lorel, entertained the Devil to a banquet, with numerous different varieties of human flesh on the menu; the Devil blew the feast away with a fart and so gave the place its name. The Gypsies are metamorphosed into white-skinned lords, and praise the King. C-Text After the Cock-Lorel song, the rustics are seized with the desire to become Gypsies themselves, and the Patrico tells them what a long process their initiation would be.
