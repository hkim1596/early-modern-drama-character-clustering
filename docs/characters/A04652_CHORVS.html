Spring all the Graces of the age, And all the Loues of time; Bring all the pleasures of the stage, And relishes of rime: Add all the softnesses of Courts, The lookes, the laughters, and the sports. And mingle all their sweets, and salts, Thai none may say, the Triumph halts. The Masquers dance their Entry or first dance. Which done, the first Prospectiue, a Mari time Palace, or the house of Oceanus is discouered to lowd Musique. The other aboue is no more seene. Your lookes, your smiles, and thoughts that meete. Ambrosian hands, and siluer feete, Do promise you will do't. The Reuels follow. Which ended, the Fleete is discouered, while the three Corners play. And may thy subiects hearts be all one flame. Whilst thou dost keepe the earth in firme estate, And 'mongst the winds, do'st suffer no debate, But both at Sea, and Land, our powers increase, With health, and all the golden gifts of Peace.

Plot summary

Plot — The Fortunate Isles and Their Union
Merefool has been duped by the Rosicrucians, and after nine days’ abstinence, he is becoming impatient for results. The invisible spirit Johphiel plays a trick on him, telling him that he is now to assume high office in Castle Rosy-Cross. In the meantime, he wants to see some famous personages from antiquity: he asks for poets and philosophers, but Johphiel can only supply Scogan and Skelton; the figures whom Merefool requested are busy, he explains. Scogan and Skelton appear and present an antimasque of characters from old demotic literature. Merefool is dismissed as a gull. Johphiel announces that the Fortunate Isles of Macaria and Britannia are to be joined. Macaria arrives, bearing its inhabitants. Dancing ensues.
