'Tis well, the time has bin when your smooth tongue Would not haue mock'd my griefes, and had I bin more Chary of mine honor, you had still bin lowly as you were. Breake vowes on your side, I expect no other, But euery day looke when some newer choice May violate your honour and my trust. But shall I be resolu'd you will be mine? Pray Sir vouchsafe me hearing. Sir, by the honor of my mothers hearse, He has protested marriage, pledg'd his faith: If vowes haue any force, I am his wife. You are too violent, his truth will proue His constancy, and so excuse my fault. Alas, to me he did. We are quite ruin'd. True, Colona, Betray'd to infamy, deceiu'd and mock'd By an vnconstant Villaine; what shall's doe? I am with childe. This; with cunning words First proue his loue; he knowes I am with child. Now by the starres he did the like to me, And said, 'twas well I was so hap'ly sped. Peace, here's a noyse me thinkes: Let's rise, wee'll take a time to talke of this? Let's fall amongst'em,—Loue—how is't chick? ha. Loue, you must goe with me. Not me! Notable. Madam, though strangers, yet we vuderstand Your wrongs doe equall ours; which to reuenge, Please but to ioyne with vs, and wee'll redeeme Our losse of honour, by a braue exploit. B … not amaz'd, great Princes, but vouchsafe Your audience; we are they haue done this deed: Looke here, the pledges of this false mans lust, Betray'd in our simplicities: He swore, And pawn'd his truth to marry each of vs; Abus'd vs all, vnable to reuenge Our publike shames, but by his publike fall, Which thus we haue contri … 'd; nor doe we blush To call the glory of this murther ours: We did it, and wee'll iustifie the deed. For when in sad complaints we claym'd his vowes, His answer was reproach; villaine, is't true? I (and I neuer shall forget the wrong) I was not faire enough, not faire enough For thee, thou monster; let me cut his gall, Not faire enough! oh scorne! not faire enough? Fo, more then all; there's not an houre shall passe, But I shall haue intelligence, she sweares. Whole nights; you know my mind, I hope you'll giue The gowne you promis'd me. Neuer but one, I sweare.

Plot summary

Plot — 478
Eteocles and Polynices are elected co-kings of Thebes, ruling in alternate years. At the end of the first year, Eteocles refuses to stand down and banishes Polynices. Six champions join Polynices to besiege Thebes, but, after heavy losses, Polynices offers to settle the case by single combat. He and Eteocles kill one another, and Creon takes the crown. Tiresias prophesies that the walls of Thebes will stand only as long as the last of the seven champions remains alive. After Adrastus’ death, the Argive troops sack the city. 1569
