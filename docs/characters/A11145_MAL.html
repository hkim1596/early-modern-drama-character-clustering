WHen first you came from Florence, wud the world Had with an universal dire ecclipse Bin ouer whelm'd, no more to gaze on day, That you to Spaine had never found the way, Here to be lost for ever. To shew you, Madam, The picture of your selfe, but so defac'd, And mangled by proud Spanyards, it woo'd whet A sword to arme the poorest Florentine In your just wrongs. Here 'tis then: Time is not scarce foure dayes old. Plot summary

Plot — The Noble Spanish Soldier (The Noble Soldier, or A Contract Broken Justly Revenged)
The King of Spain claims to have doubts about the legality of his marriage: he was pre-contracted to Onaelia and had an illegitimate son by her. He sends the Cardinal to Onaelia with an offer to have the marriage annulled; she will then be instated as Queen of Spain and her son legitimized. She accepts, and hands over her marriage contract for redrafting. But it is only a ruse to get hold of the document, and the King burns it in the presence of his council. Meanwhile, having heard that the King is considering renewing his contract to Onaelia, Queen Paulina feigns pregnancy; any divorce will thus entail the unpopular step of bastardizing his unborn child. Onaelia’s uncle, the Duke of Medina, swears revenge on the King and begins to assemble a faction. The despised soldier Balthazar offers his services to assassinate the King, but when Onaelia refuses, says that he was only testing her. With the King’s complicity, Paulina engages Balthazar to murder Onaelia and her son Sebastian, but he betrays the plot to Medina, to whom he owes allegiance. Medina counter-plots: Balthazar is to report back that Sebastian has been killed but that Onaelia was too well protected; in reality, Sebastian will be hidden in a monastery. Medina tests Balthazar’s fidelity and the King’s malice by disguising himself as a French doctor and offering the King his services as a poisoner; the King’s acceptance of the offer as a second murder option should Balthazar fail, and Balthazar’s outrage, both tell him what he wants to know. Unable to dispose of Onaelia, the King formally marries her. Paulina plans to poison her at the wedding banquet, but the poisoned wine is served to the King by mistake. The ‘friar’ summoned to hear his dying confessions turns out to be Sebastian, which the King initially takes to be a guilty hallucination. Sebastian is proclaimed the rightful heir and Paulina sent back to Florence with her dowry trebled in compensation. The King dies, and Onaelia and Balthazar are appointed Sebastian’s joint guardians during his minority.
