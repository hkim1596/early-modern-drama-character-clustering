Whither running, Gorgon? Let me but see the directions. To my beloued friend Master Rufaldo: 'tis a lye, she was mistaken, it was I should owe this appellation, Ile not beleeue the superscription, 'tis a painted face, I'de see the heart on't. If you loue me, and wish me constant, be your owne friend, and let our marriage day begin with the next mor ning: thine, mine. Oh Selina, she's mad. All woman kinde is mad; and I am mad: whom shall I rend in pieces for my wrongs, and as with Atomes fill this poysned ayre, Rufaldo? Stay, is not she a creature rationall? oh no, there is no sparke of nature in her, all is sunke, lost for euer: stay, stay, see. This is Medias brother torne in pieces, And this the way where she with Iason flies, Tom Colchos, come not neere 'em, see, looke, That's an arme rent off. And the hand beckens vs To cry out murder. That's a leg o'th boy. There, there 'tis, head and yellow curled locks, His eyes are full of teares, now they doe stare, To see where all his other members lye. But she and Iason are both slipt, and Argos is Sayling home to Greece, see how the waues Doe tosse the Vessell, and the windes conspire To dash it 'gainst a Rocke, it rides vpon A watery mountaine, and is hid in cloudes, It cannot stay there, now, now, it tumbles, Three fatham beneath Hell, let 'em goe, Here comes the Father of Medea now, Calling in vaine vnto the gods, and spies His Sonnes limbes throwne about, in stead of flowers, To his Daughters nuptials he does take 'em vp, He knowes the face, and now he teares his haire, And raues, and cryes Medea, poore old man, Command a funerall pile for thy yong Childe, And lay the pretty limbs on, from whose ashes Shalt haue another Sonne i'th shape of Phoenix. Shall I? excellent! Prepare a fire All of sweet wood for my sweet boy, a fire, a fire. Ha, doe you know Selina? she's married to Rufaldo, the old Vsurer, that went to bed afore to his money, and begate forty ith' hundred: now he beds Selina, And layes his rude hand o're her sacred brest, Embraceth her faire body, now he dares Kisse her, and suck Ambrosia from her lip. Those eyes that grace the day, now shine on him, He her Endimion, she his siluer Moone, The tongue that's able to rock Heauen asleepe, And make the musicke of the Spheres stand still, To listen to the happier aires it makes, And mend their tunes by it: that voice is now Deuoted to his eares, those cheekes, those hands, Would make gods proud to touch, are by his touch Profaned uery houre: oh, this makes me mad, But I will fit 'em for it, for Ile die, It may be then she'le weepe, and let fall teares Vpon my graue-stone, which shall be of marble, And hard like her, that if she powre out floods, No drops shall sinke thorow it to soften me, I will be wrapt in Lead to keepe out praiers, For then I know, shee'le beg I would be friends: But then I will be iust, and hate her loue, As she did mine, and laugh to see her grieue. Will you then? I will liue, & you shall be my best boy. Come, I scorne to weepe, or shead another teare: Sit downe, Ile haue a Garland for my Boy, Of Phoenix feathers: Flowers are too meane To sit vpon thy temples; in thy face Are many Gardens, Spring had neuer such: The Roses and the Lillies of thy cheekes Are slips of Paradise, not to be gathered, But wondred at. I remember, before I lou'd, but that I know not when I slept soundly, and dreamt of gathering Nosegayes. 'Tis vnlucky to dreame of hearbs and flowers. Yes, and we will picke a dish of Strawberies. It cannot be she: troth boy, thy conceit tooke me at first with much credulity: but here's our natures weak nesse, apt to credit what wee affect: were there not too much against it, thou mightst deceiue me: oh no, Infortunio is giuen vp, lost to all felicity. What, has he got thee with child? Fie vpon't, no lesse then three Bastards. Plot summary

Plot — The School of Compliment (Love Tricks)
Selina and Hilaria have each attracted the attention of several suitors. Selina’s brother Antonio is one of those interested in Hilaria; and Hilaria’s recently widowed father Rufaldo, who disapproves of Antonio and would rather she married the wealthy Bubulcus, would nevertheless like Selina for his second wife. Antonio has his friend and servant distract Rufaldo while he sneaks in to visit Hilaria; they do it by persuading Rufaldo that he has been miraculously rejuvenated. Selina’s father is happy to let her have her own way in ===== PAGE 140 ===== her love-life, because her sister Felice absconded after he interfered in hers. He proposes that she should be cautious about marrying Rufaldo, but she declares enthusiasm for the idea and asks for a hasty wedding; on the appointed day, however, she disappears and goes to live in the country disguised as a shepherd. Felice’s abandoned suitor Gasparo sets up a school of compliment to teach people refined behaviour. His clients include: Bubulcus, who wants to learn how to woo Hilaria and scorn Antonio; Delia, a chambermaid keen to brush up her etiquette: a judge who wants to learn how to address the jury; Medulla, a social-climbing widow; an old countryman who wants to turn his oafish son into a courtier; a quarrelsome gentleman; Ingeniolo, a justice’s clerk in love; and Jenkin, an amorous Welshman in search of poetry to turn a shepherdess’s head. The search for Selina sends several people off to the country, including her true love Infortunio. Antonio pretends to go too, and appears not to return; in fact he impersonates Selina and ‘marries’ Rufaldo. On the wedding night, the old man is horrified to learn that his new ‘wife’ will not sleep with him, and also has the physical strength to enforce ‘her’ will on him. Anxious not to lose ‘her’ altogether, he asks ‘her’ at least to live in his house and sleep with Hilaria; Antonio readily agrees. Bubulcus boasts to ‘Selina’ and Hilaria that he has killed the missing Antonio in a duel, stupidly forgetting that Selina is Antonio’s sister; ‘she’ fetches officers, and Bubulcus is arrested for murder. The real Selina has fallen in with a community of shepherds. Among them is her missing sister Felice, who uses herbal remedies to cure Infortunio of his love-melancholy for Selina.
