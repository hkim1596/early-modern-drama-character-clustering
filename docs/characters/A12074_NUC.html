Full speech

I cry ye mercy sir, I did not see ye. I pray sir be not angrie, I haue not any way of fended you, nor would— Sir, I come not to offend you, nor— Sir, then knowe, I scorne my eyes should stand as witnesses vnto your Ladies wrongs, and let you goe vnpu nished: slight see a sweet Lady abusde! Whome hast thou serued friend? Oh here shee is; pray God my Band sit well. Faire Lady, may I presume with the Bee to sucke Hony from thy lippes, for I dream'd the last night. That I was transfigured, metamorphosd, or trans form'd into a flea in thy Bed. Me thoght you did did, but first I dream't I flung you. And then me thought, as I was skipping from your knee vnto your thigh, & so forth; you told a Gentleman of it, a friend of yours; who most courtly and softly putting in his hand to catch mee. Spretious shee's gone, sure t'is the acutenes of my ingenuitie, which makes my ieasts so stin ging, as she cannot endure them: I must needs eat some of your new court-water-gruell, to qualifie my my inuention. T'is but for my wit she loues me: I sent her trickes alreadie: for Courtyers must aswell thriuing bee, Haue Noses to smell out, as Eyes to see.
