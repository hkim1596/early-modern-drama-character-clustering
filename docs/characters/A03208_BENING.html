Concerning Wiatt and the Kentish rebels, Their ouer-throw is past: the rebell Dukes that sought By all meanes to proclaime queene Iane cheifly Northumberland For Gilfords sake, he for'st his brother Duke vnto that warre, But each one had his merite. Pardon my Lord I sawe you not till now. Such is your sister, A meere opposite to vs in our opinion, and besides Shees next Successiue, should your maiesty Dye yssules, which heauen defend. The state of our religion would decline. The bargmen were too desperat my Lord, In staying till the water was so lowe, For then you know, being vnderneath the bridge, The barges sterne did strike vppon the ground, And was in danger to haue dround vs all. But that will neuer be, this is my censure, If she be guilty in the least degree, May all her wronges suruiue and light on her: If other wayes that she be cleered, Thus both wayes I wish her downe, Or els her state to rayse. Come madam will you goe. what traytor knaues are gather'd here to make a tumult? If they persist, I charge you soldiers stop their mouthes. My lord my Lord, my charge is great. Harke harke my Lord, what Bells are these? Traytors and knaues ring Bells, When the Queenes enemy passeth through the towne, Goe set he knaues by'th heales, make their pates ring noone, I charge thee Barwick. Exit Barwick. Come come away, this lingring will benight vs. How? no prisoner. Barwicke, is this the chayre of state. Take it downe, and pull of my boots. Well sayd Barwick, pull knaue. Well sayd, now comes. What saucy arrant knaue art thou, how? Villaine thou hast broken my crooper. Knaue, doest flout me? Exeunt. He beats him out. What to do Madam. I finde not that in my Commission. No Iaylor, but your gardian Madam. Madam I dare not, my Commission serues not. Good madam I entreate you loose that name Of Iaylor, twilbe a by-word to me and my posterity. Say I should reach you pen, inke and paper, Who ist dare beare a letter sent from you. Who euer dares, none shall. Baddy of me, what a bold knaue's this? What has she written here? He reads. Much suspected by me, nothing prou'd can be, Finis quoth Elizabeth the prisoner, Pray god it proue so, soft what book's this, Marry a God, whats here an English bible? Sanctum Maria pardon this prophanation of my hart, Water Barwick, water, Ile meddle with't no more. Your letter shalbe forth-comming Lady, I will pervse it ere it scape me now. Exit Beningfeild. Here haue you writ a long excuse it seemes, But no submission to the Queene your sister. I doe write and send, Ile crosse you still; She shall not speake to any man aliue, But ile ore-heare her, no letter nor no token Shall euer haue accesse vnto her hands, But first ile see it; So like a subiect to my soueraigns state, I will pursue her with my deadly hate. how knaue, with the Princesse? she parted euen now. Heer's for thy paynes, thou art an honest fellow: Goe take a Gard and apprehend them straight. Exit Clowne. Bring them before me, O this was well found out, Now will the Queene commend my diligent care, And prayse me for my seruice to her grace. Ha, traytors swarme so neere about my house, Tis time to looke into't: O well said Barwick, Wher's the prisoner. Lord blesse vs, knaue what hast thou there? Why knaue, this is a beast. VVhat art thou knaue? A kinne to mee, knaue ile haue thee whipt. Nay, but doest thou flout me still. He beats him. VVhat, droopes your honor? Exeunt omnes Phil: & Queene. Come come my Lord, this is but for a show, Our Queene I warrant wishes in her hart, Her sister Princesse were without her head. Long liue the Queene, long liue your Maiesty, I haue bid hard to be the first reporter, Of these glad tydings first; and all these here. God preserue your Grace.
