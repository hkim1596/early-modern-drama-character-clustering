In hope your bountie will extend it selfe, In selfe same nature of faire curtesie, Ile shunne all nicenesse; my nam's Florizell, My country Scythia, I am Amazon, Cast on this shore by furie of the sea. Madam, I am oblig'd to kisse your hand, By imposition of a now dead man. Ladie, the circumstance is tedious. Vouchsafe me then your hush't obseruances. Vehement in pursuite of strange nouelties, After long trauaile through the Asian maine, I shipt my hopefull thoughts for Britany; Longing to viewe great natures miracle, The glorie of our sex, whose fame doth strike Remotest eares with adoration. Sayling some two monthes with inconstant winds, We view'd the glistering Venetian forts; To which we made: when loe, some three leagues off, We might descry a horred spectacle: The issue of black fury strow'd the sea, With tattered carcasses of splitted ships, Halfe sinking, burning, floating, topsie turuie. Not farre from these sad ruines of fell rage, We might behold a creature presse the waues; Senselesse he sprauld, all notcht with gaping wounds: To him we made, and (short) we tooke him vp: The first word that he spake was, Mellida; And then he swouned. Why sigh you, faire? His wounds being drest, and life recouered, We gan discourse; when loe, the sea grewe mad, His bowels rumbling with winde passion, Straightswarthy darknesse popt out Phoebus eye, And blurd the iocund face of bright cheekt day; Whilst crudl'd fogges masked euen darknesse brow: Heauen bad's good night, and the rocks gron'd At the intestine vprore of the maine. Now gustie flawes strook vp the very heeles Of our maine mast, whilst the keene lightning shot Through the black bowels of the quaking ayte: Straight chops a waue, and in his sliftred panch Downe fals our ship, and there he breaks his neck: Which in an instant vp was belkt againe. Plot — 184
Mistress Missa is under attack from Protestants: they want to replace the Mass with a simple Lord’s Supper involving none of her magical powers. Knowledge persuades Freemouth that she is guilty of heresy and blasphemy, and he cites her before Justice. Initially Justice tries to have Freemouth himself arrested, but Knowledge informs him of a recent change in the crown’s religious policy: Protestants are no longer to be persecuted. Justice agrees to allow Freemouth to proceed against Missa, and he accuses her in Palemon’s court. She admits everything except claiming to be more powerful than God. Porphyry and Philargury undertake Missa’s defence. Porphyry denies the right of Knowledge and Freemouth, lay persons, to question ecclesiastical matters, and attempts to have the charges thrown out, or at least referred to a spiritual court; but Palemon insists that he is competent to try the case even though he is not a priest. Missa’s defence turns on the claim that the mass was ordained by Christ at the Last Supper; but Knowledge demonstrates that the scriptural passages cited contain no mention of it, and that it is not coextensive with the Lord’s Supper, which requires no vestments, can be administered by anyone rather than only a priest, and claims no power to control the weather, work magic, or save souls. 1548 Porphyry tries to argue on the principle of obedience of authority, but Knowledge retorts that authority cannot command things contrary to scripture (such as the mass). Transubstantiation is invoked and rebutted, and Knowledge argues that all sacrifices are superseded by the atonement at the crucifixion; thus the mass is unnecessary. Palemon finds against Mistress Missa. Though she deserves death, he commutes the penalty to banishment, and she goes home to her father, the Pope.
