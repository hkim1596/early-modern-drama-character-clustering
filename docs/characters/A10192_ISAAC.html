How Sirs, I pray you tary. Will You leaue me so behind you still? And doe ye thinke I would, Goe with you though I could, Before I knew my fathers mind? I will not fayle it (if I may) To die therefore: but will ye stay A while vntill I ronne and know My fathers will? My fellowes: God hath shewed himselfe to vs, So good, so louing and so gracious, That I can neuer any thing yit craue Ne small ne great, but that I much more haue, Than I desire. I would haue gone with you (As you doe know) to see full fayne: but now Behold my father commeth here at hand. Good mother eke adieu. Good mother, if it should not trouble you, I would desire you one thing ere I went. I humbly doe you pray To put this greef away. These teares of yours refreyne, I shall returne ageine (I hope) in better plyght, Than now I am in syght: And therefore stay this greef and wo. Good mother God you guyde. My father. Sir here is woode, with fire, and knyfe redy: But as for sheepe or lambe I see none here. For you to offer. Good father go: but yit I pray you showe Me whereupon this greef of yours doth growe, Which doth (I see) so greatly you appall. It is good reason that I should so doe. And therewithall I will ech thing addresse, That first this wood may be in redinesse. This billet first shall gin the order heere: Then this, then that shall cloze togither neere. Thus all these thinges are redie now and prest. My father shall prouide for all the rest. And now O God I will aside retyre, To pray to thee, as reason doth requyre. What pleaseth you good father. Father, me thinks that feare hath you dismayd. Sir if it may you pleaze, Be bold to tell me what doth you diseaze. Alas my father deere, Uppon my knees I humbly pray you heere, My youthfull yeeres to pitie, if you may. Alas my father, mercie I kry you. Alas alas I want both tung and hand, Ageinst you in mine owne defence to stand. But see, but see my teares for natures sake, None other fence I can or will now make Ageinst you. I am Isaac, none other But Isaac, your only by my mother. I am your sonne that through your self hath life And will you let it be bereft with knife? Howbeit, if you do't to 'hey the Lord, Then on my knees I humbly doe accord, To suffer all that euer God and yow, Shall think expedient for too doo as now. But yit what deeds, what deeds of mine deserue This death O God, my God my life preserue. Alas poore mother mine ▪ How many deathes shall my death giue to thee? But tell me yit, my killer who shall be? O father mine. Sir I am redy. Now then my father, well I see in deede That I must dye. Lord help me at my neede. My God, my God, now strengthen thou my mind And at thy hand such fauor let me find, That of my selfe I may the vpper hand Obteyne, ageinst this sodein death to stand. Now bind me, kill me, burne me, I am prest To suffer all, sith God so thinks it best. Thou Lord hast made me and created me, Thou Lord upon the earth hast lodged me, Thou hast me giuen y e grace to knowledge thee: Yit haue I not so well obeyed thee My Lord and God as dewtie doth require: Which me to pardon lowd I thee desire. And whereas I to you my Lord and father Haue not alwaies such honor yeelded rather, As your great kindnesse did deserue to haue: Therfore forgiuenesse humbly I doe craue. My mother: she is now a great way hence, Wherfore my God vowtsafe hir thy defence, And so preserue hir through thy speciall grace, As she no whit be trubbled at my cace. Alas, I go to deepe and darksom night: Farewell as now for ay all worldly light. But sure I am I shall at Gods hand find Farre better things than these I leaue behind. Good father, I am redy at your will. With right good will and hartie thankes. What doe I heere? Alas my father deere! I am at your will. Am I now well? your pleasure then fulfill. Away with all this feare of yours I pray. Will you from God yit longer time me stay? O God.

Plot summary

Plot — Abraham's Sacrifice
In their old age, Abraham and Sara have been blessed with a son, Isaac. They show concern for his proper upbringing and give thanks for God’s mercy. However, Satan has designs on Abraham’s soul. An angel brings Abraham God’s command to sacrifice Isaac, and, despite Sara’s reluctance, he sets out with his shepherds on the three-day journey to the place of sacrifice. Satan rejoices at a situation in which he cannot lose: either Isaac will die, thus removing a long-term threat, or Abraham will disobey God and be damned. As they approach the appointed place, Abraham leaves the shepherds and goes on alone with Isaac. On arrival, Isaac is puzzled by the absence of any sacrificial offering. Abraham struggles with himself. Can he trust the angel? Since God has told him that Isaac will be father to a far-flung race of people, will the sacrifice negate a prophecy? He becomes resolute, and confronts Isaac with the knife. The child begs for mercy, but submits himself to God’s will and is bound. As Abraham is about to strike, the angel stops him: he has proven his love for God, and is given a sheep as an alternative offering, and the promise of divine favour in times to come.
