Dello, thou knowest Mydas toucht his beard, and twas gold. That the Pages cosend me of it. That I must be reuenged. Thou knowest I haue taught thee the knack ing of the hands, the tickling on a mans haires, like the tuning of a Cittern. Besides, I instructed thee in the phrases of our eloquent occupation, as how sir will you be trimmed? wil you haue your beard like a spade, or a bodkin? a penthouse on your vpper lip, or an allie on your chin? alowe curle on your head like a Bull, or dangling lock like a spaniel? your mustachoes sharp at the endes, like shomakers aules, or hanging down to your mouth like Goates flakes? your loue-locks wreathed with a silken twist, or shaggie to fal on your shoulders? Wel for all this I desire no more at thy hands, than to keep secrete the reuenge I haue prepared for the Pages. Indeed thou shouldst be no blab, because a bar ber, therefore be secrete. Was it not a good cure Dello, to ease the toothach, and neuer touch the tooth? I did but rub his gummes, and presentlie the rewme euaporated. It may be that it is only the breding of a beard, and being the first beard, you shall haue a hard trauel. As likelie sir, for any thing I know, as on your chinne. May I but touch them Dello, Ile teach his tong to tel a tale, what villenie it is to cosen one of a bearde, but stand not thou nigh, for it is ods when he spits, but that all his teeth flie in thy face. I, so did you ouer-heare me, when you cose ned me of a beard: but I forget all. How now Petulus, do they still ake? Let me rub your gummes with this leafe. Nothing in the world, but to let me lay a gol den beard to your chinne. You are like to fetch it out with your teeth, or goe without your teeth. Guilded, nay boy, al my beard was gold. It was not guilt, I wil not be so ouer-matcht. I am content, but I wil haue the beard in my hand to be sure. Agreed. I am as melancholy as a cat. What a world it is to see egges forwarder than cocks? these infants are as cunning in diseases, as I that haue runne them ouer all, backward and forward. I tell you boyes, it is melancholy that now troubleth me. What meanst thou Dello? Because al the Court is sad. Because the King hath a paine in his eares. It may be, for his eares are swolne verie big. Tis a lye; and yet if I had, he might wel spare an inch or two. Let me alone. Asmuch as betweene an asses eares and mine. Nay, I meane the Kings are asses eares. Perij, vnles you pitie me, Motto is in a pit. Your seruants, or handmaides. Out villen, thou wringst too hard. Thou seest boy we are both mortall. I enioye mine eares, but durante placito; nor thou thy finger, but fauente dento. Alas, he that hath drawen so manie teeth, and neuer askt Latin for a tooth, is ill brought vp. I protest by cizars, brush and combe, bason, ball and apron, by razor, eare-pike and rubbing cloa thes, and all the tria sequuntur triaes in our secret occu pation (for you knowe it is no blabbing arte) that you shall haue the beard, in manner and forme following. Not onely the golden beard and euerie haire, (though it be not haire) but a dozen of beards, to stuffe two do zen of cushions.
