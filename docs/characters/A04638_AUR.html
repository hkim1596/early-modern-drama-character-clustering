I Faith brother you are fitted for a generall yonder, Beſhrow my heart (If I had Fortunatus hat here) and I would not wiſh my ſelfe a man and go with you, only t'enioy his pre ſence. No by my troth, but I haue ſuch an odde prety appre henſion of his humour me thinks: that I am eene tickled with the conceite of it. O he is a fine man. O Angelio, do you thinke I do vrge any compariſon a gainſt you? no, I am not ſo ill bred, as to be a deprauer of your worthines: beleeue me, if I had not ſome hope of your abiding with vs, I ſhould neuer deſire to go out of black whilſt I liued: but learne to ſpeake i'the noſe, and turne puritan preſently. Come doe you take it ſo? I faith you wrong me. No more, your drift is ſauour'd, I had rather ſeeme hard hearted Go too ſir. Your wits are freſh I know, they need no ſpur, Say I doe. They will not tire I hope? Your refuge, why ſir. And you be good at flying, be my Plouer. Tut, then you cannot fly. Maſſe then I feare me youle do ſtrange things: I pray you blame me not, if I ſuſpect you, Your owne confeſſion ſimply doth detect you, Nay and you be ſo great in Cupids bookes, T'will make me Iealous. you can with your lookes (I warrant you) enflame a womans heart, And at your pleaſure take loues golden dart, And wound the breſt of any vertous maide. Would I were hence: good Faith I am affraid, You can conſtraine one ere they be aware, To run mad for your loue? Sweet ſignior. Now God a mercy fortune, and ſweet Venus, Let Cupid do his part, and all is well.
