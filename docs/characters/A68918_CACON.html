Naw bay may Préeſt hade God giue ye ten far ene, Yai ſir that ay de, gif yowll giue me troothing, Whe ay? well a neare ay ſwer bay the Sacrament, Ay had rather han a cup af nale then a Teſtament. It is the leaſt thing ay car far bay may charge, Far ſe lang as thea han Images wharon te luke, What nede thea be diſtructed awt af a Buke. Yai, ay my ſen, bay experience thot con ſhowe, Far in may Portace the tongue ay de nat knowe, Yet when ay ſee the great gilded letter, Ay ken it ſea well, as nea man ken better: As far Example, on the day of Chra ſtes Natyuitie, Ay ſée a Bab in a Manger, and two Beaſtes ſtanding by The Seruice whilk to Newyeares day is aſſaygnd, Bay the Paicture of the Circumciſion ay faynd. The Seruice, whilk on Twalfth day mun be don, Ay ſéeke ay the marke of the thrée kynges of Colon. Bay the Deuill tentyng Chraiſt, ay faind whadrageſima, Bay Chraiſt on the Croſſe, ay ſerch out gude frayday: Paſch for his marke, hath the Reſurrection, Ayenſt Hally Thurſday, is pented Chraiſtes Aſſention, Thus in mayn owne buke, ay is a gude Clarke, But gyf the Sents war gone, the Cat had eate my mark Se the ſandry mairacles, whi ilk Sent haue done, Bay the Picttures on the walles ſal appéere to them ſoone Bay the whilk thea ar lerned in euery diſtreſſe, What Sent thea mun prea te far ſuccour doubtles: Sea that all Lepers te Sylueſter muſt prea, That he wawd frée tham, ther diſeaſe take away. Far women in trauayle, call to Sent Magdalene Far lawlynes of minde, call te Sent Katheryne, Sent Loy ſaue your Horſe, Sent Anthony your Swyne. Bay may trooth, in readyng any other, ne taym do I ſpend Far that ay ken, bay gene al cau ̄ ſell, is canonized And bay the hely Pope hymſelfe is authorized: That Buke farther, is wholly permytted, Wharas, the Bayble in part is prohibited. And therfore, gif it be lawfull to vtter my conſcience, Before the new Teſtament ays giue it credence. Yai mara is ther a vara buſy bodye, Whe will ieſt with me and call me fule and noddye, And ſets his Lads te ſpowt latin ayenſt me, But ay ſpoſe then with Deparfundis Clam aui, And oftentimes ▪ he wil reſon with me of the Sacarment, And ſay he can prooue bay the new Taſtament, That Chrayſtss body is in Heauen placed, But ays not beleue him, ay woll not be awt faced, He ſays beſayd that the Pope is Anticraiſt, Fugered of Iohn bay the ſeuen hedded beaſt, And all awre religion is but mons inuention, And with Gods ward is at vtter diſſention, And a plaguy déel mare af ſayk layk talke, That ay dar not far may nars bay his yate walke, But ay wawd he wer brunt that ay mawght be whaiet, Yai ſir that ay ken he is léped Phailelegoos. Plot summary

Plot — The Conflict of Conscience
Satan, concerned about the spread of godliness on Earth, procures Hypocrisy, Tyranny, and Avarice to counteract it by propagating vice through the Roman Catholic church. Philologus and Mathetes discuss the danger to Protestantism, and conclude that worldly torments are sent by God to test men’s faith. After arguing about their mutual precedence, the vices resolve to help the papal legation, and adopt innocuous aliases. Tyranny is commissioned as an inquisitor to hunt heretics, while Avarice accepts their bribes to save them from arrest. Caconos alerts the vices to the heresies of Philologus, who is arrested and tried by the Cardinal in consistory court. He is examined on the topics of papal authority and transubstantiation, and found guilty. His property is confiscated and his family’s distress used to put psychological pressure on him; he is also threatened with imprisonment, torture, and execution, but promised money and power if he recants. He does, persuaded by the glass of vanity given by Sensual Suggestion. Spirit and Conscience call on Philologus to renounce his apostasy and embrace martyrdom, but he rejects them. In prosperity, he is visited by Horror, who torments him with the knowledge of his own damnation. Theologus and Eusebius urge the continuing availability of God’s grace, but Philologus blasphemes and curses God. Despairing of salvation, he hangs himself. (In the B-text, he is given grace to repent at the last moment.)
