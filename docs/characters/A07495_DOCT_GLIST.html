Full speech

Nothing but commendations, farewell: your ſweet hart Gerardine is by this time cold of his hope to enioy thee: he'es gone, and a more equall and able huſband ſhall my care ere long prouide thee: what clyents haue bin here in my abſence wife? Walke you into that roome, I will bethinke my ſelfe for your good, and inſtantly reſolue you; let the gentle man come in: Lipſalue in loue with my veſſell of eaſe? come to me to helpe him to a morſell moſt affected by mine owne pallat? No more but ſo, I haue ſhapt it, the conceit tickles me. Sir, as a ſtranger J welcome you, what maſter Gudgin haue J caught you: J thought it was a gallant that walkt muffled, Come, let me behoulde you at full, heere are no Sergeants man. So lie thou there; my noble gallants ile ſo firke you: Sir my Spirit agrees in Lipſalues ſhape; to morrow twixt the howres of foure and fiue, ſhall miſtriſſe Purge be rapt with a whirlwind into Lypſalues chamber, thats the fitteſt place: for by the breake of day Lipſalue ſhall be mounted, and forſake the City for three dayes, ſo my Spirit reſolues me ▪ Now ſir, by my Art, at that very houre ſhall his chamber doore fly open, into which boldly enter in this ſort accoutered: put me on a pure cleane ſhirt, leaue off your doublet, (for Spirits indure nothing polluted) take me this whip in your hand, and being entred you ſhall ſee the Spirit in Lipſalues ſhape, in the ſelfe ſame forme that you appeare; ſpeake theſe words here ready written, take three bold ſteps forward, then whippe him ſoundly, who ſtraight vaniſheth and leaues miſtris Purge to your will. And no otherwiſe ſir; let me vnderſtand how you thriue: ha, ha, ha, now to my friend Lipſalue, I muſt poſſeſſe him with the ſame circumſtance, wherein J am aſſured to get per petuall laughter in their follies, and my reuenge. VVhat more flatterers about my carrion? more battery to my walls? ſhall I neuer be rid of theſe Petronell Flaſhes? As for my friend Gerardine, the wind of my rage has blown him to diſcouer countries, and let the ſea purge his loue away and him together, I care not: yong wenches now are all o'the hoigh: we that are gardyans muſt reſpect more beſides Titles, gold lace, perſon, or parts, we muſt haue Lordſhips and mannors, elſewhere as well as in the man: wealth commands all, and wealth ile haue, or elſe my mynion ſhall leade Apes in hell. J muſt after this gallant too, ile know his randiuous, and what company he keeps.

Plot — The Family of Love
Glister forbids his ward Maria to see Gerardine, and confines her to his house. He is invited to a supper party at Purge’s, where Gerardine’s will is to be read. On the way, Gerardine and his friends pass beneath Maria’s window, and she warns him how dangerous it would be for him to climb up to see her, thereby dropping a hint. The will is read: Gerardine is going On a voyage overseas, and has made Maria his heir; his property is in a trunk, which is to be given into Glister’s safe-keeping until he returns. Glister takes delivery of the trunk. Once Maria is alone with the trunk, Gerardine emerges from it. Their tryst is interrupted by Lipsalve, who overheard Maria’s indirect instructions and now poses as Gerardine in hope of an assignation; he goes away unsuccessful, and Gerardine has sex with Maria, promising to marry her later. Glister is consulted first by Lipsalve and then by Gudgeon, each of whom wants magical help in cuckolding Purge. Glister agrees to a stratagem: Lipsalve must pretend to be away for a few days, but stay in his lodgings; Glister will conjure a spirit to bring Mistress Purge there. Each would-be lover is told that the spirit will take the form of the other, and that he must attack it with a whip supplied by Glister. Each keeps the appointment, and whips the other before they realize the trick that has been played on them. Mistress Purge is a member of the Family of Love, a radical Christian sect which is also a women’s sex club. Her husband overhears the password for admission to their meetings, but gets it wrong when he first tries to use it and is turned away. Continuing their pursuit of Mistress Purge, Lipsalve and Gudgeon pretend a religious vocation and are admitted to the Family. Mistress Purge gives them spiritual instruction in the dark, but is taken away for sex by another man, to whom she gives her wedding ring. Unbeknown to her, this is her husband, successfully admitted on his second attempt; since she doesn’t know his identity, he has in effect cuckolded himself. Gerardine conspires with Dryfat and Club to pose as a court of morals which will summon and condemn Glister for sexual incontinence. In disguise, he delivers a forged letter to the Glister household which persuades Mistress Glister that her husband has a bastard in the country and is also the father of the pregnant Maria’s child.
