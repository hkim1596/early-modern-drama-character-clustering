My Fathers dead: I am a man of warre too, Monyes, demeanes; I have ships at sea too, Captaines. I scorne the Hollanders, they are my drunkards. I am satisfied, you shall not, Come out, I know thee, meet mine anger instantly. Thou hast wrong'd mine honor, Thou look'st upon my Mistris thrice lasciviously, Ile make it good. Thou wan'st my mony too, with a pair of base bones, In whom there was no truth, for which I beat thee, I beat thee much, now I will hurt thee dangerously. This shall provoke thee. He strikes. Hadst thou not ask'd my pardon, I had killd thee, I leave thee as a thing despis'd, assoles manus a vostra siniare a Maistre. Exit Caca. I have reason, I have mony. Yes and ruine too Captaine, If ye have no mony y'ar an asse. I shall be master of it, Twas built for my bulk, the roomes are wide and spacious, Ayerie, and full of ease, and that I love well, Ile tell you when I tast the wine my Lord, And take the heighth of hir table with my stomack, How my affections stand to the young Lady. Ile have a fling, tis but a thousand Duckets, Which I can cozen up agen in ten daies, And some few jewels to justifie my knavery, Say J should marry her, sheele get more mony Then all my usurie, put my knavery to it, Shee appeares the most unfallible way of purchase, I cood wish her a size or two stronger, for the incounter, For I am like a Lyon where I lay hold, But these Lambs will endure a plaguie load, And never bleat neither, that sir, time has taught us. I am so vertuous now, I cannot speak to her, The arrant'st shame fac'd a se, I broile away too. If thou beest her husband, I am determin'd thou shall be my Cuckold, Ile be thy faithfull friend. Preethee be answered, thou shalt crave no leave, I am in my meditations, doe not vex me, A beaten thing, but this houre a most brused thing, That people had compassion on, it looked so. The next Sir Palmerin, heres fine proportion, An Asse, and then an Elephant, sweet justice, Theres no way left to come at her now, no craving, If mony could come neere, yet I would pay him; I have a mind to make him a huge cuckold, And mony may doe much, a thousand duckets, Tis but the letting blood of a ranck heire. Plot summary

Plot — Rule a Wife and Have a Wife
Perez, gathering his troops in preparation for an expedition to Flanders, is visited by the veiled Estefania. She piques his interest by refusing to show her face; on their second meeting, he woos her, hastily marries her, abandons his military career, and moves into what she claims to be her house, bringing with him what he claims to be a trunk of jewels. In fact the house belongs to Margarita; Estefania is no lady but a former servant. Margarita intends to marry, but when her older confidantes remind her of the joys of promiscuity, she decides on a husband who will be content to be cuckolded. Her waiting-woman Altea recommends Leon, who has just evaded military recruitment by feigning folly, and who agrees to be his wife’s subordinate in the marriage, no better than her household servants. The newlyweds arrive unexpectedly at Margarita’s city house, surprising Estefania and her husband. Estefania claims that she has lent the house to Margarita (whom she claims as a kinswoman), to facilitate the cozening of a lover. Perez moves temporarily into lodgings, whereupon Estefania steals the contents of his trunk. She later discovers the jewels to be counterfeit, and gets her own back by encouraging Perez to take possession of ‘his’ house; when he attempts to do so, he is rebuffed by Leon. She presents the jewels to the rich tradesman Cacafuego, persuading him that they are a love-gift from Margarita. In return, he gives her money which she hands over to Perez: evidently she has something to bring to the marriage after all. Now married to Margarita, Leon proves less subservient than she bargained for. She entertains the Duke of Medina to dinner, but Leon refuses to serve any food until he is introduced to the guests and treated as her husband, the host. The Duke tries to get rid of him by sending him a commission to lead a troop of cavalry in Flanders; but he intends to take Margarita along with him, and has all her property packed up. When Margarita cannot dissuade him by entreaty, sickness, or feigned pregnancy, the Duke admits that the commission was a fake. Leon's response is to plan a trip to visit Margarita’s commercial interests in the Indies. To delay her departure and extend her opportunities for adultery, the Duke arranges to be ‘injured’ in a fight outside the house so that he can be lodged there during his convalescence.
