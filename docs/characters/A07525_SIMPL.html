Hah, hah! Ile goe see how the World lookes since I stept aside from't, there's such heauing and shouing about it, such toyling and moyling; now I stumbled vpon't when I least thought on't. 'Vds me, 'tis alterd of one side since I left it: hah, there's a Milke-maid got with childe since, me thinks; what, and a Shepheard forsworne himselfe? heere's a foule corner: by this light, Subtilty has laid an egge too, and will go nye to hatch a Lawyer; this was well foreseen: Ile marre the fashion on't; so, the egg's broke, and 'tas a yolke ▪ as black as buckrom: Whats heere a this side? Oh, a dainty world; heere's one a sealing with his tooth, and poore man hee has but one in all, I was affraid hee would haue left it vpon the paper, hee was so honestly earnest; heere are the Reapers singing: Ile lay mine eare to 'em: Whop! Heere's the coozningst Rascall in a Kingdome, The Master villaine, h'as the thunders property, For if he come but neere the Haruest folkes, His breath's so strong, that he sowres all their bottles, If hee should but blow vpon the world now, the staine would ne uer get out agen: I warrant, if hee were ript, one might finde a swarme of Vsurers in his liuer, a cluster of Scriueners in his kidneys, and his very puddings stuft with Bayliffes. He makes more neere me. Keepe off, and leaue your cogging; foh, how abomina bly he smells of controuersies, Schismes and Factions! me thinkes, I smell forty Religions together in him, and ne'r a good one;—his eyes looke like false lights, coozening trap-windowes. Poxe a your pipe; if I should dance after your pipe, I should soone dance to the Diuell. So, so, l'me glad hees vanisht: me thought I had much adoe to keep my selfe from a smatch of knauery, as long as he stood by me; for certainly villany is infectious, and in the greater person the greater poyson: as for example, hee that takes but the tich of a Citizen, may take the scab of a Courtier. Harke, the Reapers be gin to sing, they'r come neerer me thinks too. HAppy times wee liue to see, Whose Master is Simplicity: This is the age where blessings flow, In ioy we reape, in peace wee sow; Wee doe good deeds without delay, Wee promise and wee keepe our day, Wee loue for vertue, not for wealth, Wee drinke no healths, but all for health, Wee sing, wee dance, wee pipe, wee play, Our work's continuall Holiday, Wee liue in poore contented sort, Yet neither beg, nor come at Court, These Reapers haue the merriest liues, they haue musicke to all they doe; they'l sow with a Tabor, and get Children with a Pipe. Heer's a braue glistring Thing looks me ith' face, I know not what to say too't. I'me glad I'me rid on't. Sure this will proue a good Prince. Pray remember now, You had the world from me cleane as a pick, Only a little smutted a one side, With a bastard got against it, or such a toy, No great corruption nor oppression in't, No knauery, tricks, nor coozenage. Make as few Lawes as you can then to trouble it, the few er the better; for alwaies the more lawes you make, the more knaues thriue by't, marke it when you will. A little against knauery, I'me such an enemy too't, That it comes naturally from me to confound it. Tents, Tents; that part oth' world Shewes like a Faire: but pray take notice on't, There's not a Bawdy-Boothe amongst 'em all, You haue 'em white and honest as I had 'em, Looke that your Laundresses pollute 'em not. Lookes like a point, you meane a very prick? 'Tis the beginning of Amsterdam: they say the first brick there was laid with fresh Cheese and Creame; because morter made of Lime and hayre was wicked, and committed fornication. Blustering fellowes; the first's a Soldier, hee looks iust like March. Troth, now the World begins to bee in Hucksters handling; by this light, the Boothes are full of Cutlers, and yonders two or three queanes going to vittaile the campe: hah, would I were whipt, if yonder be not a Parsons daughter with a soldier be tweene her legs, bag and baggage. Plot — The World Tossed at Tennis
The royal palaces of Richmond and St James now have a rival, Denmark House. Richmond is afraid that the newcomer will take all Prince Charles’s attention, leaving them neglected; but Denmark House assures them that each is suitable for a particular season (herself for winter, St James’s for spring, and Richmond for summer), and that the Prince will rotate among them equally. An unemployed scholar and an unemployed soldier consider their options. Their patroness Pallas descends, argues that the complete man combines both their professions, art and arms together, and invokes Jupiter's help. Jupiter presents the Muses and the Nine 1620 Worthies as an illustration of the virtue of former times. Time weeps at the encroaching vice of the present; he finds himself continually abused and hated. Deceit and pride have damaged the world in its latter days, and Jupiter summons the five coloured starches as illustrations of the absurdity of modern fashion. White Starch claims precedence over the others, but Yellow wins acclaim. Simplicity arrives to see what has become of the world (represented on stage by a globe), and is not reassured by his observations. Deceit wants to cozen him of the world, Simplicity to protect it from Deceit’s bad influence. Deceit successively gains the confidence of a series of men with worldly power: a king, a land- captain, a sea-captain, a flamen, and a lawyer. Each in turn is persuaded that the world should belong to him: the King takes it from Simplicity, and the Land- Captain from the King (through a display of superior military power); the Sea-Captain threatens the Land- Captain with naval cannon and they agree to share the world; but the Flamen takes it from them, urging them to look to their souls, and the Lawyer in turn takes it from the Flamen, insisting on his role in maintaining proper social discipline. On each occasion, Deceit asks for his share of the world, and is rebuffed at every turn (though the Lawyer gives him all the money in the world): none of the worldly powers is susceptible to corruption. A dance ensues in which the various powers pass the world between themselves; Deceit and the Devil repeatedly attempt to possess it, and fail. The world ends up in the hands of the King. With the world now at ease, Jupiter tells the Soldier and Scholar that they have only themselves to blame if they are unemployed, and encourages them to go and get jobs.
