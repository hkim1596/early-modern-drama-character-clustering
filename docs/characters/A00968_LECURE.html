I feare not gelding As long as she holds this way. But what safety Madam Finde you in staying here? This place you will. For me I am your owne, nor since I first Knew what it was to serue you, haue remembred I had a soule, but such a one whose essence Depended wholy on your Highnesse pleasure, And therefore Madam--- She meanes on her. Amazement leaue me, 'tis hee. The beadle will resolue you, for I cannot, There is something that more neere concernes myselfe, That calls vpon me. Which if You had preuented, or would teach vs how They might againe be seuerde, we could easily Remoue all other hindrances, that stop The passage of your pleasures. If so, from whence Growes your affliction? Giue me leaue, If my art faile me not, I haue thought on A speeding proiect. The Lady is faire. And young. And loues the King with equall ordor. VVell then, what thinke you if I make a drinke VVhich giuen vnto him on the bridall night, Shall for fiue dayes so rob his faculties Of all ability to pay that duty, VVhich new made wiues expect, that she shall sweare She is not matchde to a man. She that loues mischiefe 'boue the man that does it, And him aboue her pleasure, yet knowes no heauen else: The same to you, you bring Sir. A resolution falling with most ease Of any doubt you could haue named, he is a Prince Whose fortune you enquire. He had a dukedome lately falne vnto him By one call'd brother, who has left a daughter. Heires, yes he shall haue heires. Nor thou, I neither can nor will Giue farther knowledge to thee. Your grace doth wrong your stars; if this be yours, You may haue children. You may haue fruitefull issue. It was the fatall meanes first strooke my bloud With the cold hand of wonder, when I read it, Printed vpon your birth. We that behold the sad aspects of heauen, Leading sence blinded, men feele griese enough To know, though not to speake their miseries. Then thus, The first of femalls which your eye shall meete Before the sun next rise, comming from out The Temple of Diana, being slaine, you liue Father of many sonnes. If in the supposition Of her death in whose life you die, you aske me, I thinke you are safe. I haue vsde All meanes to make her so, I saw him waiting At the Temple doore, and vsde such art within, That only she of all her sexe, was first Giuen vp vnto his fury. If you are so resolude, I haue prouided A meanes to ship him hence: looke vpon this, But touch it sparingly, for this once vsde, Say but to drie a teare, will keepe the eyelidde From closing, vntill death performe that office. Brunhild fears that Thierry's bride, Ordella, will eclipse her in preeminence, so at the wedding banquet she gives Thierry a potion that makes him impotent that night. Ordella is very understanding , but Thierry is concerned about the need to beget an heir, so Brunhild suggests he should consult a local hermit and astrologer. Her parasite, Lecure, impersonates the wise man, and tells Thierry that he will regain his potency ifhe kills the first female person he encounters the next day, whoever she may be. Lecure arranges for this to be Ordella herself. Not recognizing her in her veil, Thierry explains why she must die, and she agrees; but he cannot bring himself to go through with it when he realizes who she is. Martel dissuades Ordella from loyally committing suicide, and, suspecting a plot, has her pretend to be dead in order to smoke it out Meanwhile Brunhild spreads a rumour that Theodoret is illegitimate and has him secretly murdered by Protaldi at the continuing wedding revels. Believing that Theodoret was not his brother and that Ordella is dead, Thierry offers to make amends by marrying Memberge, Theodoret's daughter. Brunhild is forced to admit her lies in order to prevent an incestuous marriage. She gives Thierry a poisoned handkerchief, which kills him; Martel brings Ordella to his deathbed. Brunhild admits complicity in his murder, and is sentenced to remain awake for the rest of her life; disturbed by the sight of Protaldi being tortured to death, she commits suicide. Thierry and Ordella die together , and Martel becomes King of France.
