Full speech

My good Lord Lyon, most happily met wor thy Traefilme, Come gallants, whats the newcs, the season affoords vs variety, the nouilsts of our time runnes on heapes, to glut their itching eares with airie sounds, trotting to'th burse; and in the Temple walke with greater zeale to heare a nouall lye, then a pyous Anthum tho chanted by Cherubins. Thats as their intelligence serues. But like to be, and shall haue in dowry with the Princesse this Kingdome of Cycele. I could wish their experience answered their loues, Then should the much too much wrongd Phylaster, Possesse his right in spight of Don and the diuell. It must be ill done, if it be done. Who dos not? This speech calls him Spaniard, being nothing but a large Inuentorie of his owne commendations. Peace, we are all one soule. He dares not stand the shocke. But doe you way the danger you are in? Get him another wench, and you bring him to bed indeed. Heere's a male spirit fit for Hercules. if euer there be nine worthy of women, this wench shall ride aside and be their Captaine. And doubtlesse tis true. The gentry do awaite it, and the people against their nature, are all for him, and like a field if standing corne, moued with a stiffe gale: their heads bow al one way, Why Gentlemen, tis without question so. It will be best. Here he comes. Good morrow to your honor: We haue spent some time in seeking you. Sir, remember this is your honor'd friend, that comes to doe his seruice, and will shew you why he vttered this. And she was taken with her boy. How, you my Lord? Haue you knowne him so ill temper'd? He needes no teaching, he strikes sure enough, His greatest fault is, he hunts too much in the purlewes, Would he would leaue off poaching. Faith no great matter to speake of: a foolish twinckling with the eie, that spoyles her coat, but hee must be a cunning Herald that findes it. My Lord Leon — Nor will be, I thinke. There's already a thousand fatherlesle tales amongst vs, Some say, her horse run away with her; some, a wolfe pursu'd her; Others, twas a plot to kill her, and that armed men were seene In the wood: but questionlesse, she rode away willingly. No more smell sweete it selfe, if once the lungs bee but corrupted. Lady, you must go search too. That you shall, and receiue thankes. Stay sir, what are you? Reuenge, for what? Helpe to leade him hence. Feare it not, their ouer-wise heads Will thinke it but a trick. Yes, but the king must know, 'tis not in his powor to warre with heauen. We dally Gentlemen. She waites your grace. The Citie vp, this was aboue our wishes. No, no, 'twill but lose time. Yet you'ld not beleeue this. O very well Sir. There would be little gotten by it, ene keepe you as ye are.
