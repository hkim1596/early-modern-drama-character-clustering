So is Lisander. I would my father lookt but with my eyes. I doe intreat your grace, to pardon mee. I know not by what power, I am made bould; Nor how it may concerne my modesty, In such a presence, here to plead my thoughts: But I beseech your Grace, that I may knowe The worst that may befall mee in this case, If I refuse to wed Demetrius. So will I growe, so liue, so die my Lord, Ere I will yield my virgin Patent, vp Vnto his Lordshippe, whose vnwished yoake My soule consents not to giue souerainty. Belike, for want of raine: which I could well Beteeme them, from the tempest of my eyes. O crosse! too high to be inthrald to loue. O spight! too olde to be ingag'd to young. O hell, to choose loue by anothers eyes! If then true louers haue bin euer crost. It stands as an edict, in destiny: Then let vs teach our triall patience: Because it is a customary crosse, As dewe to loue, as thoughts, and dreames, and sighes, Wishes, and teares; poore Fancies followers. My good Lysander, I sweare to thee, by Cupids strongest bowe, By his best arrowe, with the golden heade, By the simplicitie of Venus doues, By that which knitteth soules, and prospers loues, And by that fire, which burnd the Carthage queene, When the false Troian vnder saile was seene, By all the vowes that euer men haue broke, (In number more then euer women spoke) In that same place thou hast appointed mee, To morrow truely will I meete with thee. God speede faire Helena : wither away? I frowne vpon him; yet hee loues mee still. I giue him curses; yet he giues mee loue. The more I hate, the more he followes mee. His folly, Helena, is no fault of mine. Take comfort; he no more shall see my face: Lysander and my selfe will sly this place. Before the time I did Lisander see, Seem'd Athens as a Paradise to mee. O then, what graces in my loue dooe dwell, That hee hath turnd a heauen vnto a hell! And in the wood, where often you and I, Vpon faint Primrose beddes, were wont to lye, Emptying our bosomes, of their counsell sweld, There my Lysander, and my selfe shall meete, And thence, from Athens, turne away our eyes, To seeke new friends and strange companions. During the rehearsal, Robin metamorphoses Bottom, putting an ass’s head on him, and scares off the other artisans. Titania wakes, sees him, and falls in love. Helena arrives, pursued by Lysander, and wakes Demetrius to find that both young men now love her; she infers that she is the object of a practical joke.
