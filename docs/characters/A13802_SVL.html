FLauia I kisse your hands. I plainely will professe my loue of Lelio, 'tis honest, chast, and staines not modesty. Shall I be married to Antonio, that hath beene a soust Sea-fish, this three months? and if hee bee aliue comes home with as many impaires, as a Hunting Gelding fal'n Pack-horse. No, no, I'le see him freeze to Christall first. In other things, good father, I am your most obedient daughter, but in this a pure wo man. 'Tis your part to offer, mine to refuse if I like not. Lelio's a hansome Gentleman, yong, fresh, rich, and well-fashioned, and him will Sulpitia haue, or dye a maide: And ifaith the temper of my bloud tel's me I was neuer borne to so cold a misfortune. Fye Flauia, fye wench, no more with teares and sighs, cheere vp, Eugenio to my knowledge loues you, and you shall haue him: I say you shall haue him. And giue't to whom? has none but him and mee: What though he dote a while vpon your beauty; hee will not proue vnna turall to his sonne. Goe to your chamber: my Genius whispers in my eare, and sweares this night we shall enioy our loues, and with that hope fare-well. Lelio? Soft, soft, y'are much mistaken: for in earnest, I am angry Lelio ; and with you. Stand still I charge you By th'vertue of my lips; speake not a sillable As you expect a kisse should close my choler. For I must chide you. First, sir, I heare, you teach Eugenio Too graue a warinesse in your sisters loue, And kill his honest forwardnesse of affection With your farre-fet respects, suspitions, feares. You haue your may-bee's; this is dangerous: That course were better: for if so: and yet: Who know's? th'euent is doubtfull: bee aduis'd: 'Tis a yong rashnesse: your father is your father: Take leasure to consider. Thus y'haue considered Poore Flauia almost to her graue. Fy Lelio, Had this my smalnesse vndertooke the businesse, And done no more in foure short winter daies Then you in foure monthes, I'de haue vow'd my maydenhead To th'liuing Tomb of a sad Nunnery. Which for your sake I loath. Peace! peace! Now y'are so wise as if y'had eaten Nothing but braines, and marrow of Machiauell. You tip your speeches with Italian Motti, Spanish Refranes, and English Quoth Hee's. Beleeue mee There's not a Prouerbe salt's your tongue, but plantes Whole colonies of white haires. O what a businesse These hands must haue when you haue married mee, To pick out sentences that ouer-yeare you. Haue I a lip? and you Made Sonnets on't? 'tis your fault, for otherwise, Your sister and Eugenio had beene sure Long time e're this. Stay, your Qu's not come yet. I hate as perfectly this gray-greene of yours, As old Antonio's greene-gray. Fy! Wise louers, Are most absurd. Were not I full resolu'd, I should beginne to coole mine owne affection. For shame consider well your sister's temper. Her melancholy may much hurt her. Respect her, Or spight of mine owne loue, I'le make you stay Six monthes, before you marry mee. Lelio whispers. This your so happy newes? return'd? and safe? Antonio yet aliue? Lelio whispers. And then? Well. All your businesse must bee compassed With winding plots, and cunning stratagems. Look to't: For if we be not married e're next morning, By the great loue that's hid in this small compasse, Flauia and my selfe will steale you both away To your eternall shame and foule discredit. What e're my father, brother, and your selfe Shall thinke conuenient pleaseth me. Content And me the same.

Plot — Albumazar
The neighbours Pandolfo and Antonio, both widowers, have arranged to marry each other’s daughters; but before they can do so, Antonio is lost and presumed dead on an overseas voyage, and the plan goes into abeyance because his son Lelio disapproves of Pandolfo as a potential brother-in-law. Albumazar’s accomplice Furbo has gained access to Pandolfo’s household, posing as his daughter’s tutor, and has learned of his .- amorous aspirations, and also his credulity about. magic and astrology. Albumazar plans to trick him, and sets about demonstrating his occult power. Ronca shows Pandolfo some of the astrologer’s amazing inventions: a telescope and an ‘otacousticon’ (actually a pair of ass’s ears) which allow him to see and hear things at a distance. Albumazar then predicts Pandolfo’s marriage, and also a stabbing which his associates then enact in a brawl. Pandolfo asks the astrologer to raise Antonio’s ghost, but instead Albumazar undertakes to transform the tenant farmer Trincalo into Antonio’s likeness for one day, so that he can give Flavia in marriage to Pandolfo. Trincalo agrees because this will give him access to Antonio’s household, and therefore to the maid Armellina, whom he loves. Pandolfo is required to prepare a room for the metamorphosis, laid out with valuable possessions. When he begins to have second thoughts about paying Albumazar, his servant Cricca suggests that he should pretend to have been robbed of his valuables; but when they are indeed stolen, Albumazar pretends in turn that the transformed Trincalo has just locked them away for safe keeping. After undergoing the fake metamorphosis, Trincalo is told he may not look at himself in a mirror for fear of breaking the spell.
