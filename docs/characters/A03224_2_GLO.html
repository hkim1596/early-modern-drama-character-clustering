Amen good brother Clarence: And send them all to heauen shortly I beseech him. I promise you hee is, and verie much, But Doctor Shaw, who prophested that G. should be so sadly ominious to vs? A great learned man he was, and as I haue heard, Hath prophested of very many things, I promise you it troubles me, I hope in me his propheste is true. I am sure it does, for looke you brother Clarence, We know not how his Highnes will applie it, We are but two, your selfe my Lord and I, Should the young Princes faile, which God defend. God blesse you al, and take you to him if it be his will. Now brother, this prophesse of G. troubling the King, He may as well applie it vnto Glocester, My Dukedomes name, if he be fealfous, As vnto George your … ame, good brother Clarence, God helpe, God helpe: … faith it troubles me, You would not thinke how: aside. that any of you liue. Who you, I, I durst answer for you, That I shall cut you off ere it be long. But reuerend Doctor, you can onely tell, Being his Highnes Confessor, how he takes it. Shaw, you know my minde, a villaine like my selfe. Ah brother Clarence, when you are suspected, Well, well, it is a wicked world the while: But shall I tell you brother in plaine tearmes, I feare, your selfe and I haue enemies, About the King, God pardon them, The world was neuer worser to be trusted: Ah brother George, where is that loue that was? Ah it is banisht brother from the world: Ah Conscience, Conscience, and true brotherhood, Tis gone, tis gone, brother I am your friend, I am your louing brother, your owne selfe, And loue you as my soule, vse me in what you please, And you shall see Ile doe a brothers part, Send you to heauen I hope, ere it be long, I am a true stampt villaine as euer liude. Do so good brother. My teares will searcely let me take my leaue, I loue you so: Farewell sweet George.
