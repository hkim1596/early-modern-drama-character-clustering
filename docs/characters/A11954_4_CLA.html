Full speech

Fellow, why do'ſt thou ſhow me thus to th' world? Beare me to priſon, where I am committed. From too much liberty, (my Lucio ) Liberty As ſurfet is the father of much faſt, So euery Scope by the immoderate vſe Turnes to reſtraint: Our Natures doe purſue Like Rats that rauyn downe their proper Bane, A thirſty euill, and when we drinke, we die. What (but to ſpeake of) would offend againe. No. Call it ſo. One word, good friend: Lucio, a word with you. Thus ſtands it with me: vpon a true contract I got poſſeſſion of Iulietas bed, You know the Lady, ſhe is faſt my wife, Saue that we doe the denunciation lacke Of outward Order. This we came not to, Onely for propogation of a Dowre Remaining in the Coffer of her friends, From whom we thought it meet to hide our Loue Till Time had made them for vs. But it chances The ſtealth of our moſt mutuall entertainment With Character too groſſe, is writ on Iuliet. Vnhappely, euen ſo. And the new Deputie, now for the Duke, Whether it be the fault and glimpſe of newnes, Or whether that the body publique, be A horſe whereon the Gouernor doth ride, Who newly in the Seate, that it may know He can command; lets it ſtrait feele the ſpur: Whether the Tirranny be in his place, Or in his Eminence that fills it vp I ſtagger in: But this new Gouernor Awakes me all the inrolled penalties Which haue (like vn-ſcowr'd Armor) hung by th' wall So long, that ninteene Zodiacks haue gone round, And none of them beene worne; and for a name Now puts the drowſie and neglected Act Freſhly on me: 'tis ſurely for a name. I haue done ſo, but hee's not to be found. I pre'thee (Lucio) doe me this kinde ſeruice: This day, my ſiſter ſhould the Cloyſter enter, And there receiue her approbation. Acquaint her with the danger of my ſtate, Implore her, in my voice, that ſhe make friends To the ſtrict deputie: bid her ſelfe aſſay him, I haue great hope in that: for in her youth There is a prone and ſpeechleſſe dialect, Such as moue men: beſide, ſhe hath proſperous Art When ſhe will play with reaſon, and diſcourſe, And well ſhe can perſwade. I thanke you good friend Lucio. Come Officer, away. The miſerable haue no other medicine But onely hope: I' haue hope to liue, and am prepar'd to die. I humblie thanke you. To ſue to liue, I finde I ſeeke to die, And ſeeking death, finde life: Let it come on. Moſt bolie Sir; I thanke you. Now ſiſter, what's the comfort? Perpetuall durance? Why giue you me this ſhame? Thinke you I can a reſolution fetch From flowrie tenderneſſe? If I muſt die, I will encounter darkneſſe as a bride, And hugge it in mine armes. The prenzie, Angelo ? Oh heauens, it cannot be. If it were damnable, he being ſo wiſe, Why would he for the momentarie tricke Be perdurablie fin'de? Oh Iſabell. Death is a fearefull thing. I, but to die, and go we know not where, To lie in cold obſtruction, and to rot, This ſenſible warme motion, to become A kneaded clod; And the delighted ſpirit To bath in fierie floods, or to recide In thrilling Region of thicke-ribbed Ice, To be impriſon'd in the viewleſſe windes And blowne with reſtleſſe violence round about The pendant world: or to be worſe then worſt Of thoſe, that lawleſſe and incertaine thought, Imagine howling, 'tis too horrible. The wearieſt, and moſt loathed worldly life That Age, Ache, periury, and impriſonment Can lay on nature, is a Paradiſe To what we feare of death. Sweet Siſter, let me liue. What ſinne you do, to ſaue a brothers life, Nature diſpenſes with the deede ſo farre, That it becomes a vertue. Nay heare me Iſabell. Oh heare me Iſabella. Let me ask my ſiſter pardon, I am ſo out of loue with life, that I will ſue to be rid of it. As faſt lock'd vp in ſleepe, as guiltleſſe labour, When it lies ſtarkely in the Trauellers bones, He will not wake.

Plot summary

Plot — Measure for Measure
Duke Vincentio appoints Angelo as his deputy during his absence from Vienna, but arranges to remain in the city disguised as a friar. He explains to Friar Thomas, who helps with the disguise, that his objective is the restoration of the strict penal laws against sexual immorality which he has allowed to slip; Angelo, famous for his rectitude, will be able to effect this without risk of personal censure. But the Duke is also interested in how Angelo himself will respond to the challenges and temptations of power. Angelo does as expected, enforcing the laws which make begetting a child out of wedlock a capital crime. Claudio is arrested: he has made his fiancée Julietta pregnant, having been unable to have their marriage publicly solemnized because her family disapproves and would withhold her dowry. Claudio asks his friend Lucio to get his sister Isabella, who is about to enter a nunnery, to intercede with Angelo on his behalf. Isabella goes to see Angelo, who initially rejects her pleas, but then defers a decision and asks to see her again. At the second meeting, he offers her a bargain: Claudio’s life will be spared in exchange for sex with her. When he learns of the proposal, Claudio is torn between honour and fear, but finally urges Isabella to accept; she is chastely unwilling to do so. The Duke overhears their conversation, enjoins Claudio to prepare himself for death, and offers Isabella an option. Angelo was engaged to marry Mariana, but jilted her after her dowry was lost at sea. As the Duke suggests, Isabella consents to sex with Angelo, but briefly and in darkness; Mariana then takes her place for the act itself, preserving Isabella’s chastity. Angelo, however, sends a message to the prison reinforcing the execution order and demanding that Claudio’s head be sent by way of proof. There is another prisoner under sentence of death, Barnardine, so the Duke proposes beheading him in Claudio’s place; but Barnardine is drunk and would therefore be damned if executed now. Luckily a third prisoner has just died of a fever, and his head is sent to Angelo as Claudio’s; Claudio himself is hidden in the prison pending the Duke’s return, and Isabella told that he has been executed. The Duke sends Angelo orders concerning his return: Angelo is to meet him at the city gates to give up his commission, and any grievances against him will be dealt with there.
