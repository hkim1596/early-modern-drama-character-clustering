Good signior Leonato, are you come to meet your trouble: the fashion of the world is, to auoyd cost, and you in counter it. You embrace your charge too willingly: I thincke this is your daughter. You haue it full Benedicke, wee may ghesse by this, what you are, being a man, truely the Lady fathers her selfe: be happy Lady, for you are like an honourable father. That is the summe of all: Leonato, signior Claudio, and signior Benedicke, my deere friend Leonato, hath inuited you all, I tell him we shall stay here, at the least a moneth and he heartily praies some occasion may detaine vs longer, I dare sweare he is no hypocrite, but praies from his heart. Your hand Leonato, we wil go together. What secret hath held you here, that you followed not to Leonatoes? I charge thee on thy allegeance. Amen, if you loue her, for the Lady is very well worthy. By my troth I speake my thought. That she is worthy, I know. Thou wast euer an obstinate heretique in the de spight of Beauty. I shall see thee ere I die, looke pale with loue. Well, if euer thou dost fall from this faith, thou wilt prooue a notable argument. Well, as time shal trie: in time the sauage bull doth beare the yoake. Nay, if Cupid haue not spent all his quiuer in Ve nice, thou wilt quake for this shortly. Well, you will temporize with the howres, in the meane time, good signior Benedicke, repaire to Leonatoes, commend me to him, and tell him I will not faile him at sup per, for indeede he hath made great preparation. The sixt of Iuly: your louing friend Benedicke. My loue is thine to teach, teach it but how, And thou shalt see how apt it is to learne Any hard lesson that may do thee good. Plot summary

Plot — Much Ado About Nothing
Returning to Messina after a war, Claudio falls in love with Hero. At a masked revel, Don Pedro woos her for him by proxy; Don John maliciously tells Claudio that Pedro has wooed her for himself, but the truth soon purges Claudio’s jealousy and he and Hero are engaged to be married in a week’s time. In the interim, Don Pedro undertakes to make Beatrice and Benedick, who have a humour of mutual hatred, fall in love with each other. This is done by letting each of them overhear a conversation about how much in love the other is. Don John attempts to prevent Claudio’s marriage by showing him what he takes to be a nocturnal assignation between Hero and another man; in fact it is Margaret in Hero’s clothes, wooed by Borachio whom Don John has suborned. Claudio rejects Hero at the wedding, and the Friar proposes that she feign death to inculcate remorse in him. Borachio and his associate Conrad are arrested and examined, and confess the plot. As penance, Claudio undertakes to marry another ===== PAGE 61 ===== 1598 lady, said to be Leonato’s niece, without first seeing her; she is actually Hero. Benedick and Beatrice join Hero and Claudio to make it a double wedding. Don John flees Messina, but is apprehended and brought back to face punishment.
