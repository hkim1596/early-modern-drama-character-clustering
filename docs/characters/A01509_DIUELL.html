¶How long haue I with toyling payne, sought Susans blood to get, What engines, snares, and other craftes, about that haue I set, And yet that God still doth me wrong, he doth my force withstande, And them that I doe seeke to get, he keepes them in his hande. He suffers some to leane to me, as they would come alone, And when I thinke with them to méete, by him my pray is gone, Oh God wilt thou not yet thy spight leaue of, wilt thou yet still, Seeme for to offer as thou doest, and yet denye my will. Well Ill Reporte thou villayne boy, thy bones I meane to gnaw, Because of that I gaue thée charge, I am no whit in aw. Why stand I heare and suffer him, all this whyle to take rest, His soule, his bones, his flesh and all, by me shall be possest. And what there is in Hell to harme, or punish him withall, Or what I may deuyse anew, his flesh shall séele it all. Oh Boy, oh Knaue, oh foolish Sot, shouldst thou be put in trust, And haste not wit to bring to passe, that thing I after lust. Well, well thou villayne Boy and wretch, I ioy thy selfe art come, And what I would haue done to her, thou shalt haue all and some. And semblably my Lord my God, amidst my heauy cheare, I hopte at length, as now it doth, my quiet would appeare, I haue to thanke thy maiesty for euery kinde of gifte, But chéefely for myne honest wyfe, my harte to thée I lifte. How didst thou Lorde agaynst her foes her simplenesse defende, How didst thou cleane deliuer her, euen at her latter ende. I thanke thée Lord yet once agayne, thou haste deserued so, That from thy prayse my hart nor lippes shall hence forth neuer go. ¶Nay then am I good Lord moste bound, to yéelde thée harty prayse. That haste by helping this my chyld, prolonged these my dayes. Sorry I was to heare her fault, more sorry for her death, Her fault was none, and she liues still, what ioy lyke this on earth, To serue thée still as I haue done, it is my whole pretence, Thou art my strength, my hope, my ayde, I séeke no other defence.

Plot summary

Plot — The Most Virtuous and Godly Susanna
Satan commissions Ill Report to bring about the downfall of Susanna, a paragon of virtue. Since pride, gluttony, envy, sloth, and covetousness have failed to achieve this, Ill Report decides to use lust. He selects the elders Voluptas and Sensualitas as his instruments of temptation, and they are tempted in turn when they see Susanna. Susanna’s husband Joachim leaves to inspect his realm, leaving the elders to administer justice in his absence. The elders spy on Susanna as she bathes in her orchard after dinner, and proposition her for sex. When she refuses, they slander her with whoredom. Meanwhile Joachim discovers that the elders are corrupt and hated by the people. The elders’ perjured testimony ensures that Susanna is sentenced to death, but God intervenes, sending Daniel to reveal the truth. The elders are stoned, and die penitent. Ill Report is hanged, and a frustrated Satan arrives to carry him to hell and tormenters for his failure. Susanna and her family count their blessings.
