Full speech

Is a gallant? I dare doe more then this, if a bee a the true Court cut; Ile take him out a lesson worth the learning: but wee are but their Apes; whats he worth? There is a gentlewoman sent hither by a Lord, shee's a peece of dainty stuffe my rogue, smooth, and soft, as new Satten; shee was neuer gumb'd yet boy, nor fret ted. Shee lies aboue, towards the street, not to be spoke with, but by the Lord that sent her, or some from him, we haue in charge from his seruants. Be gone, I shall doe reason with him. Sir, you haue giuen it a more speciall regard by your good language, then these blacke brows can merit. Faire sir? I thanke yee; all the poore meanes I haue left to be thought gratefull, is but a kisse, and ye shall haue it Sir. Prooue it againe Sir, it may bee your sence was set too high, & so ouer wrought it selfe. Foure a clocke sir. You will haue the better stomacke to your supper; in the meane time, Ile feed ye with delight. Sir, we can haue a Capon ready. 'Tis Friday Sir. Come sweet, ye shall not thinke on meat; Ile drow it with a better appetite. 'Tis now too late to send; I say ye shall not thinke on meat: if ye doe, by this kisse Ile be angry. What wil ye haue Sir? name but the Fish, my maid shall bring it, if it may be got. It is so now: but cou'd ye stay till supper? Plot summary

Plot — The Woman Hater (The Hungry Courtier)
Oriana is on her way to visit the Duke’s court when she is caught in a hail-storm, and takes refuge in the house of Gondarino. Gondarino is a confirmed misogynist, and makes her duly unwelcome. The Duke, on his way to visit Oriana at home, is also caught in the storm and he too shelters in Gondarino’s house. Gondarino suspects that he is unwittingly being made the host of an illicit assignation (and indeed the Duke is attracted to Oriana). Oriana takes umbrage at Gondarino’s attitude to women, and pretends to pursue him amorously in an effort to torment him out of it. He sends her to stay in a bawdy-house, and tells the Duke that she is unchaste. Her brother Valore disputes this, and Gondarino is suspected of slander. To defend himself, he shows her to the Duke in her new lodgings, but his story is discredited when it emerges that he sent her there himself. The Duke sends the courtier Arrigo to kill Oriana, but he offers to spare her in return for sex. She refuses, and thereby proves her chastity: the Duke was testing her, and she now accepts his proposal
