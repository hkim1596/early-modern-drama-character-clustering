Full speech

You doe not well, Cornelio, to dis urage the bashfull youth. O the good God of Gods, How blinde is Pride? what Eagles we are still, In matters that belong to other men? What Beetles in our owne? I tell you Knight, It is consest to be as I haue tould you; And Gratiana, is by young Rinaldo, And your white sonne, brought to me as his wife: How thinke you now Sir? Nay good Gostanzo, thinke you are a Father. Be not so violent, I pray you good Gostanzo, Take truce with passion, licence your sad sonne, To speake in his excuse. He knows he does it but to blinde my eyes. Yes by heauen: A knauerie thus effected might deceiue A wiser man then I, for I ah las, Am noe good polititian, plaine beleeuing Simple honesty, is my policy still. Not much honesty, if I may speake without offence to his father. You must remember too, to let your sonne Vse his accustom'd visitations, Onely to blinde my eyes. Well sir let me alone, Ile beare a braine. Why Sir: Ahlas Ile wager with your wisedome, His consorts drew him to it, for of him selfe He is both vertuous, bashfull, innocent: Comes not at Cittie: knowes no Cittie Art, But plies your Husbandrie; dares not view a Wench. Whose wife is Gratiana now I pray? Tis true, Mine eyes soone blinded: and your selfe would say so, If you knew all: Where lodg'd your Sonne last night? Doe you know that with all your pollicie? By heauen I chaunc' this morne, I know not why To passe by Gra anas bed-chamber, And whom saw I fast by her naked side, But your Ualerio? That was not all, for by them on a stoole, My Sonne sate laughing, to see you so gull'd, Pray you why Sir? I marrie Sir, now I commend your wisedome.

Plot summary

Plot — 219
Christ has recently come to Jewry, and Infidelity is keen to interfere with his work: his plan is to influence the religious establishment against Christ and thereby ensure his crucifixion. Meanwhile, he encourages Mary Magdalen to enjoy her youth while she can, and introduces her to three companions who will help: Pride, Cupidity, and Carnal Concupiscence (all of whom go under less damaging names). They recommend fashionable clothes and hairstyles, which will attract men, but also advise her against marriage. Mary meets The Law of God and Knowledge of Sin, and experiences the stirrings of conscience: she despairs, thinking herself surely damned and God unjust. Christ casts seven devils out of Mary and drives away Infidelity: she is given the grace to repent. Simon the Pharisee is suspicious of Christ because he is lower-class and uneducated, and invites him to dinner. During the meal, Mary washes his feet with her tears and anoints them. Simon is outraged, but Christ emphasizes the universality of forgiveness: Mary is a genuine penitent, whereas Simon is merely a hypocrite wanting to show off his wealth and presumed holiness. Simon is offended and sets Infidelity and Malicious Judgement to follow Christ, spy on his preaching, and have him brought to trial. Mary ends the play in the company of Love and Justification, who emphasize that she is saved through her faith alone.
