Why now the womb of mischiefe is deliuer'd, Of the prodigious issue of the night. Lies thy cold father dead, his glossed eyes New closed vp by thy sad mothers hands? Hast thou a loue as spotlesse as the browe Of clearest heauen, blurd with false defames? Are thy moyst entrals crumpled vp with griefe Of parching mischiefs? Tel me, does thy hart With punching anguish spur thy galled ribs? Then come and let's sit and weep & wreath our arms: Ile heare thy counsell. May I be cursed, but I credit thee. Alas, for what? God be with you. Suspect, I scorn't. Here's cap & leg; good night: Thou that wants power, with dissemblance fight. THE black iades of swart night trot foggy rings Bout heauens browe. (12) Tis now starke deade night. Is this Saint Markes Church? So I may sleepe toumb'd in an honour'd hearse, So may my bones rest in that Sepulcher, May I be cursed by my fathers ghost, And blasted with incensed breath of heauen, If my heart beat on ought but vengeance, May I be numd with horror, and my vaines Pucker with sing'ing torture, if my braine Disgest a thought, but of dire vengeance: May I be fetter'd slaue to coward Chaunce, If blood, heart, braine, plot ought saue vengeance. O, for thy sisters sake, I flagge reuenge. See, vnamaz'd, I will beholde thy face, Outstare the terror of thy grimme aspect, Daring the horred'st obiect of the night. Looke how I smoake in blood, reeking the steame ▪ Of foming vengeance. O my soule's inthroan'd In the tryumphant chariot of reuenge. Me thinks I am all ayre, and feele no waight Of humane dirt clogge. This is Iulios bloode. Rich musique, father; this is Iulio's blood. Why liues that mother? By wisdomes heart there is no essence mortal, That I can enuie, but a plumpe cheekt foole: O, he hath a patent of immunities Confirm'd by custome, seald by pollicie, As large as spatious thought. What? not a foole? Why friend, a golden asse, A babl'd foole are sole canonicall, Whil'st pale cheekt wisdome, and leane ribd arte Are kept in distance at the halberts point: All held Apocrypha, not worth suruey. Why, by the Genius of that Florentine, Deepe, deepe obseruing, sound brain'd Macheueil, He is is not wise that striues not to seeme foole. When will the Duke holde feed Intelligence, Keepe warie obseruation in large pay, To dogge a fooles act? Pish, most things that morally adhere to soules, VVholly exist in drunke opinion: VVhose reeling censure, if I valew not, It valewes naught. Alberto, see you streight rumour me dead: Leaue me, good mother, leaue me Luceo, Forsake me all. Now patience hoope my sides, With steeled ribs, least I doe burst my breast With struggling passions. Now disguise stand bolde. Poore scorned habits, oft choyce soules infould. What villanie are they decocting now? Vmh. Plot — 185
Simplicity, a new arrival in London, is horrified to find the city full of heresy, where ‘old custom’, the traditional religious observances of earlier generations, is held in contempt. Knowledge offers to explain the truth, and shows how the mass has no basis in scripture and certainly does not involve transubstantiation. He teaches Simplicity the doctrinal basis of Protestant worship: pray and fast simply, and shun popish idolatry. Simplicity is duly grateful.
