Thou hast thy wish, Orlando, passe vnknowne, Sforsa shall onely goe along with thee, To see that Warrant serued vpon thy Sonne. Away: ply you the businesse. You know what I haue said. Infaelice. Th' old Fellow sings that note thou didst before, Onely his tunes are, that she is no Whore, But that she sent his Letters and his gifts, Out of a Noble Triumph o're his Lust, To shew she trampled his Assaults in dust. I doubt no lesse. It may be so too, for to turne a Harlot Honest, it must be by strong Antidots, 'Tis rare, as to see Panthers change their spots. And when she's once a Starre (fixed) and shines bright, Tho 'twere impiety then to dim her light, Because we see such Tapers seldome burne. Yet 'tis the pride and glory of some men, To change her to a blazing Starre agen, And it may be, Hipollito does no more. It cannot be, but y'are acquainted all With that same madnesse of our Sonne-in-law, That dotes so on a Curtizan. I doubt then you haue all those stinking breaths, You might be all smelt out. It's well yet you confesse: but Girle, thy bed Shall not be parted with a Curtizan—'tis strange, No frowne of mine, no frowne of the poore Lady, (My abused child, his wife) no care of fame, Of Honor, Heauen or Hell, no not that name Of Common Strumpet, can affright, or woo Him to abandon her; the Harlot does vndoe him, She has bewitched him, robd him of his shape, Turnd him into a beast, his reason's lost, You see he lookes wild, does he not? He's no more life vnto Hipollito, Then dead men are to liuing—neuer sleepes, Or if he doe, it's dreames; and in those dreames His armes worke,—and then cries—Sweet—what's her Name, what's the drabs name? Oh, Bellafront! And catching her fast, cries, My Bellafront. Ile try all Phisicke, and this Med'cine first: I haue directed Warrants strong and peremptory (To purge our Citty Millan, and to cure the outward Parts, the Suburbes) for the attaching Of all those women, who (like gold) want waight, Citties (like Ships) should haue no idle fraight. This (Carolo.) If she whom my Son doates on, Be in that Master-booke enrold, he'll shame Euer t' approach one of such noted name. Yet on Harlots heads New Lawes shall fall so heauy, and such blowes shall Giue to those that haunt them, that Hipollito (If not for feare of Law) for loue to her, If he loue truely, shall her bed forbeare. Those onely shall be caught that are of note, Harlots in each street flow: The fish being thus i'th net, our selfe will sit, And with eye most seuere dispose of it.—come, Girle. Your Bridewell? that the name? for beauty, strength, Capacity and forme of ancient building, (Besides the Riuers neighbourhood) few houses Wherein we keepe our Court can better it. 'Tis Fortunes sport: These changes common are: the Wheele of Fate Turnes Kingdomes vp, till they fall desolate. But how are these seuen hundred Markes by'th yeere Imployde in this your Worke-house? Thus wholsom'st Lawes spring from the worst abuse. His name? For a robbery? where is she? Fetch you him hither— Is this the Party? Are your two Seruants ready? 'Tis well: this day in Iudgement shall be spent, Vice (like a wound launc'd) mends by punishment. Tell him we wish his presence. A word Storsa: On what wings flew he hither? 'Tis excellent: away, goe call him hither. You are to vs a stranger (worthy Lord) 'Tis strange to see you here. Attomyes neither shape, nor honour beare: Be you yourselfe, a Sunne-beame to shine cleare. Is this the Gentleman? Stand forth & heare your accusation. 'Tis well done to confesse. Did any hand worke in this theft but yours? Old fellow, was thy hand in too? What fury prompts thee on to kill thy wife? Is she a Whore? Your Father has the true Phisicion plaid. Fetch him: and when these payments vp are cast, Weigh out your light Gold, but let's haue them last. In Bridewell, Candido? What make you here? I'm here to saue right, and to driue wrong hence. You ha bought stolne Goods. Well, stand by, If you take wrong, wee'll cure the iniurry. Stay, stay, what's he? a prisoner? Bots, and where ha you serued, Bots? Well, sir, because you say you are a Soldier, Ile vse you like a Gentleman: make roome there, Plant him amongst you, we shall haue anon Strange Hawkes flie here before vs: if none light on you, You shall with freedome take your flight: But if you proue a Bird of baser wing, Wee'll vse you like such Birds, here you shall sing. Why, is he furnisht with those in p yments? This does sauour Of Iustice, basest Slaues to basest labour. Now pray, set open Hell, and let vs see The Shee-Deuils that are here. Let them be marshall'd in: be couerd all, Fellowes, now to make the Sceane more Comicall. O fie, abuse her not, let vs two talke, What mought I call your name, pray? Why is this wheele borne after her? Past shame: past penitence, why is that blue Gowne? Are all the rest like this? Variety is good, let's see the rest. Come off so, and 'tis well. What is your name, I pray? Euen now she wept, and praid, now does she curse? Is she a Citty-dame, she's so attyred? And what were you the whilest? Why, doe you know him? he saies he is a Sol dier. Is't Silke 'tis lined with then? Tell her the Duke is here. Why before her does the Bason ring? No, shut vp shop, wee'll now breake vp the faire, Yet ere we part—you, sir, that take vpon yee The name of Soldier, that true name of worth, Which, action not vaine boasting best sets forth, To let you know how farre a Soldiers name Stands from your title, and to let you see, Soldiers must not be wrong'd where Princes be: This bee your sentence. First, all the priuat sufferance that the house Inflicts vpon Offenders, you (as the basest) Shall vndergoe it double, after which You shall bee whipt, sir, round about the Citty, Then banisht from the Land. Away with him, see it done, Panders and Whores Are Citty-plagues, which being kept aliue, Nothing that lookes like goodnes ere can thriue. Now good Orlando, what say you to your bad Sonne-in-law ▪ Then heare, Matheo: all your woes are stayed By your good Father-in-law: all your Ills Are cleare purged from you by his working pills. Come Signior Candido, these greene yong wits (We see by Circumstance) this plot hath laid, Still to prouoke thy patience, which they finde A wall of Brasse, no Armour's like the minde; Thou hast taught the Citty patience, now our Court Shall be thy Spheare, where from thy good report, Rumours this truth vnto the world shal sing, A Patient man's a Patterne for a King.

Plot summary

Plot — 2 The Honest Whore (2 The Converted Courtesan)
On his way to court, Hippolito receives petitioners, including the reformed courtesan Bellafront: she wants his help in petitioning the Duke to pardon her husband Matheo, who has been condemned to death for homicide. Hippolito solicits the help of Bellafront’s father Orlando, but he is still morally outraged at her former sexual misconduct. Nevertheless he secretly arranges for Matheo’s release, and enters his household disguised as a servingman, Pacheco. Matheo proves dissolute, and when ‘Pacheco’ gives him money for safe keeping, he gambles it all away. Bellafront is trying not to let poverty and Matheo’s prodigality force her back into prostitution. Hippolito, who converted her from whoredom, now tries to seduce her back again. She sends ‘Pacheco’ to return his love-tokens, but ‘Pacheco’ instead takes them to Infelice, who identifies them as her own gifts to Hippolito. She interrogates Hippolito’s go-between, Brian the footman, then tells her husband that Brian gave her the love-tokens and had an affair with her. Brian is dismissed and goes home to Ireland. Desperate for money, Matheo strips the gown from his wife’s back and sells it. Lodovico promises to furnish him as a gentleman, so he is properly dressed when Orlando visits in his own person. The old man feigns lack of sympathy, then returns as Pacheco in time to prevent Matheo assaulting Bellafront. Prompted by Orlando, the Duke takes a hand: he issues a warrant to arrest Matheo for robbing two pedlars (who were actually Orlando’s men); and, hearing that Hippolito is in love with a whore, but not knowing her identity, passes new anti-prostitution laws. Meanwhile, Candido is married a second time, but his bride tests his patience with unruly behaviour on their wedding day. He determines to tame her, and Lodovico helps in the guise of an apprentice; she submits herself after a mock duel with measuring sticks. Some gallants attempt to seduce her into a brothel, but fail, and Candido still keeps his temper when, drawn to an alehouse for a business deal, he is forced to drink to a bawd’s health. Matheo is arrested at the alehouse on the Duke’s warrant, and Candido too for receiving the cloth stolen from the pedlars. At Bridewell, Matheo admits the robbery, but attempts to incriminate Bellafront out of jealousy for her supposed relationship with Hippolito.
