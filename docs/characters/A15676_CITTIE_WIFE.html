God buy ye. Exeunt I, your Dogge or Bitch: Or ſolely your ſword, better a bad toole then none at all. Wee make no doubt of that, come into a chamber, ye ſhall lye downe a while, perhaps youle bee ſtiffe anon, then you ſhall vſe your legges, the more you ſtriue with it, the better, alas good hearts. Exeunt Out al as, where's my Goſip? oh woman! haue you not heard the newes? Oh woman, did your childes childe euer ſee the like, nine men to bee buried too day, that drunke healthes laſt night. They ſay one is your gueſt Philantus, Well, he was a propper man yfaith. Now well fare your harts, by my truth tis ioy to a woman, to ſee men kinde, faith you courtiers are mad fellowes, you care not in your humors to ſtab man or woman that ſtandes in your way, but in the end your kindenes appeares. Sit, nay if there were but good ſtandinges, we care not. Of his charge, dyed he not able to purchaſe a Winding ſheete? I, I, mine Hoſt, you are well imployed to giue a man counſell againſt his wife, they are apt enough to ill I warrant ye. I, and twere more ſeemely you were at your owne houſe too, your wife cannot goe abroad but you muſt follow, husbands muſt bee fringed to their wiues Petticoates, I pray you tarrie you, ile goe home. By my troth methinkes hee ſhould be aliue, I could finde in my heart to feele his fleſh. Why I hope your husband is able to main taine you better: are there not nights as well as daies? does he not ſleepe ſome times? has hee no pockets about him? cannot you ſearch his breeches? anye thing you finde in his breeches is your owne. I and more, why ſhould ſhee not? why what is his is yours, what's yours, your owne: Or if all faile, you may hire a good ſuite at a Iewes: or at a broakers, tis a common thing and ſpeci ally among the common ſort. Heele be horne mad, & neuer able to indure it: why woman if he haue but as much man in him as a Maribone, heele take the burthen vppon his own necke, and neuer diſcouer you Let me helpe ye woman. What qualities hath he Accutus? And truely by his head one ſhould be mine. I warrant he ranne mad for loue, becauſe no good face could endure the ſight of him, and euer ſince he railes a gainſt women like a whot ſhot.

Plot — Every Woman in Her Humor
Misogyny has made the normally jovial Acutus.- melancholy. After scaring some foolishly fashionable characters, he joins them at the Hobby tavern, where Gaetica has taken lodgings with her dog. Disguised as a © soldier, Acutus picks a fight with Scilicet and Servulus, © then exposes their hypocrisy by returning disguised as first a friend and then an enemy of the ‘soldier’, and eliciting inconsistent responses. Scilicet attempts to woo Gaetica; she is more concerned about the disappearance of her man Bos with her dog, but becomes betrothed to him. , Lentulus is in love, and asks Cicero to woo Terentia for him. They go to dine with Flaminius, Terentia’s father, and Cicero is left alone with the ladies of the house. The upshot is that Terentia falls in love — but -- with Cicero himself rather than Lentulus. Rumours circulate that she is to marry Cicero, but Flaminius has not given his consent and takes offence. Lentulus makes Cicero and Terentia admit wronging him, and. _ he sentences Cicero to death — but only if Terentia will kill him. He woos and wins Terentia’s sister Flavia, and plans a double wedding. Acutus gives Philautus a sleeping potion, and he appears to die. He is rumoured to have drunk himself to death, but the reported numbers of alcohol victims escalate as the news circulates. The Hostess allows him to hide in her chamber, but Acutus, pretending to have been robbed, sends a constable to search the tavern; Philautus is found and arrested. The drunken Bos has his clothes stolen, and is brought back to the tavern in a barrel. Gracchus agrees to give him a new suit if he can demonstrate that drunkenness is a virtue, which he proceeds to do in a debate. Gracchus promises to arrange for the Hostess and Dama to attend the wedding. At the wedding, Caesar declares a general amnesty; Acutus and Gracchus have Lentulus intercede to procure Philautus’ release.
