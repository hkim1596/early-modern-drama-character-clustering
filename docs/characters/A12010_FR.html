Plot summary

Plot — Romeo and Juliet
After a street fight breaks out between servants of the rival Veronese households of Capulet and Montague, the Prince decrees death for any further breach of the peace in the matter. Romeo, the Montague son, is hopelessly in love with a Capulet lady, Rosaline, and discovers by chance that she is to be present at Old Capulet’s feast that night. He and his friends gatecrash _ the party disguised as masquers; Tybalt notices Romeo and wants to throw him out, but Old Capulet discourages this. Also present is Capulet’s daughter, Juliet, who is intended for marriage to Count Paris. Romeo meets her and they fall in love before knowing one another's identity as members of the two enemy houses. Afterwards, Romeo climbs into the Capulet 1595 orchard, overhears Juliet’s private acknowledgement of her love, declares himself, and makes arrangements for a follow-up contact. Seeing an opportunity to end the feud, Friar Laurence agrees to marry the lovers. Juliet rendezvouses with Romeo at the Friar’s cell under cover of going to confession, and they become husband and wife. After sending Romeo a challenge, Tybalt attempts to fight him in the street; when Romeo refuses, Mercutio steps in and is killed, whereupon the enraged Romeo avenges his friend by killing Tybalt. The Prince banishes him for the murder. Friar Laurence and the Nurse arrange for the lovers to have a night together before Romeo leaves the city. Thinking Juliet is distressed by Tybalt’s death, her father arranges to hasten her marriage to Paris, and threatens to disown her when she refuses to comply. The Nurse advises her to accept the situation and marry Paris, but Friar Laurence gives her a sleeping potion to take the night before the wedding: it will make her appear dead, and meanwhile he will send to Romeo to fetch her from the family tomb after her funeral. When she returns home apparently ready to submit to the marriage, her father brings the date forward. Juliet takes the potion and is found, apparently dead, on the morning of the planned wedding. The Friar’s letter miscarries, so Romeo hears only of Juliet’s death; he buys poison from a needy apothecary and returns to Verona. Hearing that his arrangements have gone awry, the Friar plans to rescue Juliet from the tomb and keep her hidden until Romeo can fetch her; but Romeo gets there first.
