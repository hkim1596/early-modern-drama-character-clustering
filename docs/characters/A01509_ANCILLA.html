¶No, no, Madam, he will ere long be with you, you may trust. ¶When fyrst I came into the Court, where we doe serue and dwell, What I did thinke would moue you laugh, if I that tale should tell. ¶I heard once in my fathers house, a Gentleman declare, The worthy customes of the court, and ake the Princely fare, The gorgeous Garmentes of eche Dame, their tyne and famous lyfe, The noble workes of amorous Knights, their stoute and louing stryfe, The pleasure of eche worthy Dame, how they doe hunt and hawke, And wearyed with eche pastyme thus, the streates how they did walk The noble maskes that were showed forth in euery winters night, The Reuels and the reueling cheare, that did eche harte delight, Musitions how they did deuyse, with songes to please the eare, And Musickes arte by instrument that gladded hartes to heare, And sometyme the pore mans chylde, that there is plaste to be, To honour clymes for vertues sake, and brought to hye degrée, Tush, then was I on fyre set, till I could get the grace, Of Fathers and of mothers graunt, to dwell in such a place, They graunted me, and I was glad, thus came I here to dwell, But now I thinke both you and I, another tale can tell, And yet can none of vs denye, but that his wordes were true, But that pertaynes not to my state, nor yet belonges to you. For we but wayters are on them, that leade these happy days, We trudge and trauayle and take payne, they do possesse the prayse, Thus when I thinke how wyde I shot, when I shot at this marke, I laugh to sée how ready youth is still to chaunge his worke, I leapte from ease and quyet state, within my natiue soyle, And came into a stranger ayre, to trauayle and to toyle. ¶Yea Madam ech thing is pr st, and shall be in good hower, He is right carefull for his parte, I doe it you assure. ¶What is that Madam? ¶Prone with your foote, if that the Dore, as we were had he lockt, ¶How say you friend and fellow both, did we not go hence well, Gods blessing haue his honest harte, that fyrst did found that bell. You s d if you remember well ▪ when I did tell my tale, To recompence the same with like, you sayd you would not fayle. ¶You say but true ▪ and to it then, for losing of the tyme, My Mother is a Farmers wyfe, her maydes doe serue her swyne, But here you what, if I agayne were at my countrey home, I would not leape thus to the court, to sit there lyke a mome. For pleasure more is in one hower, there with our Gill and Iack, Then here is in fyue hundred yeres, for nothing there doth lack, There had I for to run abroade my frée and youthly will, Here may we not once péepe for feare, our mouthes are shut vp still, I nothing doubt but when I shall, to mother make my mone, For lack of talke my tongue and lippes togither will be growne, Well chaunge I would this my straight life, I tell you for my parte, And he that brought me to the court beshrew his naked harte. ¶Not long ago I heard one say, I thinke he ment by vs, A latten sence, and this it was, Nemo sua sorte contentus, Women are inquisitiue, I asked him what he ment, He sayd not one in all this world, was with his chaunce content, ¶Well we must now, and therefore peace, and let vs go our way, For we it is that must accoumpt which way we spend the day. ¶The greatest scourge that may be giuen, to any liuing wight, I feele it now, alas, alas, that she is in my sight. Why Cupids flames doe burne my harte, why sée my tormentes here, And ye to ease my hugy smart, I dare not proue for feare. Now is the tyme and let it go, we neuer fynde the léeke, And yet to heale my secrete wo, my hart is now to seeke. ¶Peace friend, consyder well your state, and let your sorrow slake, For I it is that must and will this matter on me take, if you do this with languishing our presence here descrye, Before that we lay handes on her then were we neare the nye. Plot summary

Plot — The Most Virtuous and Godly Susanna
Satan commissions Ill Report to bring about the downfall of Susanna, a paragon of virtue. Since pride, gluttony, envy, sloth, and covetousness have failed to achieve this, Ill Report decides to use lust. He selects the elders Voluptas and Sensualitas as his instruments of temptation, and they are tempted in turn when they see Susanna. Susanna’s husband Joachim leaves to inspect his realm, leaving the elders to administer justice in his absence. The elders spy on Susanna as she bathes in her orchard after dinner, and proposition her for sex. When she refuses, they slander her with whoredom. Meanwhile Joachim discovers that the elders are corrupt and hated by the people. The elders’ perjured testimony ensures that Susanna is sentenced to death, but God intervenes, sending Daniel to reveal the truth. The elders are stoned, and die penitent. Ill Report is hanged, and a frustrated Satan arrives to carry him to hell and tormenters for his failure. Susanna and her family count their blessings.
