My name is called good counsell, I praye God guide you with hys gracyous assistans Unto y e knowlege of hys truth, your ignoraunce to vndo That you may be one of those numbred christians Which foloweth the lambe whether he doth go, The lambe Iesus Christ, my meanynge is so, By sure fayth & confidence, in his bitter death & passion, The onely pryce of oure health and saluacyon. Fyrst of all it is moste expedient. Ful true be these wordes, which Christ hym self did saye He that seketh shal surely fynde. Be holde youth now reioyce we may For I se knowledge of God, verite stand here behinde knowleg entreth. He is come now to satisfey your mynde In those thynges which you will desire, Therfore together let vs approche him nere. Be these wordes which vnto you he doth expresse He teacheth that you ought to haue a stedfast faith With out it is impossible doutlesse, To please god, as sainct Paul sayth: Where fayth is not, godly lyuing decaieth For whatsoeuer is not of fayth, sayth. s. Paul, is synne, But where a perfite faith is, there is good working to Gods word you must onely encline, Al other doctrine cleane set a parte. Here you shall reseaue Christes testament To comfort your consience when nede shal requyre To learne the contentes there of, se that you be dilygent The which all chrystian men ought to desyre For it is the wel, or fountayne moste clere Out of the which doth spryng swete consolacyon To all those that thyrst after eternal saluacyon. Greatly I do be wayle thy misrable estate, The tyrrible plagues, which in Gods lawe are written, Hang ouer thy head both early and late: O fleshly capernite, stubburne and obstinate, Thou haddest leuer forsake Christ thy sauiour and kinge Then thy fleshly swinish lustes, & abbomynable lyuynge. The deuell hath you dysceiued whych is y e auctor of lyes And trape you in hys snar of wicked hypocrye, Therfore all that euer you do deuyse Is to maintayne your fleshly lybertie For thys thyng most cheifly I do complaye: Haue ye not professed the knowledge of Chrystes gospell And yet I thynke no more vngodlines doth raigne, In any wicked Heathen, Turke or infidell: who can deuyse that synne or euyll, That you practyse not from day to day yea and count yt nothyng but a iest or a play, Alas, what wantonnes remaineth in your flesh? Plot summary

Plot — Lusty Juventus
Juventus accepts Good Counsel’s advice not to waste time but lead a pious life; Knowledge explains that he can be saved by faith alone, but that good works are also a necessary sign of his penitence. The Devil worries that his plans are going awry: although Hypocrisy has set up the Catholic church to further his cause, piety and respect for God’s word are rife among young people. Commissioned by the Devil, Hypocrisy intercepts Juventus on the way to a sermon and persuades him to go instead to breakfast with Abominable Living, who seduces him into a dissolute lifestyle. Good Counsel confronts Juventus and persuades him into repentance and despair. He is restored to hope by God’s Merciful Promises, and the play ends with prayers for the King and nobility.
