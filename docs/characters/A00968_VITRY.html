I haue some occasion, I should not else; and the same cause perhaps That makes me do so, may beget in you A contrary effect. I see and ioy to see that sometimes poore men, (And most of such are good) stand more indebted For meanes to breathe to such as are held vitious, Then those that weare like Hypocrites on their foreheads, Th'ambitious titles of iust men and vertuous. Who would e're haue thought The good old Queene, your Highnesse reuerend mother, Into whose house (which was an Academ,) In which all principles of lust were practis'd, No souldier might presume to set his foote; At whose most blessed intercession All offices in the state, were charitably Confer'd on panders, o're-worne chamber wrastlers, And such phisitions as knew how to kill, With safety vnder the pretence of sauing, And such like children of a monstrous peace, That she I say should at the length prouide That men of warre and honest younger brothers, That wold not owe their feeding to their cod-peece, Should be esteem'd of more then mothers, or drones, Or idle vagabonds. One that cannot faile, she and vertuous traine With her Iewells and all that was worthy the carrying, The last night left the court; and as 'tis more Then sayd, for 'tis confirm'd by such as met her, She's fled vnto your brother. Nay storme not, For if that wicked tonge of hers hath not Forgot its pace, and Thierry be a Prince Of such a fiery temper, as report Has giuen him out for; you shall haue cause to vse Such poore men as my selfe; and thanke vs too For comming to you, and without petitions; Pray heauen reward the good old woman for't. Yes any where, To be worse then I am here, is past my feare. What is that to me? Though they and all the polecats of the Court, Were trustde together, I perceiue not how It can aduantage me a cardekue, To helpe to keepe me honest. You seeke too hard a penywoth. I must confesse I haue bin subiect to lewd company. A couple of vnprofitable ones, that haue only seru'd to get me a stomacke to my dinner. You should haue heard me say I had din'd first, I haue built on an vnwholesome ground, rais'd vp a house before I knew a Tenant, matcht to meete wearines, fought to find want and hunger. It may be your worship will say bawdry. Why is their fiue kinds of em, I neuer knew but one. But thus much. After that rate, I and my friends would begger the kingdome. Sir you haue made me blush to see my want, whose cure is such a cheape and easie purchase, this is male bawdery belike. Leaue it to my handling, ile fall vpon't instantly: Will you haue a rellish of wit Lady? If you affect cheapenes, how can you prize this sullied ware so much? mine is fresh, my owne, not retailde. The fitter to be in the dish with such dry stockfish as you Are, how strike? That boxe was left out of the bargaine. Yes, let him; now comes my turne. i'le see if hee can an swere me: saue you Sir, they say you haue that I want, mony. Stand, deliuer. Do you prate? You haue done this, braue man be proud to make him happy, by the bread of god man thou hast a bony countenance, comrade man of vrship, St. Tauy be her patron, out vpon you, you vncurried colts, walking cans that haue no soules in you, but a little rosin, to keepe yonr ribs sweete, and hold in liquor. Then you will not beg againe.

Not recognizing her in her veil, Thierry explains why she must die, and she agrees; but he cannot bring himself to go through with it when he realizes who she is. Martel dissuades Ordella from loyally committing suicide, and, suspecting a plot, has her pretend to be dead in order to smoke it out Meanwhile Brunhild spreads a rumour that Theodoret is illegitimate and has him secretly murdered by Protaldi at the continuing wedding revels. Believing that Theodoret was not his brother and that Ordella is dead, Thierry offers to make amends by marrying Memberge, Theodoret's daughter. Brunhild is forced to admit her lies in order to prevent an incestuous marriage. She gives Thierry a poisoned handkerchief, which kills him; Martel brings Ordella to his deathbed. Brunhild admits complicity in his murder, and is sentenced to remain awake for the rest of her life; disturbed by the sight of Protaldi being tortured to death, she commits suicide. Thierry and Ordella die together , and Martel becomes King of France.
