Sir, I haue done your remembrances to Mistris Hilaria, and told her you should find her comming by and by, but you were best passe in some obscurity, for her fa ther Rufaldo is hard by sir. Lupus in Fabula. God saue you sir. I confesse, you tooke off the rough-cast, but 'twas Gorgons head brought forth the proiect, from my Ioues braine came this Minerua. If I faile, call me Spider-catcher. Not out of my wit, Sir, I haue a Letter from my yong Mistris Selina, must in all haste haue cleanely conuey ance to old Rufaldo. I hope, sir, you will not throw open the sheet, and discouer my Mistris secrets? How he stares! He has made a Taylors bill on't, torn't apieces ere it be discharg'd: What shall I doe? This? Ile but hold it by the hand. This sir, a leg, it shall goe with me then. So I haue all his quarters, Ile presently, sir, get poles for 'em, and hang 'em vpon the Gates in their po stures for you. My most ingenious and noble Criticotaster, braue ly bils, pils you should say, not fac'd, but lin'd, with gold they swallow 'em greedily, and still flocke to 'em, and con glomerate my sonne and heire of the Muses: a proclamati on is as quiet as the poore mans box, no man lookes after it, not a Balladmonger has any audience: but happy is the man that rides first poast to your papers & cryes admirable: your old men looke vpon 'em with their spectacles, as they would eye an obligation within a minute of forfeyture. Foot, 'tis Bubulcus. Three cuts and a halfe, hey. I giue you, sir, gene rous salutation, and wish a faire morne descend vpon you: thrice Noble Spirit, welcome, does your worship desire to bee sprinkled with the drops of Hellicon, to gather the Pippins of Pernassus, and haue your forehead fillited with Apollinean Bayes, or Laurell? You shall be verberated, and reuerberated, my ex act piece of stollidity: please you draw neere, there is the starre of Eloquence, vnder whom I am an Hypodidascall, in English, his Vsher. Approach without feare: here is a Pupill, sir, desires to suck the hony of your eloquence, he is a Gentleman in Foli . He askes what kind of verbosity you would be practis'd in? because I perceiue you are raw, I will descend to your capacity, he askes what complement you would learne? How? There is an Vshers fee belongs to my place. Simple, I am sure: so, sir, looke you, I should teach you to make a leg first, but these postures anon. Resplendent Mistris, for thy face doth farre Excell all other, like a blazing Starre We mortals wonder at, vouchsafe to cast Out of those sparkling Diamond eyes thou hast, A sacred Influence on thy vowed creature, That is confounded with thy forme and feature. Goddesse of Cyprus. Oh, there's a conceit, Cyprus is the embleme of morning, and here by Cyprus you declare how much you pine and mourne after her, sir. Goddesse of Cyprus, Venus is a slut. You doe tell the Goddesse, that Venus is a slut. No, sir, your Loue is Venus faire, and shee is not: That makes plaine the other, that she is a slut. O that I were a flea vpon thy lip, There would I sucke for euer, and not skip. That is, you would not bite her by the lip. Or if thou thinkst I there too high am plast, Ile be content to sucke below thy waste. Thy foote I'de willing kisse, but that I know, Thou wouldst not haue thy seruant stoope so low. She will giue you leaue to kisse higher. Oh speake thou, wilt be mine? and I will be The truest Worme e're trod on shoo to thee. By Worme you doe insinuate and wriggle your selfe into your affection, and shee by shoo will conceiue your desire the length of her foote: how doe you like it, sir? Oh, any ordinary Lady, you must get it without Booke. Now to make your legs. See me salute her. As many happinesses waite on Delia, As beames shoote from the Sunne this pleasant morne. Her Mistresses best moueable, a Chamber-maide. She is an early riser, and yet sometime as soone downe as vp, she cannot be quiet for the Seruingmen, 'tis her houre betweene eight a clocke and her Mistris rising, to come to discipline. Oh, sir, all her credit is in her good name: it was Diana's the Goddesse of chastity, and therefore when she marries, she may cuckold her Husband by a priuiledge, for Diana gaue hornes to Actaeon. Good men of the Iury for this Session, I will not implicate you with ambages and circumstances, I am vn willing to confound your little wits with affected diuisi ons of my narration, into quis, quid, quomodo, and quandoes: I will neither vtter by grosse, nor part my speech into a doozen of long points, knotted often in the middle, and vntagd in the end; your are to present Malefactors, where of you are the chiefe—Reformers, and seeing you stand ready for your charge, I will giue fire to this great piece of seruice, & send you all off with a powder, that in any case we may goe to dinner betimes, &c. And doe you heare, if he chance to be at a nonplus, he may helpe himselfe with his beard and handkercher, or it will be a good posture for his hand now and then to be fumbling with his bandstrings. Farewell. Lady, wounded by your beauty, I will acknow ledge mercy if you kill me not, yet rather murther me, then vulnerate still your creature, vnlesse you meane to medi cine where you haue hurt, and I implore no better remedy then I may deriue from the instrument wherewith you pierced mee, like Achilles Speare, your eye hauing shot lightning into my brest, hath power with a smile to fetch out the consuming fire, and yet leaue my heart inflam'd. So, very well, this is your cunning lesson. Sir, the Country comes in vpon vs. Orlando Furioso. So, so, 'tis well, you shall take forth a new lesson, sit downe and breathe. Indeed, sir, I haue made inquisition, both my tongue and my feete haue walked, but my Mistris is not to be found or heard of, Ile assure you. Mistris, then we are all made.— He capers. Gon in wine, sir, for ioy of his sisters finding agen. Pray', sir, let me take my iourney in the morning, the wedding night is fatall: I hope your worship does but iest, I may be drunke too night, and wake early enough to be gon afore day too, I beseech you sir. Gorgon begins to be a wandring Knight. I thinke Iupiter has snatch'd vp my Master An tonio, to make a Ganimede on him, hee is not to be found yet, I haue search 't all the Tauernes ith Towne, I am sure, and that method my nose led me to, hoping he had beene a good fellow, but, non est inuentus, well, my stocke is spent, but with this terrible face a buffe Ierkin, and a roaring basket-hilt, Gorgon will haue a tricke of wit to beare his owne charges: but here comes a Gentleman, to my po stures now. Good your worship bestow a small piece of siluer vpon a poore Souldier, new-come out of the Low-Coun tries, that haue beene in many hot seruices, against the Spaniard, the French, and great Turke. I haue beene shot seuen times thorow the body, my eyes blowne vp with gun-powder, halfe my skull seard off with a Canon, and had my throat cut twice in the open field: good your wor ship take compassion vpon the caterwaking fortunes of a forlorne Gentleman, that haue lost the vse of my veines: good your generous nature take compassion vpon me, I haue but foure fingers and a thumbe vpon one hand: can worke, and woonnot: one small piece of gratefull siluer, to for my lodging, I beseech you venerable sir. Onely a little glimmering, sir, the beames of your gentility haue radiated, and infused light into my poore lanternes, sir. Oh, sir, that faculty alone, fortune and nature haue left inuiolated. Gasparo, is it you? Pox on your beneuolence. Oh, I could caper for't. Phillis faire, doe not disdaeine The loue of Coridon thy Swaine. But that were pretty, shall I be a woman? Foote! shall I runne into my coates agen? goe to, put me into what shape you will, Ile play my part: mee thinkes I doe feele an hundred rurall animals taking vp my petticoate already. It will be a quarter of a yeere next grasse. Laugh, and sing him to his graue shall I? Oh no, his loue like a canker hath eate such a great hole into my heart, I cannot forget him, but Ile sing a song of him. Yes forsooth. With a Gentleman that has prou'd vnkind, for sooth, broken his vowes, and oathes, forsooth, hee made much of me, time was, before his father dyed, forsooth, who was a good Yeoman, then he kist me, forsooth, and coll'd surreuerence, but now hee scornes Mopsa: I was his equall once, and haue danced with him vpon our Church greene in a Morris ere now. He is now about to commit makrimoniall busi nesse with a young girle I wisse. Beleeue him? nay, you may beleeue him, he's abo mination cunning man, he told me my fortune as right as if he had beene in my belly. Partly, sir, as other womens fortunes doe. Oh, no, no, your suite is in vaine, hands off, Shep heards. Beauty! I doe confesse I haue reasonable beauty, for blacke and white, for all other colours are but compoun ded of them: but the truth is, I cannot so soone forget my old loue; though he hath prou'd false vnto me, Mopsa will proue true vnto him: oh, and it were not for shame, now I would die for loue. Noble sir. Sir, you know who I am, I am yet walking Terra incognita, I haue a great minde to Bubulcus, you know what I haue suffered for him, and so forth. Oh, sir, you sung another song in my Mothers Dairy, when we sate vp all night together, and had a Sack posset. I beseech you, Gentlemen, speake for mee, for I will haue him, I am asham'd to shew my reasons. More then that, sir. Indeed Mistris, Ile tell you; hee hath begot thr children of my body. Yes, beare witnesse, Gentlemen, I doe accept his wise beneuolence, and will neuer trouble him with mar riage—while Gorgon liues. Your seruant, and your pardons: nay, Gorgon has had his deuices and vagabunduloes as well as the best on yee: giue you all ioy, I wish you wit, sir.

Plot summary

Plot — The School of Compliment (Love Tricks)
Selina and Hilaria have each attracted the attention of several suitors. Selina’s brother Antonio is one of those interested in Hilaria; and Hilaria’s recently widowed father Rufaldo, who disapproves of Antonio and would rather she married the wealthy Bubulcus, would nevertheless like Selina for his second wife. Antonio has his friend and servant distract Rufaldo while he sneaks in to visit Hilaria; they do it by persuading Rufaldo that he has been miraculously rejuvenated. Selina’s father is happy to let her have her own way in ===== PAGE 140 ===== her love-life, because her sister Felice absconded after he interfered in hers. He proposes that she should be cautious about marrying Rufaldo, but she declares enthusiasm for the idea and asks for a hasty wedding; on the appointed day, however, she disappears and goes to live in the country disguised as a shepherd. Felice’s abandoned suitor Gasparo sets up a school of compliment to teach people refined behaviour. His clients include: Bubulcus, who wants to learn how to woo Hilaria and scorn Antonio; Delia, a chambermaid keen to brush up her etiquette: a judge who wants to learn how to address the jury; Medulla, a social-climbing widow; an old countryman who wants to turn his oafish son into a courtier; a quarrelsome gentleman; Ingeniolo, a justice’s clerk in love; and Jenkin, an amorous Welshman in search of poetry to turn a shepherdess’s head. The search for Selina sends several people off to the country, including her true love Infortunio. Antonio pretends to go too, and appears not to return; in fact he impersonates Selina and ‘marries’ Rufaldo. On the wedding night, the old man is horrified to learn that his new ‘wife’ will not sleep with him, and also has the physical strength to enforce ‘her’ will on him. Anxious not to lose ‘her’ altogether, he asks ‘her’ at least to live in his house and sleep with Hilaria; Antonio readily agrees. Bubulcus boasts to ‘Selina’ and Hilaria that he has killed the missing Antonio in a duel, stupidly forgetting that Selina is Antonio’s sister; ‘she’ fetches officers, and Bubulcus is arrested for murder. The real Selina has fallen in with a community of shepherds. Among them is her missing sister Felice, who uses herbal remedies to cure Infortunio of his love-melancholy for Selina.
