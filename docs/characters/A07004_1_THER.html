Full speech

Before the Moone renew her borrowed light, Doubt not my Lord and gratious Soueraigne, But Tamburlaine, and that Tartarian rout, Shall either perish by our warlike hands, Or plead for mercie at your highnesse feet. Where is this Scythian Tamberlaine? Tamburlaine? A Scythian Shepheard, so imbellished With Natures pride, and richest furniture, His looks do menace heauen and dare the Gods, His fierie eies are fixt vpon the earth. As if he now deuis'd some Stratageme: Or meant to pierce Auernas darksome vaults. To pull the triple headed dog from hell. Not Hermes Prolocutor to the Gods, Could vse perswasions more patheticall. What stronge enchantments tice my yeelding soule Are these resolued noble Scythians? But shall I prooue a Traitor to my King? Won with thy words, & conquered with thy looks, I yeeld my selfe, my men & horse to thee: To be partaker of thy good or ill, As long as life maintaines Theridamas. Nor thee, nor them, thrice noble Tambutlain Shal want my heart to be with gladnes pierc'd To do you honor and securitie. A God is not so glorious as a King: I thinke the pleasure they enioy in heauen Can not compare with kingly ioyes in earth, To weare a Crowne enchac'd with pearle and golde, Whose vertues carie with it life and death, To aske, and haue: command, and be obeied. When looks breed loue, with lookes to gaine the prize; Such power attractiue shines in princes eies. I know they would with our perswasions. Goe on for me. And that made me to ioine with tamburlain For he is grosse and like the massie earth, That mooues not vpwards, nor by princely deeds Doth meane to soare aboue the highest sort. Euen he that in a trice vanquisht two kings, More mighty than the Turkish Emperour: Shall rouse him out of Europe, and pursue His scattered armie til they yeeld or die. I long to see those crownes won by our swords That we may raigne as kings of Affrica. Giue her the Crowne Turkesse you wer best. Here Madam, you are Empresse, she is none, Your tentes of white now pitch'd before the gates And gentle flags of amitie displaid. I doubt not but the Gouernour will yeeld, Offering Damascus to your Maiesty. But if his highnesse would let them be fed, it would doe them more good. He stamps it vnder his feet my Lord. Doost thou think that Mahomet wil suffer this Me thinks, tis a great deale better than a con sort of musicke. So it would my lord, specially hauing so smal a walke, and so litle exercise. I (my Lord) but none saue kinges must feede with these. And since your highnesse hath so well vouchsaft, If we deserue them not with higher meeds Then erst our states and actions haue retain'd, Take them away againe and make vs slaues. We know the victorie is ours my Lord, But let vs saue the reuerend Souldans life, For faire Zenocrate, that so laments his state. Then let vs set the crowne vpon her head, That long hath lingred for so high a seat.
