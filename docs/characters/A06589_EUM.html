I hope you be not sotted vpon the man in the Moone. Is Endimion mad, or doe I mistake? doe you loue the Moone Endimion? There was neuer any so peeuish to imagin the Moone eyther capable of affection, or shape of a Mi stris: for as impossible it is to make loue fit to her humor which no man knoweth, as a coate to her forme, which continueth not in one bignesse whilst she is measuring. Cease of Endimion to feede so much vpon fancies. That melancholy blood must be purged, which draweth you to a dotage no lesse miserable then monstrous. But they haue vanities, which beeing refor med, you may be restored. Stay there Endimion, thou that committest I dolatry, wilt straight blaspheme, if thou be suffered. Sleepe woulde doe thee more good then speech: the Moone heareth thee not, or if shee doe, regardeth thee not. Without doubt Endimion is bewitched, other wise in a man of such rare vertues, there could not har bor a minde of such extreame madnes.
