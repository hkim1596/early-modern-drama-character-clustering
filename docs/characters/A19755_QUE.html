Plot summary

Plot — The Bloody Banquet
Although peace between Lydia and Lycia is guaranteed by an exchange of hostages, the King of Lycia persuades the Lydian hostage, Lapyrus, to betray his country. When hostilities resume, Lydia solicits military assistance from Armatrites, King of Cilicia, who repels the invaders - but then seizes the Lydian throne. The Queen flees with her infant children into the forest, where Lapyrus rescues her from the advances of two soldiers. In remorse for his treachery, he gives her the opportunity to kill him, but she cannot bring herself to do it. He becomes her protector, and goes in search of food, but falls into a wolf-trap. One of the babies dies of starvation. The old King of Lydia rescues Lapyrus from the pit, but the Queen has gone when Lapyrus shows the King where he left her. Armatrites’ son, Zenarchus, promises Tymethes, the heir of Lydia, that he will give back the kingdom when he succeeds to the throne. Tymethes falls in love with Zenarchus’ sister, Amphridote, who has been promised in marriage to the court favourite Mazeres. Zenarchus and Tymethes visit the young Queen of Cilicia, whom Armatrites has jealously locked up in a castle. She conceives a passion for Tymethes, and bribes her keeper Roxano to arrange an assignation, with the proviso that Tymethes must not know her identity lest he boast of his conquest; but Mazeres also bribes Roxano to kill Tymethes, and his gold seems to weigh the heavier. Tymethes keeps the appointment, is taken to the Queen with a hood over his head, and is feasted, then seduced. With Roxano’s help, Mazeres poses as one of the masked waiters; he attempts to poison Tymethes’ wine, ===== PAGE 92 ===== but the wine is spilt by accident. Though enjoined not to inquire into the lady’s identity, Tymethes purloins her ring in the hope of learning who she is. Mazeres and Armatrites secretly watch a liaison between Tymethes and Amphridote. She begs the ring from Tymethes, and is given it on condition that she keeps it safe. When the King confronts his daughter, he recognizes his wife’s ring, and Mazeres explains how Tymethes came by it. Amphridote is horrified, switches her affections to Mazeres, and swears revenge.
