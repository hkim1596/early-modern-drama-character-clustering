Vnto my Lord the Duke, The best of well come, More lights, attend the Duke. I did nothing to displease him, I carued to him at supper-time Sir in the way of pittie I wish you hart-hole. Sure Sir a loathed crueltie in Ladyes Is as to Doctors many funeralls: It takes away their credit. Tis the ornament Of a weake fortune. Heare sir. To passe away the time I'le tell your grace, A dreame I had last night. A foolish idle dreame, Me thought I walkt about the mid of night, Into a Church-yard, where a goodly Eu Tree Spred her large roote in ground, vnder that Eu, As I sat sadly leaning on a graue, Checkered with crosse-sticks, their came stealing in Your Dutchesse and my husband, one of them A picax bore, th'other a Rusty spade, And in rough termes they gan to challenge me, About this Eu. This harmelesse Eu: They told me my entent was to root vp That well-growne Eu, and plant i'th steed of it A withered blacke-thorne, and for that they vow'd To bury me aliue: my husband straight With picax gan to dig, and your fell Dutchesse With shouell, like a fury, voyded out The earth & scattered bones, Lord how me thought I trembled, and yet for all this terror I could not pray. When to my rescue there arose me thought A whirlewind, which let fall a massy arme From that strong plant, And both were strucke dead by that sacred Eu In that base shallow graue that was their due. Dearest mother heare me. Deere my Lord. The Dutchesse, I do protest if any chast deniall, If any thing but bloud could haue alayed, His long suite to me. O me accurst. Whats he? Plot — The White Devil (Vittoria Corombona)
Count Lodovico is banished from Rome after a career of drunkenness, riot, and murder; but, he comments, not all malefactors fall foul of the law, for the Duke of ===== PAGE 257 ===== 1612 Bracciano seems to be getting away with his designs against the honour of Vittoria Corombona. In this, Bracciano is abetted by Vittoria’s brother Flaminio, who persuades her husband Camillo that their sleeping apart will make her more amorous. Vittoria tells Bracciano of a dream which seems to suggest the murder of Camillo and the Duchess Isabella; but the liaison is forestalled by the intervention of Vittoria’s mother, Cornelia. Flaminio inveighs against Cornelia for compromising his chances of preferment. Isabella comes to Rome to effect a reconciliation with her husband. Cardinal Monticelso and the Great Duke of Florence also put pressure on him to fulfil his responsibilities, both marital and political. In a private interview with Isabella, Bracciano vows never again to sleep with her, but she tries to limit the political damage by making the same vow herself in public. Florence and Monticelso plan to tempt Bracciano into adultery by getting Camillo out of Rome on public business, but Bracciano plots murder: Isabella is killed when she kisses a poisoned picture of Bracciano, and Flaminio breaks Camillo’s neck. Vittoria is arrested and arraigned before an ecclesiastical court; unable to prove her complicity in Camillo’s murder, it convicts her of adultery and commits her to a house of convertites. Flaminio feigns madness to evade interrogation. Lodovico witnesses Isabella’s murder. He returns to Rome illegally to bring Florence the news, but his pardon is signed after the event. He and Flaminio meet and quarrel. Florence borrows from Monticelso a list of villains, in order to select an agent of vengeance. By writing a letter feigning love for Vittoria, he induces Bracciano to contrive her escape from the house of convertites, disguised as a page. Monticelso is elected Pope; his first act is to excommunicate Bracciano and Vittoria. Florence sends Lodovico to Padua to assassinate Bracciano, and by trickery makes it appear that Monticelso supports the conspiracy. Bracciano marries Vittoria and accepts into his court Mulinassar, a Moorish soldier, who is actually Florence in disguise.
