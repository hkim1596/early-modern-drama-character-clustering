Her Father shall be in the Campe releiu'd, She grac'd in Court, how will she braue you then? If suffer this take all? why the meanest Lady Would neuer brooke an equall? you a Princesse? And can you brooke a base competitor? As euer Hellen was. The ioyfull day's to morrow. Passe this plunge And we are made for euer. Thee friend I should haue knowne? Thou seru'st the Generall Sforsa. How doth thy noble Lady, faire Lauretta? They haue left Millaine long, reside they here Neere to the City Florence? I pray thee recommend me to them both, And say, It shall goe hard with mine affaires But Ile find season'd houres to visit them. Tell them, The newes that they are well Is wondrous pleasing to me, and that power I haue in Millaine is reseru'd for them, To worke them into grace: I can but smile, To see how close I haue plotted thier exile. Now businesse calls me hence: farewell. Who can acuse her, speake? what probabilities? What ground? the place? the meanes? the season how Shee did become corrupt? Produce the witnesse; and behould, I stand The Champion for her honour, and will auerre Her Chaste, aboue degree; infinitely honest: Oh Prince! what, can you ground such iniury Vpon vaine heare-say? Speake for your selfe, take spirit Was the slaue neuer Christen'd, hath hee no name? Doe not stand off my Lord, If she be wrong'd, shee's not much behind-hand. What if you find her chaste? Then ioyne their hands. All goes not well, This iugling will be found, Then where am I then? would I were safe in Millaine. Here Matchiuell th'wast hatcht: Could not the same Planet inspire this pate of mine with some Rare stratagem, worthy a lasting Character: No, 'twill not be; my braine is at a non-plus, For I am dull. My Lord. I am turn'd Foole, Asse, Iddeott; Are they married? But the hell is, That they must lie together, ther's the Deuill. And then we are disgrac'd and sham'd. Why you would make A man—midwife, woo'd you? I haue no skill. Peace, interrupt me not, I ha'te: so to reuenge mee vpon her Whom most I hate. To Strumpet her 'twere braue. Youle make me mad my Lord: And in this sweet reuenge, I am not onely Pleas'd (with iust satisfaction for all wrongs) But the great Prince most palpably deceiu'd. If youle eate grapes vnripe, edge your owne teeth, Ile stay the mellow'd season, doo't your selfe, Vnlesse you giue me time for't. Peace, giue me way my Lord, so shall the Prince Bee palpably deceiu'd, Faire Iulia's honor Most prosperously deseru'd, The Duke my master, Freed from all blame, Wane hindred, Peace confirm'd, And I secur'd; Oh I am fortunate Beyond imagination! Hee was a a meere Asse That rais'd Troys Horse: 'twas a pritty structure. Synon, a foole, I can doe more With precious Gold, then hee with whining Teares. Pray my Lord, giue mee Fiue hundred crownes. See how you stand on trifles; when our liues, Your honour; all our fortunes lie a bleeding: What shall I haue the Gold? I know a desolate Lady, whom with Gold I can corrupt. But say I cannot win her, They bide the brunt of all, heere let them stay, With these fiue hundred Crownes Ile poast away. Lady bee happy, and from this blest houre Euer reioyce faire Virgin, for I bring you Gold, and Inlargement; with a recouerie Of all your former losse, and dignitie, But for a two houres labour: Nay, that no labour Nor toyle, but a meere pleasure. Haue wee not heere Too many ares? You haue seene the Prince of Florence? Is he not for his Feature, Beauty, Goodnesse, The most Compleate? So absolute in all things. How happy doe you thinke that Lady then That shall Inioy him? Nay, that shall bee the first To prooue him, And exchange Virginitie, Were't not bright Lady a great happinesse? Loue him: What's she doth not, if shee haue eyes? Were I my selfe a Woman: I would lay My selfe a prostitute vnto the Prince: Shee is not wise that would refuse him Lady. To thee sweete Lady: I offer all these pleasures, Oh happie fate that hath selected mee To be your raiser: Lady take this gold, But that's not all: For there are greater honours Prepared for you; the Duke of Millaine doth Commend him to you: Iulia his daughter Hath in her honour late miscarried, Now 't lies in you to salue and make all good. Yes, in her, Shee hath the power to make the Duke her friend, Iulia her sister, and all Millaine bound To offer vp for her thier Orrisons. This night lie with the Prince In Iulia's stead: There's way made for you, Who would not woo, for what you are wooed too? Nay Lady, heare me out; You shall preserue her honour, gaine the Duke, Redeeme your fortunes: Strengthen you in friends, You shall haue many Townes and Turrets standing, Which future Warre may ruine: Thinke on that.
