Full speech

A question right necessary to be moued, For therby many errors shall be reproued, It were a great errour for any man to beleue That your loue dyd deserue that Christ shold forgeue Your synnes or trespasses, or any synne at all: For so to beleue is an errour sanaticall. And how can your loue desyre forgiuenesse of your yl Seing that the law it is not able to fulfill? The law thus commaundeth as touchyng loue: Thou shalt loue thy Lord God as it doth behoue, With al thy hert, with al thy soule, & w t al thy stre ̄ gth, And thy neighbor as thy self. He saith also at length: There was neuer man borne yet that was able, To performe these preceptes iust, holy, and stable, Saue onely Iesus Christ, that lambe most innocent Which fulfilleth the law for suche as are penitent: But loue foloweth forgiuenesse of synnes euermore, As a fruict of faith, and goth not before, In that parable which vnto you he recited, Wherin he declared your sinnes to be acquited, He called you a detter not able to pay. Then your loue paid not your dets perceiue you may The forgiuenesse of your sinnes you must referre, Only to Christes grace, then you shall not erre. Of this thing playn knowledge you may haue In these wordes go in peace thy fayth doth thee saue. So by faith in Christ you haue Iustification Frely of his grace, and beyond mans operation, The which Iustification here I do represent, Which remayn with all suche as be penitent. Here commeth loue a speciall fruicte of Faith, As touchyng this, heare mekely what he saith.

Plot summary

Plot — The Life and Repentance of Mary Magdalene
Christ has recently come to Jewry, and Infidelity is keen to interfere with his work: his plan is to influence the religious establishment against Christ and thereby ensure his crucifixion. Meanwhile, he encourages Mary Magdalen to enjoy her youth while she can, and introduces her to three companions who will help: Pride, Cupidity, and Carnal Concupiscence (all of whom go under less damaging names). They recommend fashionable clothes and hairstyles, which will attract men, but also advise her against marriage. Mary meets The Law of God and Knowledge of Sin, and experiences the stirrings of conscience: she despairs, thinking herself surely damned and God unjust. Christ casts seven devils out of Mary and drives away Infidelity: she is given the grace to repent. Simon the Pharisee is suspicious of Christ because he is lower-class and uneducated, and invites him to dinner. During the meal, Mary washes his feet with her tears and anoints them. Simon is outraged, but Christ emphasizes the universality of forgiveness: Mary is a genuine penitent, whereas Simon is merely a hypocrite wanting to show off his wealth and presumed holiness. Simon is offended and sets Infidelity and Malicious Judgement to follow Christ, spy on his preaching, and have him brought to trial. Mary ends the play in the company of Love and Justification, who emphasize that she is saved through her faith alone.
