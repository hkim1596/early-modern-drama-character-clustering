CAESAR, Liue long, Another forme. and happy, great, and royall CAESAR , The gods preserue thee, and thy modestie, Thy wisedome, and thy innocence. Guard His meekenesse, IOVE , his pietie, his care, His bountie— Hee's euery thing. I'le doe with others. So will I. And I. Men grow not in the state, but as they are planted Warme in his fauours. Reade, reade 'hem, open, publiquely, reade 'hem. Honour'd, and noble! Good, and great SEIANVS! How! how!) to the highest, and most conspicuous point of greatnesse, and (wee hope) deser uingly; yet, not without danger: it being a most bold hazard in that sou'raigne, who, by his particular loue to one, dares aduenture the hatred of all his other subiects. O! good, good.) Though we could haue wished his zeale had runne a calmer course against A GRIPPINA , and our Nephewes, howsoeuer the opennesse of their actions, de clared them delinquents; and, that he would haue remembred, no innocence is so safe, but it reioyceth to stand in the sight of mercie: The vse of which in vs, hee hath so quite taken away, toward them, by his loyall furie, as now our clemencie would be thought but wearied crueltie, if we should offer to exercise it. NOw great SEIANVS, you that aw'd the state, And sought to bring the nobles to your whip, That would be CAESARS tutor, and dispose Of dignities, and offices! that had The publique head still bare to your designes, And made the generall voyce to eccho yours! That look'd for salutations, twelue score off, And would haue pyramid's, yea, temples rear'd To your huge greatnesse! now, you lie as flat, As was your pride aduanc'd. And praise to MACRO, that hath saued Rome. Liberty, liberty, liberty. Lead on, And praise to MACRO, that hath saued Rome.
