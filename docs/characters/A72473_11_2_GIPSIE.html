WHere the Cacklers, but no Grunters, Shall uncas'd be for the Hunters, Thoſe we ſtill muſt keepe alive; I, and put them out to thrive In the Parkes, and in the Chaſes, And the finer walled places; As Saint James-es, Greenwich, Tibballs, Where the Acornes plumpe as Chibballs, Soone ſhall change both kinde and name, And proclaime'em the Kings game. So the act no harme may be Unto their keeper Barnabee; It will prove as good a ſervice, As did ever Gipſie Jervice, To our Captaine Charles the tall man, And a part too of our Salmon. AS my Captaine hath begun With the Sire, I take the Sonne, Your hand Sir. Of your fortune be ſecure, Love, and ſhe, are both at your Command Sir. See what States are here at ſtrife, Who ſhall tender you a Wife, A brave one; And a fitter for a man, Then is offer'd here, you can Not have one. She is Siſter of a ſtarre, One the nobleſt now that are, Bright Heſper. Whom the Indians in the Eaſt, Phoſphore call, and in the Weſt, Hight Veſper. Courſes even with the Sunne, Doth her mighty brother runne, For ſplendor. What can to the marriage night, More then morne, and evening light Attend her? Save the promiſe before day, Of a little James to play Hereafter. Twixt his Grandſiers knees, and move All the pretty wayes of love, And laughter. Plot summary

Plot — The Gypsies Metamorphosed
A troop of gypsies arrive with children and stolen meat, and proceed to read the palms and tell the fortunes of the King, the Prince, and various members of the aristocratic audience. Some rustics turn up, attracted by the sound of the Gypsies’ dancing. They decide to have a dance themselves with their wenches, and collect money to pay the musicians. The Gypsies steal their purses and other possessions, but then return the items to their pockets by legerdemain. The rustics are interested in the Gypsies’ place of origin, Devil’s Arse, and ask where the name came from. A song is sung by way of an answer, telling of how the first captain of the Gypsies, Cock-Lorel, entertained the Devil to a banquet, with numerous different varieties of human flesh on the menu; the Devil blew the feast away with a fart and so gave the place its name. The Gypsies are metamorphosed into white-skinned lords, and praise the King. C-Text After the Cock-Lorel song, the rustics are seized with the desire to become Gypsies themselves, and the Patrico tells them what a long process their initiation would be.
