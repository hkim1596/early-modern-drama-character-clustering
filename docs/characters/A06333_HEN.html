Peace doting foole. Ile not ſubſcribe to this indignity, Ile not be call'd a King but be a King; Allow me halfe the Realme, giue me the North, The Prouinces that lye beyond the Seas, Wales and the Iſles that compaſſe in the mayne. Our mothers liberty is ſome content. The Kingdome. And why my Lord? What's that to me, by Acte of Parlament, If they be mine confirm'd, he muſt be pleaſ'd. Why hayre-brain'd brother can yee brooke no ieſt? I doe confirme you Earle of Nottingham. I and Moorton too. Richard I haue had wrong about his wardſhip. No matter for his bill let him come in. Welcome good Skinke thou iuſtly doſt complaine, Thou ſtandſt in dread of death for Roſamond, Whom thou didſt poyſon at our dread commaund, And the appointment of our gratious Mother; See heere my Fathers hand vnto thy pardon. Beſides thou ſueſt to haue ſome maintenaunce, We haue bethought vs how wee will reward thee, Thou ſhalt haue Rowden Lordſhip. Your lands? it is our gift and he ſhall haue it. Take Gloſter to you Liefetenant of the Tower, Keep him aſide till we conferre a while, Father you muſt ſubſcribe to his committing. I prethee Faukenbridge be patient, Gloſter muſt of force anſwere this contempt. Why be it ſo, yet ſtay with him a while, Till we take order for the company That ſhall attend him, and reſort to him. Heere take your charge, let no man ſpeake with him, Except our ſelfe, our brethren, or Earle Leiceſter. Yes Faukenbridge thou ſhalt. No but the houſholde ſeruants of the Fleete. On our diſpleaſure ſee he ſpeake no more. And wee'll by land through the Citty ride, Making the people tremble at our pride. Exeunt with Trum pets two waies. Mother and yceſter adde not oyle to fire. Wrath's kindled with a word, and cannot heare The number leſſe perſwaſions you infort, Haue I not ſworne by that eternall arme That puts iuſt vengance ſword in Monarcks hands, Gloſter ſhall die for his preſumption? What needs more coniuration gratious Mother? And honorable Leyſter marke my words. I haue a Bedrole of ſome threeſcore Lords, Of Gloſters faction, The deſtenies run to the booke of Fates, And read in neuer-changing Characters Robert of Gloſters end he dies to day, So fate, ſo heauen, ſo doth King Henry ſay. Off with theſe ſilkes, my garments ſhall be gray, My ſhirt hard hayre, my bed the aſhey duſt, My pillow but a lumpe of hardned clay: For clay I am, and vnto clay I muſt, O I beſeech ye let me goe alone, To liue, where my looſe life I may bemone. Plot summary

Plot — Look About You
Before the start of the action, Queen Eleanor had her rival, Rosamund, poisoned by Skink, and was imprisoned for it; Skink escaped and has taken the identity of a fortune-telling hermit. Her son Henry and his brothers attempted a coup, and there are now two kings in England, father and son. At a parliament, the younger King Henry offers to split the kingdom with the elder, but alienates his supporters by his readiness to seize their lands. Skink, summoned to the parliament by Prince Richard, is rewarded with Gloucester’s lands, and Gloucester imprisoned when he protests and strikes Skink. Richard, who has an interest in Gloucester’s sister, Lady Falconbridge, promises to free him. Eavesdropping on a meeting between them, Sir Richard Falconbridge sees his wife reject the Prince’s advances. Pursued by the watch, Skink evades arrest by swapping clothes with Redcap, but is taken to the Fleet prison for identification by Redcap’s father, the porter. Gloucester swaps clothes with him in turn and escapes, and finally Skink too gets away by purloining Prince John’s clothes. Lady Falconbridge disguises Gloucester as her husband, fooling the watch sent to search for him. The substitutions cause misunderstandings when Skink (as John) is given a chain by Falconbridge and Gloucester (as Falconbridge) is given John’s sword; in each case the real man denies what his doppelganger has done. The porter of the Fleet is to be hanged for Gloucester’s escape, but the old King is persuaded to reprieve him. Gloucester intercepts the pursuivant, gets him drunk, steals the pardon, and ensures that it reaches its destination. Skink evades capture at the tavern by swapping clothes with the drawer and pretending to have been assaulted by the escaped Skink. John and Falconbridge consult the hermit: John wants to locate Gloucester, and Falconbridge to know for sure whether his wife is committing adultery. Lady Falconbridge also goes there in disguise, leaving Robin Hood standing in for her at home, cross-dressed. She too wants to locate Gloucester, to save him from the malice of John and his mother; John and Falconbridge both arrange assignations with her. Skink resumes the hermit’s identity, tells them how Gloucester escaped, and predicts that they will be able to catch Skink committing a robbery nearby; he then changes his disguise, induces them to fall in a hole, and steals their purses.
