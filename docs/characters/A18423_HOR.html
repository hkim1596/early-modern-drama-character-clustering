Faith, neighbour Rustico, I am euen of your mind. For Ile bide by it, I ha more weeds growes in one Holyday, then in three workie dayes. Indeed 'twer a gay thing if wee could put downe these Holy-dayes, as you say. Me thinks' twer an easie mat ter as well as we ha put downe the Fasting-dayes pell mell, hand ouer head. An wee wood hang together 'twer soone done, but when some are so arseward 'twill neuer be. M r Acuto talke with me, hee speakes like a foole. I haue more wit then twentie such loggerheads. Now Acuto ▪ let vs two conclude what shall be come of these holydaies, and as we agree, I warrant it shall goe. And thou hast almost perswaded me to let them stand. But let vs goe and read a booke I haue at home of the liues of Saints to furnish vs with stronger reasons against our next encounter with these Dunce , and then wee will end this controversie. O Histornoggerfers. a braue word. Ile make a knot of these letters. Now I haue it Histriagerfers. That I will be sure to doe. Stay there. Now gape (he gapes & Hortano looks in his mouth) lets tell, how many teeth ha you? 22. hough, whoos within there? a paire of pincers. Why to pull two teeth out a your mouth for I ha but iust 20. Mary because youle ha me say it iust as you doe, & that I cannot except you & I haue the like number of teeth. I, and Ile measure your tongue, that if it bee longer then mine it may be cut even: for it is my dutie being a gardner, to doe all b line and levell. Yes I make no question, though I lack a few teeth but I can swallow a sullebub as full as you. No? that you did. Ile be iudg'd by all this compa nie. Go too then ▪ I wooll: vp with it once againe. No, not I more then my dog. Let me alone for that: Ile make a shift. Doe you say the word, and if there be ere a one there, Ile finde him I war rant you. Very well: how shood I misse them then? turne me loosse. Ile take it as soone as it peeps out of thy mouth as pa rats doe bread and butter. Ile lay ten pound to nothing, Ile not misse a straws breadth, His-to-ri-o-gra-fers. Law, did no I tell you so? Nay you never had an apter scholler in your life then I am: twool doe you good to teach me. I will: but you need not doubt that it's safe, & will never be lost. But to satisfie you Ile doe it, and that quickly, Histor-cockafurrs. This is your faut: that wood ha me say it so fast: & is a very s urvie word to hit, except a man haue a mouth made thereafter. Thanks gentle Master: and the next time you teach me, weele doe it very closely ant please you. For I doe not meane to be very lavish of my learning, when it shall bee at the biggest in no companie but yours. Sir you are much mistaken. The Tailer is an honest man, and a true man, (although it be impossible) and we are fully agreed they shall stand, & be kept as holy as they were the last yeare. What a villaine is this? then thou wilt not pray for thy selfe? Yes; a man would thinke so; art thou not? What a goose art thou? Is it possible thou canst speake, and yet be no liuing creature? proue this and proue anything. That were strange. How? He that was the last yeare and yet liues, is not. I cannot devise how this should be: for if he liue, how can he chuse but haue a being? This may be. For I haue heard an old saying (and I thinke it bee true) that a man may as soone depart with his honestie, as with any thing he hath. I promise you I am in some doubt of it now: go on. Goe to then: if you bee such a scholler I will not dispute with you about the Holy dayes, but my neighbour Acuto hath beene a scholler as well as you, and hee is able to shew you many reasons why they ought to continue. And he hath fully converted me. Sure their bitternesse pass'd the bou ds of huma nitie. They make me afraid of marying, if such jarres fall out betweene those that be one flesh. How can that be neighbour Rustico? if you can teach me that, you doe much. For I haue heard many say, that it is impossible to tame a shrew by any meanes, except by hauing no woman at all. I thinke this is very likely: but who can endure his wife to doe what she listeth at all times? But I haue heard say ▪ it is better to haue honorable warres, then inconvenient peace. How commest thou to be so cunning in this kinde of argument? But who could abide his wife to teach and con trolle him?
