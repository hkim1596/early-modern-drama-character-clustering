Full speech

He may see your hand perde so he may, I cry you mercy, as angry as a thing of nought: Yea forsooth, she worketh from morning to euening, With the needle, and very well she can inbrayd. By my troth husband you are like to haue no roast meat to night, For I haue had other busines to day in hand: Here is come such another wight, As the like was neuer heard of in this land. Nay alasse poore wench, a while she may tarry, For in faith she hath neither house nor land. Yea either they be Alians, or Aliant sonnes indeed, Who through marriage of English women of late, Hath altered the true English blood and seed, And therewithall English plaine maners and good state. All the naughtie fashions in the world at this day, Are by some meanes brought into England. If by some meanes they be not commanded away, Within a while they will vs all withstand. For here they do not only deuoure and spend; As they be most deuourets truly: But our commodities away they do send, Rob and steale from English men daily. Is there such abeast as you speake off? And will he deuoure none, but women that be old? By Saint Anne, but those words make me afraide, The man knoweth more then we perchance: Much mony for wares you may take in Innes, And besides the same, your charges and expence. Marry good Lord haue mercy on vs all, Husband, I pray you bid him home to our house. By God yongman, come and welcome heartily, And care not greatly what my daughter saith: For though she speake to you somewhat angerly, Yet I warrant you she meaneth no very good faith. You shall be sure to haue good Ale, for that haue no bones, VVithout any chewing, it will go downe right.

Plot summary

Plot — The Pedlar's Prophecy
An itinerant pedlar possesses a stone which confers on him the gift of prophecy. The play shows him encountering a series of social types to whom he utters cryptic, apocalyptic prophecies about the land of Ilion and the city of Tyre, whilst also offering goods for sale. First he touches on family discipline with two parents and their daughter, who is secretly no longer a virgin. Next he meets three working men and deals with economic malpractice, but learns that he stands in danger of being charged with witchcraft. Then he poses as a third party reporting the pedlar’s words to members of the ecclesiastical and legal professions, and focuses on matters of religion and judicial corruption.
