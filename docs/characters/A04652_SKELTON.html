O, vir amplissimus! (Vt scholis dicimus) Et gentilissimus! An Howleglasse To come, to passe On his Fathers Asse; There neuer was, By day, nor night, A finer sight. With fethers vpright In his horned cap, And crooked shape, Much like an Ape. With Owle on sist, And Glasse at his wrist. With Elinor Rumming. To make vp the mumming; That comely Gill, That dwelt on a hill, But she is not grill: Her face all bowsy, Droopie, and drowsie, Scuruy, and lowsie, Comely crinkled, Wondersly wrinkled, Like a rost pigs eare, Bristled with haire. Or Westmister Meg, With her long leg, As long as a Crane; And feet like a plane: With a paire of heeles, As broad as two wheeles; To driue downe the dew, As she goes to the stew: And turnes home metry, By Lambeth ferry. Or you may haue come In, Thomas Thumbe, In a pudding fatt With Doctor Ratt.

Plot summary

Plot — The Fortunate Isles and Their Union
Merefool has been duped by the Rosicrucians, and after nine days’ abstinence, he is becoming impatient for results. The invisible spirit Johphiel plays a trick on him, telling him that he is now to assume high office in Castle Rosy-Cross. In the meantime, he wants to see some famous personages from antiquity: he asks for poets and philosophers, but Johphiel can only supply Scogan and Skelton; the figures whom Merefool requested are busy, he explains. Scogan and Skelton appear and present an antimasque of characters from old demotic literature. Merefool is dismissed as a gull. Johphiel announces that the Fortunate Isles of Macaria and Britannia are to be joined. Macaria arrives, bearing its inhabitants. Dancing ensues.
