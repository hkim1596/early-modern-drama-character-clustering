Harke you, me Lord Codigune, By the pones of Saint Tany, you haue prattled to the King a great deale of good Phisicke, and for this one of her good lessons and destructions, how call you it, be Cad, I know not very well, I wil fight for you with all the George Stones, or the Vrsa maiors vnder the Sunnes. Harke you me, Kings: I pray you now, good Kings, leaue your whimbling, and your great proclamations: let death come at her, and ha can catch her, and pray God blesse her. As for the Rebell Monmouth, I kanow very well what I will do with her. I will make Martlemas beefe on her flesh, and false dice on her pones for euery Conicatcher: I warrant her for Case bobby and Metheglin: I will make her pate ring noone for all her resurrections and rebellions. Cousin Caradoc, well, in all these pribble prab bles, I pray you, how dooth our vncle Cadallan ? bee Cad, I heard he had got a knocke: if it bee so, I pray you looke that the leane Caniball, what doe you call him that eate vp Iulius Cesars and Pompeyes : a saucy knaue, that cares no more for Kings, then lowsie beggers & Chimney-swee pers. I, I, Death, a poxe on her: as Cad shudge mee, hee will eate more Emperours and Kings at one meale, then some Taylors halfepenny loaues, or Vsurers decayed shen tlemen in a whole yeare: therefore I pray you Cousin, haue a care of her vncle. In heauen! why did you let her goe thither? Angells! Cots blue-hood: I warrant her, there is ne're a Lawyer in the whole orld, but had rather haue e leuen shillings, then the best Anshell in heauen. I pray you who sent her thither? Monmouth ! Iesu Christ! did hee send her vncle to Saint Peters and Saint Paules, and not suffer her cousin Morgan to bid her Nos Dhi u ? harke you, Cousin, Ile seeke her out be Cad. Farewell, Cousin, Ile make her pring packe her Nuncle with a venshance. God plesse her. Be Cad, Kings, all the Sybilles in the whole orld speake not more tales and prophesies, then our Cousin Morgan: Looke you now Kings, our cou sin Caradoc, and our cousin Constantine, breake our fasts with mince-pyes and Gallymawfryes of legs and armes. Is your Grace a hungry? If you bee, I haue prought you a Calues head in wooll, bee Cad; tis in my Knappesacke. Cads blue-hood, know not our Cousin? Ile giue her such a blow on the pate, Ile make her know her cousins. Cads zwownes, hee had best tell her, he knowes not her nose on her face. This fellow was porne at hogs Norton, where pigges play on the Organ. Posts call you her? Sploud, were a simple Carpenter to build house on such posts: not know our Cousins? But harke you me, sonne Morion ; is all this true, or inuented of her owne foolish pates and imaginashions? Her tell her true in that; tis the prauest Nati on vnder the Sunnes for that Harke you me, sonnes; be Cad, it is a great teale petter to be a thiefe, then a lyar, I warrant her. I warrant her, tis for more knocks on the pate. Romans call you her? Be Cad, scuruy Romanes, that can not let her alone, in her own Countries. Ile choke some of her with cause bobby, or drowne her in hogsheads of Perry and Metheglin. I pray you, from whence come her? From Rome! And I pray you, what a poxe ayles her, that you cannot keepe her at home? haue you any Waspes in her tayles? or liue Eeles in her pelly, you cannot keepe her at home? Harke you me: I pray you, how toth M. Cesar ? toth he neede era parbour? Looke you now: let him come to Wales, and her Cousin Caradoc shall trim his crownes, I warrant her. Cads blue-hood, fight with Teuils. I warrant her, some Embassadors from Belzebubs shortly. Here's a great teale of sturies. I pray Cad plesse her from T uils. They are a great teale worse then Marshall men, and Bum-Bayly. From all of them, Cood Lord deliuer her. I come, Cousin. I warrant her. By shesu, tis a hote whore. Harke you me, you remember our Cousin Ca radoc and Morgan, do you not? Giue me your hands. Be Cad, I shall loue the Teuill, til breath's in her pody, for this tricke. Be Cad, he hath done more good then any Iustice of Peace this seuen yeres, for all her stocks and whipping posts. Harke you me now.

Plot summary

Plot — The Valiant Welshman (Caradoc the Great)
The Earl of. Monmouth seeks to usurp old King Octavian’s throne, and has won two significant victories over him. Cadallan and his sons come to support Octavian. Cadallan is killed in the ensuing battle, but his son Caradoc avenges him by killing Monmouth. Re-established on the throne, Octavian rewards Caradoc with marriage to his daughter and heir, Guinevere. This displeases his bastard son, Codigune, who had regarded Guinevere as his only obstacle to the succession. King Guiderius has failed to pay tribute to the Romans, so the Emperor Claudius invades Britain. Guiderius appeals to Octavian, and Caradoc is sent, fresh from his wedding, to offer military aid. In his absence, Codigune conspires with Gloucester and Cornwall: they poison Octavian and imprison Guinevere, and Codigune seizes power. Gloucester writes warning Guiderius not to trust Caradoc, so Guiderius, about to go into battle, orders the Welsh troops to observe from a hilltop and intervene only ifa British defeat seems likely. Caradoc disregards this, joins the battle in disguise, and bests Claudius in hand- to-hand combat; he refuses Claudius’ offer to buy his freedom, but accepts a token of the Emperor’s friendship, a golden lion. Prince Gald is impressed by Caradoc’s single-handed contribution to the British victory, accompanies him back to Wales, and marries his sister Voada. Finding Codigune installed as King of Wales, Caradoc offers to settle matters in single combat. Codigune is beaten, refuses an offer to make him Viceroy of North Wales, and flees to the Romans. Cornwall submits to Caradoc, who is crowned King. Gloucester flees to make more mischief: he persuades a witch to create a ravaging monster which Caradoc will be expected to fight, and which will kill him. The Romans send Caradoc an ultimatum, requiring him to resign the throne to Codigune; at the same time, he is summoned to deal with the monster, a giant serpent. He gives priority to the serpent, and defeats it with the help of a magic herb, then disposes of the witch and forces her son Bluso to swear that in future he will use his magic powers only for good. Gloucester hangs himself. While Caradoc is away, Cornwall hands over the town of Gloucester to the Romans. Gallicus, the Roman legate who brought the ultimatum, falls in love with Voada and abducts her. Bluso enables Gald to visit her bedchamber, magically unseen.
