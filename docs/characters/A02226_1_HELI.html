Full speech

What be these agonies indefinite? These sudden changes, secret, violent, Both argue euill lucke, and ill intent. How growes this change? Reueale this secret work: Both cures, and wounds doe oft together lurcke. T'is rashnesse to commit our right to chance. Vnfold this factious clowdie mysterie; What cannot help, yet will experience be. Their craft, and power against you thus combui'd How could you shunne? or worke the Basshas so, As they might leaue to seeke your ouerthrow? Alaham! I grant these trialls be seuere: But know; temptation is misfortunes spie, To worke in resolution change, or feare: Attend your fathers death; still hold you there; Before to vndermine a Monarchy Is hard. Besides, iudge you your own intent: For such your brother is in this to you, As you before vnto your brother were; He hath his owne, and you liue out of feare. Will you exceed his mischiefe whom you blame? Goodnesse is only at the greatest best. Thou art but one: for all a sufferer be. Faults to the State all priuate faults exceed. Let father moue thee: Pittie thou the State. Fame euer liues, and euer will defame: The ruine of thy father; and his Crowne. Yet feare thy selfe, if fame thou doest not feare; Reuenge falls heauie, when God doth forbeare. Alaham! If hands you on your father lay For priuate ends, and make the Church your stayres, By which you clime your owne ambitious way; Your glory will be short, and full of feares: Since nothing for the Church is done amisse; And nothing well done that against her is. Then see the means: For though the end were good, Yet for a priuate man to change a State, With Monarchs sleights to alter Monarchie, Seemes hard, if not impossible, to me. What hope can blot the feare of Princes power? These hopes are poore: For feare is with the ̄ mixt. Dissolue (tis true) you may with enuy, feare, Craft, treacherie, contempt, neglect; Not build: these sands will no foundation beare: These engines are to ruine, not erect. Will you a father, can you a King throw downe? The Christians with what faith, or policy, Can you call in? Such remedies are ill: For what they conquer, that enioy they will. Besides, the force lies in Caine Basshas hands, In Mahomet wealth, law, and gouernment: What way to them? Who would bestow his wife in works of shame? The shame is sure; the good in hazard lies.

Plot summary

Plot — Alaham
Before the start of the action, an oracle foretold that the kingdom’s security depended on the death of the King’s eldest son, Zophi; but the pashas, ruling during the King’s senility, merely banished him. Hala, wife of the younger son, Alaham, has had a series of affairs with pashas: first Muhammad, who was forced into exile as a result, then Cain. Seeking to secure his position, Alaham has recalled Muhammad, ===== PAGE 189 ===== 1600 but Muhammad has recalled Zophi in turn, cutting Alaham out of the succession. Hala seeks to make Cain king, and persuades him to murder Muhammad; but he changes his mind after Muhammad warns him of Alaham’s ambition: Cain will tell Alaham that Muhammad is dead, but in reality he will go into hiding. Meanwhile Alaham corrupts the guard, blinds his father and brother, and seizes power. When Cain tells him of Muhammad's death, he is sent for execution — if not for murder then for lying about it, as he is told when he tries to save himself. Alaham’s sister Caelica helps Zophi and their father to take sanctuary in the temple. Alaham has the city searched, finds Caelica, and arrests her. Her cries bring the old King out of hiding, and he too is arrested. The King, Caelica, and Zophi are all burned to death; as they die, their souls call out for vengeance. Seeking to avenge Cain’s death, Hala sends Alaham a poisoned robe, and plans to kill their son before his face, so that the other child, Cain’s bastard, will inherit the kingdom; but she mistakenly kills the wrong infant. The robe kills Alaham; Hala intends to kill herself too.
