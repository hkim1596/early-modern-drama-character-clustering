Full speech

How doe you Sir Andrew, will you send for some qua uite for me, I haue had no drinke neuer since the last great raine that fell. Nay by gad, then you lie, for all y'are Sir Andrew, I was a dapper ogue in Portingall voiage, not an inch broad at the heele, and yet thus high, I scornd I can tell you to be druncke with raine water then Sir, In those golden and siluer dayes: I had sweete bitts then Sir Andrew : how doe you good brother Timothy ? Neuer in bride-wel I protest, as Ime a virgin: for I could neuer abide that bride-wel I protest, I was once sicke, and I tooke my water in a basket, and cary'd it to a doctors. Yes Sir: you arrant foole there was a vrinall in it. The Doctor told me I was with child, how many Lords Knights, Gentlemen, Cittizens, and others promist me to be god-fathers to that child: twas not Gods will: the prentises made a riot vpon my glasse-windowes the Shroue-tuesday fol lowing and I miscaried. oh, they as soone as they come to their lands get vp to London, and like squibs that run vpon lynes, they keepe a Spitting of fire, and cracking till they ha spent all, and when my squib is out, what sayes his punke, foh, he stinckes. Me thought this other night, I saw a pretty sight, Which pleased me much. A comely country mayd, not squeamish nor afraid, To let Gentlemen touch. I sold her maiden-head once, and I sold her maiden-head twice, And I sould it last to an Alderman of Yorke. And then I had sold it thrice. mary muffe, sing thou better, for Ile goe sleepe my old sleepes. Exit.

Plot — Northward Ho
Luke Greenshield has failed to seduce Moll Mayberry in her husband’s absence; now he dines with Mayberry at Ware and describes how he slept with an unnamed wife, showing him her wedding ring. Mayberry grows jealous and hurries back to London to confront Moll, but is satisfied by her explanation that the ring was stolen. Seeking revenge, he has her play up to Greenshield’s advances on his next visit to the house. Greenshield arranges lodgings at one of Mayberry’s houses for himself and Kate, a young woman whom he introduces as his sister, but who is actually his wife. Jenkins hides during Bellamont’s meeting with Doll, and hears her ease her conscience by confessing that she is a common whore; he proceeds to blacken her name among her other lovers. Doll blames Bellamont and swears vengeance. The characters now gravitate back northward to Ware. Featherstone plans an assignation there with Kate, using a ruse to get Greenshield out of London. Mayberry encourages Greenshield to think that Moll is also going there for sexual purposes, and they follow together, accompanied by Bellamont, Philip, and others.
