The Masculin gender is more worthy then the feminine, therfore Licio backare. What then? Pulchrè sanè. Gods blessing on thy blewe nose, but Licio, my mistres is a proper woman. I care not for her qualities, so I may embrace her quantitie. I and so expert, that I can aswel tel the thoughts of a womans heart by her eyes, as the change of the weather by an almanack. No, but faithful and seruiceable. I pray thee doe. Nothing els? Proceed. I would my bed were a hazard. Nothing, but that I would haue her head there among other balles. O that I were a partridge head. That she might tire with her eyes on my coun tenance. Scilicet. Thats a leaden dagger in a veluette iheath, to haue a black tongue in a faire mouth. Then will I mutter, a rope for Parrat, a rope. O monstrous mouth! I would then it had been a sheepes eye, and a neates tongue. Sweetly meant. Doth she want eares? Why then if one aske her a question, it is likely she wil hearken to it. Then belike there she weares her wedding ring. Let vs go farther Licio, she hath both vs in the wind. What, is she beetle browed? What lips hath she? What is then the chin? I perceiue you are driuen to the wall that stands behind the dore, for this is ridiculous: but now you can say no more of the head, begin with the purtenances, for that was your promise. Notes quoth you, I note one thing. That if euerie part require so much as the head, it wil make the richest husband in the world ake at the heart. Neither shalt thou Pipenetta, for first, they are too little for thy bodie, and then too faire to pull ouer so fowle askinne. Worse and worse. Why, is it not on her head? No, except this be it, to haue ones haire lie all night out of the house from ones head. The deuil it is. I would he would geue me a good boxe on the eare, that I might haue a golden cheeke. What difference to be beaten with gold, and to be beaten gold? Tush Licio, thou art vnlettered, al the earth is an egge, the white, siluer, the yolk, gold. Blirt to you both, it was layd by the Sunne. I weep ouer both your wits, if I proue in euerie respect no difference between an egge and golde, will you not then graunt gold to be an egge? Gold wilbe crackt: A common saying, a crackt crowne. Nay then I see thou hast a broken wit. So wil an eg. An egge is rosted in the fire. So is gold tried in the fire. An egge (as Physicions say) will make one lustie. And who knowes not that gold will make one frolike? Egges potcht are for a weake stomach, & golde boyld, for a consuming bodie. An egge is eaten at one sup, and a portague lost at one cast. Egs make custards, and gold makes spoones to eat them. Only this ods I finde betweene mony and egs, which makes me wonder, that being more pence in the world than egs, that one should haue three egges for a peny, and not three pence for an egge. Mops I pittie thee, gold hath egs, change an an gel into ten shillings, and all those peeces are the angels egges. We durst not go to Bacchus, for if I see a grape, my head akes.
