How now ſweet Lord, whats the matter? Marry in the galery, where your Lordſhip left him. Before your Lordſhip do proceed too far, Let me be bould to intimate thus much; That what ſo ere your wiſedome hath t'expoſe, Be it the waightieſt and moſt rich affaire, That euer was included in your breaſt, My faith ſhall poiſe it, if not— And me thinks another may be as fine as he. I thanke you Lady: I know you can flout. How ſhall we rid him hence. Before God, ſhe is a ſweet wench. Troth well my Lord, but ſhall I ſpeake my mind. She is deriud too meanely to be wife To ſuch a noble perſon, in my iudgement. Come you are ſo ſententious my Lord. God ſaue thee Iaques.
