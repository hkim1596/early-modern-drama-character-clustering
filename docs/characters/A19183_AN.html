Full speech

What fooles they are that seem most wise in loue, How wise they are, that are but fooles in loue: Before I was a Louer, I had reason To iudge of matters, censure of all sorts, Nay I had wit to call a Louer foole, And looke into his folly with bright eyes, But now intruding Loue dwels in my braine, And frantickly hath shouldered reason thence, I am not old, and yet alas I doate: I haue not lost my sight, and yet am blind, No bondman, yet haue lost my libertie, No naturall foole, and yet I want my wit. What am I then, let me define my selfe, A doatar yong, a blind man that can see, A wittie foole, a bond-man that is free. Now where is your instructio ̄ ? wheres the wench? Where are my hopes? where your directions? How many parsons are there? What frantike humor doth thus haunt my sence, Striuing to breed destruction in my spirit? When I would sleepe, the ghost of my sweete loue, Appeares vnto me in an Angels shape, When I am wake, my phantasie presents As in a glasse, the shadow of my loue: When I would speake, her name intrudes it selfe Into the perfect ecchoes of my speech. And though my thought beget some other word, Yet will my tongue speake nothing but her name: If I do meditate it is on her, If dreame on her, or if discourse on her, I thinke her ghost doth haunt me, as in times Of former darknesse old wiues tales report, Here comes my bitter Genius, whose aduice Directs me still in all my actions. How now, from whence come you? Take here my hand & faithful hart to gage, That I will neuer tempt you more to sinne: This my request is, since your husband doates Vpon a leaud lasciuious Curtezan, Since he hath broke the bands of your chaste bed, And like a murderer sent you to your graue, Do but go with me to my mothers house, There shall you liue in secret for a space, Onely to see the end of such leaud lust, And know the difference of a chaste wifes bed, And one whose life is in all loosenesse led. My faith is pawnd, ô neuer had chaste wife, A husband of so leaud and vnchast life. Tis true as I relate the circumstance, And she is with my mother safe at home, But yet for all the hate I can alledge Against her husband, nor for all the loue That on my owne part I can vrge her too, Will she be wonne to gratifie my loue. I thinke euen as a vertuous Matron should; It may be that report may from thy mouth Beget some pittie from her intie heart, And I will vrge her with it presently.

Plot summary

Plot — How a Man May Choose a Good Wife from a Bad
Arthur has fallen out with his wife: he is bored with her, convinced she is always trying to put him down, and also just wants a change. When the couple’s fathers visit in an effort to achieve marital harmony, they enter without knocking and find Arthur beating his wife. Her father, Old Lusam, wants to take her home, but she won’t go; she also resists the efforts of Anselm to seduce her and rejects his criticisms of her husband. The fathers take her to Justice Reason in an attempt to have him intervene, but she will not co-operate and Reason will not get involved. Arthur becomes attracted to Mary, a courtesan whom the schoolmaster Aminadab also loves. Seeing her attentions to Arthur makes Aminadab suicidal, and he tries to obtain poison from Fuller, pretending he needs it for an infestation of rats; Fuller gives him a sleeping potion instead. Seeking to kill his wife, Arthur takes the ‘poison’ from Aminadab under cover of preventing his suicide, and holds a dinner party. One of the guests is Mary, but Mistress Arthur puts up even with this, only to be given ‘poisoned’ wine by her husband. Mistress Arthur is interred in her family vault. Anselm visits her tomb and she revives when he kisses her. He hides her in his mother’s house, but despite everything she remains faithful to her husband 1601 and resists her rescuer’s advances. Meanwhile Arthur marries Mary, but finds her an unloving, disobedient, spendthrift wife. In an attempt to win her trust, he tells her the guilty secret of his first wife’s death; she immediately puts the information in the hands of the authorities, and Arthur is forced to go on the run. Rejected by his family, Arthur is starving when he calls at the house of Anselm’s mother and meets a young woman who reminds him of (and, of course, is) his ‘dead’ wife. Stricken with conscience, he gives himself up and is brought before Justice Reason. The use of poison, which it is felony to sell, brings first Aminadab, then Fuller into trouble, until Mistress Arthur arrives and the truth comes out. After his experiences, Arthur now knows the difference between a good wife and a bad.
