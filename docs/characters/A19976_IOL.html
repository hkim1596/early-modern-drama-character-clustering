Nay, and his face be good, let mee alone to tricke his fore head, a country-gentlewoman taught me how: But father I wonder how you dare vndertake ſuch a peremptory challenge againſt all co ̄ mers, conſidering you haue beene ſo long troubled with an Ague. And the Preſence were not exceeding empty-ſtomackt, it would neuer diſgeſt ſuch Almes-basket-ſcraps, the very fall & gar bidge of gentry; fie vpon him, he becomes the great chamber worſe then a Gentleman-vſher with wry legges. Had hee not beene of my fathers owne making, I ſhould ha condemnd his taylor for an exceeding botcher. We are your loues detters kind Dametas. As I loue vertue I pittie theſe poore beaſtes, Theſe Syluane comoners, to ſee what taskes Our couetous Forreſters impoſe vpon them, Who not content with impoſt of their breath, (Poore harts,) purſue them ſmiling to their death. Then let me alone, Ile cracke a couple a ſtones betwixt eue rie cherry, rather then ſurfet on em. Well, god a mercy of all curſen ſoules, I was neere the knowledge ont laſt night I can tell you. Some maintaine it is. Got in the eye, conceiued in a kiſſe: Others whoſe ſpeech ſeeme neere akin to truth Say tis a paſsion, bred ith heate of youth, Some callt a ſigh, and ſome an amorous grone, All differ in the definition, But in the allowd opinion of moſt, Tis neuer truly had till it be loſt. But lying thus alone, as maydes doe vſe, Me thought I dreampt, as maydes can hardly chuſe, And in my dreame me thought twas too much wrong A prettie maid ſhould lie alone ſo long: With that a gallant comes, gallants can doe Much with young maydes, In our wedding ring, Time, place, and howre, indeede in euery thing: The day appointed, and each thing in frame, I thought each howre an age vntill it came, VVell, come it is, the morning once in ſight, I thought it tenne times longer till twas night. At dinner time me thought I ſweld with pride To be drunke to by name of Miſtris bride, Muſicke ſpake loude, no delicates were ſcant, Yet ſtill me thought another thing did want, For ſure thought I, theres ſomething in a man That wiues loue well, hope brides may wiſh it than. Long lookt for comes at laſt, to bed we goe. Lets caſt a choice, the neereſt two take one. Ile lead. We haue both one mind. In leauing the old folke behinde. Whither? I am ſure you would not? I hope you would not hurt me, and you ſhould. Sowre, ſo I heard you ſay. Of what acquaintance pray? None of my Maidenhead Father, I am gone, The Amaſon hath wonne one. The caſt I doe. Nay ſcrape out that. Me yours? Much: in my tother hooſe. I take your lay. Pardon me mother, Ile beare all or none. Me? Looke to the Dukes ſtanding Madam. Barre ſtealing Father; yet and all hit right, Heer's one would ſteale a piece of fleſh to night. Then all your game's bold wrong: furniſh you with neceſ ſaries befitting an eſcape, & my wil ſhal be as ready to take wing, as yours; put in a caſt now mother, or the game is gone indeede. In ieaſt? Tis the faſhion, Much in requeſt among our Nation. Signeor Noe. O tis Manaſſes; and he could make Armes aſwel as he mars Legges, he would grow in great requeſt for Heraldrie: What's your newes? How? Hath any one heere, clipt eares? Of a long tongue thou ſpeakſt verie little.

Plot — The Isle of Gulls
Three members of the audience ask the Prologue about the play they are to see, The Isle of Gulls. They prove to have irreconcilable tastes in drama: one wants satire, one bawdy, and one bombast, but all eventually agree to give the play a chance. Concerned about his daughters’ unsuitable suitors, Basilius retires with them for a year to an uninhabited, fortified island, leaving his brother to rule Arcadia in his stead. A proclamation challenges royal would-be suitors to win the princesses by stealth. This attracts Julio and Amintor to the island, alerted by Basilius’ unworthy favourite Dametas; and also Lysander and Demetrius, respectively disguised as Zelmane, an Amazon, and Dorus, a woodman. ‘Zelmane’ becomes Violetta’s companion, and Demetrius enters Dametas’ service. Dametas arranges an opportunity for Julio and Amintor to rape the princesses by isolating them during a hunt. They kidnap the girls, but ‘Zelmane’ and ‘Dorus’ rescue them. ‘Dorus’ is rewarded with an appointment as Hippolita’s attendant, but the incident also casts doubt on ‘Zelmane’s’ gender, so Lysander loses his access to Violetta. Basilius and Gynecia are both enamoured of Lysander (each believing him to be a different sex), and each persuades him to accept the other’s advances for sport. Amintor and Julio continue to have ill success: they pose as envoys from Basilius’ brother, appealing for him to deal with an outbreak of abuses, corruption, and crime, but they are denied their promised reward by the avaricious Dametas. ‘Dorus’ gets rid of Dametas and his family by sending each of them on a wild-goose chase: Dametas to find buried treasure, his jealous wife Miso to Dametas’ supposed mistress, and Mopsa to the chapel in Adonis’ bower where a priest will marry them. ‘Zelmane’ similarly disposes of Basilius and Gynecia by arranging assignations with each at the same chapel, with the priest Manasses standing in for the Amazon. This frees Lysander and Demetrius to elope with the princesses; but their plans are overheard by Amintor and Julio, whom they then employ to smuggle the girls off the island.
