Plot summary

Plot — The Masque at Lord Hay's Marriage (Apollo's Knights)
Flora and Zephyrus strew flowers over the dancing area in honour of the bride and groom. Two sylvans debate in song the relative virtues of maid and wife. Night appears and upbraids Flora for colluding with Hymen in his theft of one of Diana’s nymphs (that is, the bride). She introduces Apollo’s knights, whom Diana has transformed into trees for attempting to seduce her nymphs. Hesperus brings news that Apollo has pacified Diana, and relays her command that Night change the trees back into men. She does, but makes them dance first. After their metamorphosis, they dance again. Night’s servants pull off the leafy camouflage robes in which the knights came wooing, and more dancing follows. It is getting late, and since the happy couple undoubtedly want to go to bed, Night calls an end to the masque; it concludes with another dance and a song. ===== PAGE 358 =====
