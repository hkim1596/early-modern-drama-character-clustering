¶Fye wyfe how oft ha e thou, thy neighbours gréefe releeued, And why in this thy owne distresse, are all thy sences méeued, Doest thou not know our Lorde and God is able to make right, The weakest parte of simple men, agaynst all Tyrantes spight, Doest thou not know how God himselfe did Toby alwayes ayde, And sent him home his sonne agayne, for whome he was dismayde, And knowest not eake, how that that God, the more to shew his might When Toby had long tyme bene blynde, he gaue to him his sight, Remember how the children thrée for seruing God aright, Were thrust into the burning Ouen, with Terrour and with spight, And how that he defended them, amidst the flaming fyre, And made the Prince prayse him by them, and mittigate his yre, These and a thousand samples mo, Gods goodnesse still doth show, That God will guiltlesse let her dye, good wyfe doe not thinke so. ¶You say right well, and in distresse, friendes comfort euery man, We will go sée her heauinesse, and ease her if we can. ¶And to her noble counsayle Lord, giue wisedome and good helth, Graunt that they doe may glory thée, and mende the common welth.

Plot summary

Plot — The Most Virtuous and Godly Susanna
Satan commissions Ill Report to bring about the downfall of Susanna, a paragon of virtue. Since pride, gluttony, envy, sloth, and covetousness have failed to achieve this, Ill Report decides to use lust. He selects the elders Voluptas and Sensualitas as his instruments of temptation, and they are tempted in turn when they see Susanna. Susanna’s husband Joachim leaves to inspect his realm, leaving the elders to administer justice in his absence. The elders spy on Susanna as she bathes in her orchard after dinner, and proposition her for sex. When she refuses, they slander her with whoredom. Meanwhile Joachim discovers that the elders are corrupt and hated by the people. The elders’ perjured testimony ensures that Susanna is sentenced to death, but God intervenes, sending Daniel to reveal the truth. The elders are stoned, and die penitent. Ill Report is hanged, and a frustrated Satan arrives to carry him to hell and tormenters for his failure. Susanna and her family count their blessings.
