Well done my pretty ones, raine Roſes ſtill, Untill the laſt be drapt: Then hence: and-fill Your fragrant prickles for a ſecond ſhower, Bring Corn-flag, Tulips, and Adonis flower, Faire Oxe-eye, Goldy-locks, and Columbine, Pinkes, Goulands, King-cups, and ſweet Sops-in-wine, Blew Harebells, Pagles, Panſies, Calaminth, Flower-gentle, and the faire-hair'd Hyacinth, Bring rich Carnations, Floure-de-luces, Lillies, The chequ'd, and purple-ringed Daffodillies, Bright Crowne-imperiall, Kings-ſpeare, Holy-hocks, Sweet Venus Navill, and ſoft Lady-ſmocks, Bring too, ſome branches forth of Daphnes haire, And gladdeſt myrtle for theſe poſtes to weare With Spikenard weav'd, and Marjoram betweene, And ſtar'd with yellow-golds, and Meadowes ueene, That when the Altar, as it ought is dreſt, More odour come not from the Phaenix neſt; The breadth thereof Panchaia may envie, The colours China, and the light the skye. 'Tis boldly attempted, and muſt be a Baeotian enterpriſe by the face of it, from all the parts of Greece elſe, eſpecially at this time when the beſt, and braveſt ſpirits of Arcadia, called together by the excellent Arcas, are yonder ſitting about the Fountaine of light, in conſultation of what ho nours they may doe the great Pan by encreaſe of anniverſarie rites fitted to the Muſique of his peace. This is ſhort, though not ſo ſweet. Surely the better part of the ſo lemnitie here will be dauncing. A high one. A moſt politique proviſion. Faith, your folly may deſerve pardon, becauſe it hath delighted: But, beware of preſuming, or how you offer compariſon with perſons ſo neere Deities. Behold where they are, that are now forgiven you, whom ſhould you provoke againe with the like, they will juſtly puniſh that with anger, which they now diſmiſſe with contempt, Away. And come you prime Arcadians forth, that taught By PAN the rites of true ſocietie, From his loud Muſicke, all your manners wraught And made your Common-wealth a harmonie Commending ſo to all poſteritie. Your innocence from that faire Fount of light As ſtill you ſit without the injurie Of any rudeneſſe, Folly can, or ſpight: Daunce from the top of the Lycaean mountaine Downe to this valley, and with neerer eye Enjoy, what long in that illumin'd Fountaine You did farre of, but yet with wonder ſpye. Nay, then they are paſt pittie, let them come, and not expect the anger of a Deitie to purſue them, but meet them. They have their puniſhment with their fact. They ſhall be ſheepe. You ſpeake in time Sir. Now let them returne with their ſolide heads, and carry their ſtupidi tie into Boeotia, whence they brought it, with an embleme of themſelves, and their Countrey. This is too pure an aire for ſo groſſe Braines. End you the rites, and ſo be eas'd Of theſe, and then great Pan is pleas'd. Now each returne unto his Charge, And though to day you have liv'd at large, And well your flocks have fed their fill, Yet doe not truſt your hirelings ſtill. See, yond' they goe, and timely doe The office you have put them to, But if you often give this leave Your ſheepe, and you they will deceave.

Plot summary

Plot — Pan's Anniversary, or The Shepherds' Holiday
It is the shepherds’ holiday, the annual feast of Pan. A group of Boeotians come to Arcadia to challenge the shepherds to a dancing contest. The Arcadians prove the better dancers. The Boeotians make a second attempt, but are turned into sheep and sent home.
