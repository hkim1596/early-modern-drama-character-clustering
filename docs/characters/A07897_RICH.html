God giue his soule a pardon for that sinne. What, doth Matilda smile? That still like sadnesse solitary sat: Then off with widowes weedes, and teach your feete, (That haue forgot for want of exercise, And by the meanes your sorrowe had no meane) To tread a measure: for a gallant crue, Of courtly maskers landed at the staires, Before whome vnintreated, I am come, And haue preuented, I beleeue, their page, Who with his torch is entred. Nay by my faith, we had much merriment; Yet thought it long, you neither came, nor sent. Mowbray, if you stand by, He dares perchance, els will the dastard fly. And Richmond saies as noble Lester saith; Alreadie haue we plighted fame and faith: Which being scornde, returns to vs againe, And by the kings owne mouth, we are discharg'd, Let vs away Bruse, least we come to late, And with vs take some score of men well armde. The Castle gates are shut. what ho? what ho? You that are seruants to the Lady Bruse, Arise, make entrance for your Lord and friends. The worse our fortune, Bruse let vs goe hence, We haue no power to fight, nor make defence. From thee good Winchester: Now y Lord defen . O let not such a thought abuse thy age: Weele neuer yield vs to the tyrants rage. Lester, weele stand like three Battalions: What saies our noble Generall theretoo? Earle Richmond will not yield. O that we three, who in the suns arise, Were (like the three Triumuirates of Rome ) Guids of an hoast, able to vanquish Rome, Are n alone, inclos'd with enemies! The like Richmond desires; & yields his sword. Not of the land, but of a publike life. How cheer'st thou Lester? Goo , sir William Blunt: Passe briefely to the Ladies famishment. But by the way. Tell vs I pray you Blunt, While she remained with you, was she distraught With griefe, or any other passions violent? List Lester, hearst thou not a mournfull march?
