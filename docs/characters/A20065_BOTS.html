We want tooles, Gentlemen, to furnish the trade: they weare out day and night, they weare out till no mettle bee left in their backe; wee heare of two or three new Wenches are come vp with a Carrier, and your old Goshawke here is flying at them. We haue meates of all sorts of dressing; we haue stew'd meat for your Frenchmen, pretty light picking meat for your Italian, and that which is rotten roasted, for Don Spaniardo. We haue Poulterers ware for your sweet bloods, as Doue, Chickin, Ducke, Teale, Woodcocke, and so forth: and Butchers meat for the Cittizen: yet Muttons fall very bad this yeere. Pox a your old face, giue it the badge of all scuruy faces, a Masque. Some Cambricke for my old Lady. A word, I pray, there is a waiting Gentlewomon of my Ladies: her name is Ruyna, saies she's your Kinswoman, and that you should be one of her Aunts. If it please you to bestow the poore labour of your legs at any time, I will be your conuoy thither? Doe you heare? the naked troth is: my Lady hath a yong Knight, her sonne, who loues you, y'are made, if you lay hold vpont: this Iewell he sends you. Doe? they shall all doe if Bots sets vpon them once, she was as if she had profest the trade, squeamish at first, at last I shewed her this Iewell, said, a Kuight sent it her. Copper, Copper, I goe a fishing with these baites. Dam me if— The twenty crownes, sir. Who the pox should come along with you but Bots? Here's a hot day towards: but zounds, this is the life out of which a Soldier sucks sweetnesse, when this Ar tillery goes off roundly, some must drop to the ground: Can non, Demy-cannon, Saker, and Basalisk. So, so: Must I venture first vpon the breach? to you all, Gallants: Bots sets vpon you all. There's no casting ouer boord yet. Gray-beard, Goats pizzle: 'tis a health, haue this in your guts, or this, there: I will sing a bawdy song, sir, be cause your vergis face is melancholly, to make liquor goe downe glib: will you fall on your maribones, and pledge this health, 'tis to my Mistris, a whore? Cap, Ile teach you. Zounds, who dare? This will make you cast em vp better. Pipe with a pox, sir, then, or Ile make your blood dance— Is't Shroue-tuesday, that these Ghosts walke. I, sir? sometimes a figure, sometimes a cipher, as the State has occasion to cast vp her accounts: I'm a Soldier. Bots is my name, Bots is knowne to this Company. A Gentlewoman, my Mother. Me? Sirrr. Gentlemen, Gentlemen, whither will you drag vs? You will answer this. I am what I seeme, Sir, one of Fortunes Bastards, a Soldier, and a Gentleman, and am brought in here with Master Constables band of Bilmen, because they face mee downe that I liue (like those that keepe Bowling-alleyes) by the sinnes of the people, in being a Squire of the body. Yes, sir, that degree of scuruy Squiers, and that I am maintained by the best part that is commonly in a woman, by the worst players of those parts, but I am knowne to all this company. In most of your hottest Seruices in the Low-coun tries: at the Groyne I was wounded in this thigh, and halted vpon't, but 'tis now sound. In Cleueland I mist but little, hauing the bridge of my nose broken downe with two great stones, as I was scaling a Fort: I ha beene tryed, Sir, too, in Gelderland, and scap'd hardly there from being blown vp at a Breach: I was fired, and lay i'th Surgeons hands for't, till the fall of the leafe following. No Soldier, sir? I hope these are Seruices that your proudest Commanders doe venture vpon, and neuer come off sometimes. I wish to be tried at no other weapon. No, your brauest whores haue the worst noses. Your Grace sees I'm sound yet, & no Bullets hit me. A very honest woman, as I'm a Soldier, a pox Bots ye. Is there any Gentleman here, that knowes not a Whore, and is he a haire the worse for that? I doe but trauerse to spy aduantage how to arme my selfe. Nay what art thou prethee? A Thiefe, no, I defie the calling, I am a Soldier, haue borne Armes in the Field, beene in many a hot Skyr mish, yet come off sound. Old Bawd, doe not discredit me, seeme not to know me. Pox ruyne her nose for't, and I be not reuenged for this—vm yee Bitch. Beseech your Grace.

Plot — 2 The Honest Whore (2 The Converted Courtesan)
On his way to court, Hippolito receives petitioners, including the reformed courtesan Bellafront: she wants his help in petitioning the Duke to pardon her husband Matheo, who has been condemned to death for homicide. Hippolito solicits the help of Bellafront’s father Orlando, but he is still morally outraged at her former sexual misconduct. Nevertheless he secretly arranges for Matheo’s release, and enters his household disguised as a servingman, Pacheco. Matheo proves dissolute, and when ‘Pacheco’ gives him money for safe keeping, he gambles it all away. Bellafront is trying not to let poverty and Matheo’s prodigality force her back into prostitution. Hippolito, who converted her from whoredom, now tries to seduce her back again. She sends ‘Pacheco’ to return his love-tokens, but ‘Pacheco’ instead takes them to Infelice, who identifies them as her own gifts to Hippolito. She interrogates Hippolito’s go-between, Brian the footman, then tells her husband that Brian gave her the love-tokens and had an affair with her. Brian is dismissed and goes home to Ireland. Desperate for money, Matheo strips the gown from his wife’s back and sells it. Lodovico promises to furnish him as a gentleman, so he is properly dressed when Orlando visits in his own person. The old man feigns lack of sympathy, then returns as Pacheco in time to prevent Matheo assaulting Bellafront. Prompted by Orlando, the Duke takes a hand: he issues a warrant to arrest Matheo for robbing two pedlars (who were actually Orlando’s men); and, hearing that Hippolito is in love with a whore, but not knowing her identity, passes new anti-prostitution laws. Meanwhile, Candido is married a second time, but his bride tests his patience with unruly behaviour on their wedding day. He determines to tame her, and Lodovico helps in the guise of an apprentice; she submits herself after a mock duel with measuring sticks. Some gallants attempt to seduce her into a brothel, but fail, and Candido still keeps his temper when, drawn to an alehouse for a business deal, he is forced to drink to a bawd’s health. Matheo is arrested at the alehouse on the Duke’s warrant, and Candido too for receiving the cloth stolen from the pedlars. At Bridewell, Matheo admits the robbery, but attempts to incriminate Bellafront out of jealousy for her supposed relationship with Hippolito.
