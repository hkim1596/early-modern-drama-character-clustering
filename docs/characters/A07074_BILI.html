Heere's a noise in Court, you thinke you are in a Tauerne, do you not? Second one another. So haue I this twentie yeare, and yet there was a gen tleman vsher cald me cocks-coombe tother day, and to my face too: wast not a backe-biting rascall? I would I were better tra uaild, that I might haue beene better acquainted with the fashi ons of seuerall country-men: but my Secretary, I thinke he hath sufficiently instructed me. Mary my good Lord quoth hee, your Lordship shall euer finde amongst a hundred French-men, fortie hot shottes: amongst a hundred Spaniardes, threescore bragarts: amongst a hundred Dutch-men, fourescore drunkardes: amongst a hun dred English-men, fourescore and ten mad-men: and amongst an hundred Welch-men. Fourescore and nineteene gentlemen. Why doost thinke I cannot mourne, vnlesse I weare my hat in cipers like an Aldermans heire, that's vile, very olde, in faith. Prowde ynough, and t' will do well ynough; as I walke vp and downe the chamber, Ile spit f ownes about me, haue a strong perfume in my jerkin, let my beard grow to make me looke terrible, salute no man beneath the fourth button, and t'will do excellent. Ile tell you that when the Lady hath entertainde me: but to satisfie thee, heere comes the foole: foole thou shalt stand for the faire Lady. Ile salute her in Latine. I but your Lady can.
