A poore distressed boy Sir, Friendlesse and comfortlesse, that would entreate Some charitie and kindnesse from your worship, I would faine serve Sir, and as faine indeavour With dutious labour to deserve the love Of that good Gentleman shall entertaine me. I can learne any thing That's good and honest, and shall please Master. I would not willingly. Doe you love such boyes, Sir? Doe you speake this seriously? Would you have your Boy Sir Read in these morall mischiefes? And be a well traind youth in all activities? Or doe you this to try me, Fearing a pronenesse. Then take me Sir, and cherish me and love me; You have me what you would: beleeve me Sir I can doe any thing for your advantage, I guesse at what you meane; I can lie naturally, As easily, as I can sleepe Sir, and securely: As naturally I can steale too. Steale any thing from any body living. A rich man, that like snow, heapes up his moneys I have a kind of pious zeale to meet still; A foole that not deserves 'em, I take pittie on, For feare he should run mad, and so I ease him. I scorne all hazard, And on the edge of danger I doe best sir, I have a thousand faces to deceive, And to those twice so many tongues to flatter, An impudence no brasse was ever tougher, And for my conscience. This title, and I ha done sir; I never can confesse, I ha that spell on me; And such rare modesties before a Magistate, Such Innocence to catch a Judge, such ignorance. Have at you. Snap sir. I have walked through all the lodgings. A silence as if death dwelt there inhabits. Nought but a sad confusion thing Every left in such a loose disorder That were there twenty theeves, they would be laden. Certainely there is some cause, some death or sicknesse Is falne suddenly upon some friend, Or some strange newes is come. I thinke so, and sound a sleepe, unlesse it be Some women that keepe watch in a low parlour, And drinke, and weepe, I know not to what end. Why lockt up in that roome. I saw the old Lady, ere she went to bed Put up her plate, and some of the rich hangings In a small long chest, her chaine and rings are there too, It stands close by the Table on a forme. I saw them sad too, and all take their leaves, But what they sayd I was too farre to heare sir. A close one, A backe doore, that the women have left open, To goe in and out to fetch necessaries, Close on the Garden side. Fearefull? Ile be hang'd first. I was then determain'd To have cry'd downe right too, and have kept 'em company, As one that had an interest in their sadnesse, Or made an errand to I know not whom sir. And cleere the Roome of drunken Jealousies, The chest is of some weight, and we may make Such noise ith carriage we may be snap'd. No, no, sir, weele have no shape so terrible, We will not doe the divell so much pleasure, To have him face our plot. Thats too cold a shift, I would not weare the reward of my wickednesse, I wonder you are an old theefe, and no cunninger, Where's the long cloake? Give me the Turbant And the false beard, I heare some comming this way, Stoope, stoope, and let me sit upon your shoulders And now as I direct: stay let 'em enter, And when I touch move forward, make no noise. We have some reason; Things compasd without feate or eminent danger Are too luxurious sir to live upon. It had beene nothing To ha left him in the Porch; I call'd his Servants, With wonders they acknowledg'd him, I pretended It was some spice sure of the falling sicknesse, And that was charitie to bring him home; They rub'd and chaf'd him, ply'd him with strong water, Still he was senselesse, clamors could not wake him; I wished 'em then get him to bed, they did so, And almost smothr'd him with rugges and pillowes; And cause they should have no cause to suspect me, I watch'd them till he wak'd. Plot summary

Plot — The Night Walker, or The Little Thief
The usurer Justice Algripe has seized the wastrel Lurcher’s lands to settle a mortgage; he was also amorously interested in Lurcher’s sister, who has since disappeared. The destitute Lurcher is left to steal for a living; invited to join him, his friend Wildbrain prefers womanizing, on the grounds that it will not end on the gallows. Lurcher is approached by a boy, Snap, and, after ascertaining his skills at deception, takes him into his service. Algripe marries Wildbrain’s cousin Maria, who consented out of filial obedience rather than love: she loves Hartlove. Her mother’s household opposes the match, suspecting that old Algripe is impotent, and Wildbrain arranges for Hartlove to meet her on her way to bed and cuckold Algripe on his wedding night. She refuses his advances and faints when they are found together; she is taken for dead and prepared for burial. Her mother throws Wildbrain out for his part in the incident. Lurcher burgles the house that night, frightening the servants by pretending to be a ghost; but what he supposes to be a chest of jewels is actually Maria’s coffin. Upon discovering what their swag really is, he and his accomplices attempt to bury her in secret, but have a chance encounter with Algripe in the churchyard. Snap frightens him away by impersonating Maria’s ghost, but the thieves are scared off themselves when Maria wakes up in her coffin. Wildbrain meets Hartlove in the dark; Hartlove bears him a grudge and fights him. Maria hears the commotion and pretends to be her own ghost, enjoining them not to fight. Wildbrain is already wounded, and takes refuge in the next house he finds, which belongs to Lurcher’s mistress and partner in crime. They fall for one another and he shows off her gifts to Lurcher, who recognizes them as stolen 1615 property — his own booty. Maria’s mother accuses Algripe of stealing the body; when he refuses to repay the marriage portion, she begins litigation. Lurcher cons his way into Algripe’s house posing as a bookseller, ties him up, and steals his valuables and papers, including his own mortgage. Maria returns to her mother’s household posing as her nurse’s Welsh niece. Struck by the resemblance to dead Maria, Hartlove falls in love with her. Lurcher impersonates a constable and takes Algripe to a church where, he says, the villains who robbed him are hiding out; he then drugs Algripe and stows him in the vault.
