Plot — A Larum for London, or The Siege of Antwerp
Sancho D’Avila needs money to pay his troops, and plans to raise it by sacking Antwerp. He conspires with the captain of the city’s garrison, Cornelius van End, and summons Spanish forces from across the Netherlands. Claiming to be retaliating over Antwerp’s support for the Prince of Orange, he makes an artillery attack on the State House while the rulers are dining there; three people are killed. The Spanish commander, Alva, fakes his own death in order to give a pretext for further reprisals against the city. The rulers of Antwerp, including a fat burgher, do not take the threat seriously, considering defence expenditure to be a waste of money; but they are finally persuaded to accept help from the Marquis d’Havré and Count Egmont, so long as they don’t have to pay for it. Van End leaks the information to D’ Avila. The Spaniards attack and the garrison under van End defects to their side. The rulers of Antwerp struggle to organize their troops. Havré and Governor Champagny are killed, but Egmont is saved by Alva and sent to Spain as a prisoner. Antwerp falls swiftly; thousands of citizens are killed, and the victorious Spaniards execute a further 10,000 survivors in reprisal for 300 Spanish deaths. For the purposes of pillage, the Spanish commanders divide the city between them. Meanwhile the one- legged veteran Stump musters the survivors to resist the invader. D’Avila sets about despoiling his quarter of the city, demanding not only money but sexual favours from a nun; but when he cannot find a safe place to stow her pending her rape, he kills her instead. Other casualties include children, a blind man, and his wife. Van End demands a woman’s treasure, but she pushes him into a vault and Stump stones him to death. The fat burgher is captured by the Spaniards, but neither threats nor tickling can persuade him to reveal where his treasure is hidden. He is rescued by Stump, and offers his hidden gold, but Stump isn’t interested: he refused to pay the soldiers in peacetime, and it is too late now.
