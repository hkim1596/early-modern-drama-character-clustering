Full speech

My long concealed thoughts Marke Anthony, Must seeke discouerie through thy pliant words: Beleeue me Scilla ciuill mutenies, Must not obscure thy glories and our names: Then sith that factious Marius is supprest, Goe spread thy colours midst the Asian fields, Meane while my selfe will watch this Cities weale. Lictors proclaime this our vndanted doome, we will that Marius and his wretched sonnes, His friends Sulpitius, Claudius and the rest Beheld for traytors, and acquit the men That shall endanger there vnluckie lines, And henceforth Tribunes name and state shall cease, Graue Senators how like you this decree? scilla farewell and happy be thy chaunce, VVhose warre both Rome and Romaines must a duaunce. VVhat think you Citizens, why stand ye mute punc; Shall Scilla be Dictator here in Rome? Hereto the Senate frankly doth agree. Your Grace hath small occasions of 〈◊〉 , Nor seeke these Citizens for your disclaime. Then to conclude his happines my Lords, Determine where shall be his Funerall. Plot summary

Plot — The Wounds of Civil War, or Marius and Scilla
King Mithridates has defied Roman power in Asia Minor, and the consul Sulla has been selected by lot to take reprisals. But Sulla lingers with the army in Capua, and the Senate votes to transfer the command to Marius, a more experienced soldier. Sulla invades the Senate with his troops and threatens to uphold his rights with violence. The senators take sides, and a civil war follows. Sulla is victorious; Marius takes refuge at Minturnum. Sulla proscribes Marius and his allies as traitors, and leads the army to Asia Minor. Marius is warned that his hosts plot his murder, but accepts his fate and goes to sleep. The assassin, Pedro, is unable to go through with the killing, so the magistrates simply ask Marius to leave town. He flees to Africa, and lives in the mountains on a diet of roots. Marius’ son comes to recall him to Rome: the new consul, Cinna, is sympathetic to him. But Sulla returns victorious from the east, and attempts to prevent his rival from entering Italy. Nevertheless Marius reaches Rome, and begins to settle old scores with violence. Cinna deposes and murders his fellow consul Octavius, Marius’ enemy, and Marius is appointed to fill the vacancy. Sulla is proscribed and his property confiscated, and Marius sends his son to engage Sulla’s army in battle. Sulla’s wife and daughter are arrested as they attempt to leave Rome; Marius threatens them with death, but then sends them honourably back to Sulla. Marius dies in office. After a bloody battle, Sulla enters Rome victorious and compiles an extensive death-list of his enemies. Young Marius holes up in Praeneste, the last stronghold of resistance, but after a siege he concedes the inevitable and commits suicide; Sulla’s forces conquer the city and put the inhabitants to the sword. Sulla is proclaimed dictator, and insists that the appointment be made perpetual; but he soon finds that supreme power brings with it a troubled mind, and resigns the office. He plans to retire to the country, but a spirit appears with a prophecy of his death. He takes his leave of his family, and is buried with great pomp.
