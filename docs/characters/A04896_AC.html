Thou zealous patron of the winged Boy, Well hast thou pleaded thy blind Archers Case; Pray Iove thou maist deserve a lusty fee For this Herculean labour of thy tongue. When Sol shall make the Easterne Seas his bed, When Wolves and Sheepe shall be together fed; When Starres shall fall, and planets cease to wander, When Iuno proves a Bawd, and Iupiter a Pander; When Venus shalturn Chast, and Bacchus become sober, When fruit in April's ripe, that blossom'd in October; When Prodigals shall money lend on use, And Vsurers prove lavish and profuse; When Art shal be esteem'd, and golden pelfe laid down, When Fame shal tel all truth, & Fortune cease to frown, To Cupids yoke then I my necke will bow; Till then, I will not feare loves fatall blow. Nay, stay faire Nimph, I would request A private Conference with you. I that of late was made of Scythian snow, And Hyperborean ice, am now quite thaw'd In the uncessant flames of hot desire. A new Vesuvius burnes within my brest, But shall I overturne those noble trophies Which I most firmely have on vertue sounded; Or shall I singe the wings of reason so, In the outragious flames of passion; That I must needs fall downe and perish quite In the blacke hideous gulfe of deepe despaire, No: no: I will not, Of this I am resolv'd whatso're befall, Or not to love too much, or not at all. Nay, be assur'd (proud man) not any smart, Can cure the courage of a valiant heart: No force a heart of adamant can breake; And loosers must, and shall have leave to speake. Before this guiltlesse woman shall endure Such shamefull injuries: thy selfe assure Ile empty all these azure rivulets Of their virmilion streames; and quite discharge This contemn'd bulke of mine, of living ayre; And stretch'd upon the gelid bed of death, Ile to the world this Epitaph bequeath, Here lyes a Swaine that spent his deerest blood, To kill a Tyrant for a Virgins good. Now recollect thy spirits Rhodon, Let Spartan resolution spread it selfe Into each angle of thy noble heart. For now our hostile forces are assembled, Covering the fields from Ossa to Olympus. Their painted banners with the windes are playing: Their pamper'd coursers thunder on the plaines: The splendor of their glistring armes repels The bashfull sun-beames backe unto the clouds. Their bellowing drums and trumpets shrill, Doe many sad corrantos sound, Which danger grimand sprawling death must dance. Now therefore Rhodon, doe reflect thy eye Upon the glories of thy ancestours, And strive by emulation to transcend Those trophies which were yet nere paralleld. Th'eleventh at least: for see Orion hath Advanced very high his starry locks in our horizon. They doe but blush to see what crimes are acted By mortall under covert of the night. I saw the blazing meteor stoupe, And bend his course toward the humble Center. He that by honourable meanes is rais'd, And hath his seat establish'd on the square Of never sliding vertue, cannot fall. Who dares contest with Iove, or question what His Soveraigne highnesse shall doe or determine. Too true it is, I shrewdly feare, For every circumstance makes it appeare That Rhodon in the mirtle grove, last night, Had private conference with Iris, From whom (it seemes) he tooke the venom'd potion, For now he doth, in his extremest fits, Exclaime on the untruth of woman kind, Bewailing the unlucky houre that did present Your sister Iris to his sight. Nay, never worse, thou wouldst say, gentle Panace, If thou knew'st all. Rhodon's mishap's the cause of all our sorrow: Rhodon's betray'd, poyson'd, and lies at point of death. Doubtlesse it was the false Ponerias plot, Whom Martagon hath lately entertaind, With her companion; old Agnostus; For, know the malice of your foes is such, That if by open force they can't destroy you, By hidden plots they'll seeke your overthrow. Imperious Martagon, that art no lesse Knowne for thy power, then thy wickednesse: In Rhodons name I doe defie thee here, Who chalengeth the Combat at thy hands, To be aveng'd on thee for thy foule wrongs: But if thou dar'st not in a single fight, Give satisfaction to the noble Shepheard; Then thee and all thy troopes he doth invite, To a bloudy breakfast to morrow morne. Attended by a vigorous army he Stands in the confines of his owne dominions, Swearing that he will prove it in the field, That thou a tyrant and a traitour art. Vpon my life, & by the honor of a souldier it doth. I will great Martagon; and misdoubt not, But that your cheere shall be exceeding hot. Yov must away, for no entreaties can prevaile. Arme, arme: the hostile forces are in sight, And thus come marching on in proud array: The battaile's led by Martagon himselfe, Wherein are marshal'd neere five thousand Bill men, All clad in coats of red: A furious Amazon cald Tulipa, Brings on three thousand burley Swissers, Arai'd in gorgeous Coats of red and yellow; And these make up the vanne: To which are added for a forlorne hope, Two hundred melancholy Gentlemen. The fierce Cynosbatus brings up the Rere, Wherein about two thousand souldiers be Clad all in greene, and arm'd with pikes of steele. Narcissus with a thousand Daffadils, Clad in deepe yellow coats doth flanke The right side of the battaile. The left wing is by Hyacinthus led, Wherein a thousand Souldiers march, Arraid in purple coats.

Plot — Rhodon and Iris
: Aiming to sow dissension among the flowers, Poneria and Agnostus disguise themselves as a virtuous maiden and a wise man. Rhodon has recently breken off his suit to Eglantine, and is now expected to marry Iris. His sister Violetta appeals to him for help against the marauding Martagon, who has trampled on her flowers and forced her to live away from her home. Martagon conspires with Eglantine’s brother to forestall Rhodon’s match with Iris. The jilted Eglantine grows melancholy, but Poneria stops her killing herself and gets her to tart herself up for an assignation with Rhodon. It becomes clear that Martagon has no intention of making restitution to Violetta, so Rhodon will have to take him on by force: a floral war seems inevitable. Iris gives Rhodon a gern which makes the wearer victorious in battle; Violetta gives him a lettuce which guarantees sustenance and prevents poisoning. Martagon suborns Poneria to kill Rhodon, so she gives Eglantine a poison, telling her it is a love-philtre. Posing as Iris, Eglantine gives it to him, claiming it to be a restorative, and expects him to fall in love with her.
