VVe craue fayre goddesse at thy heauenly hands, To haue as euery other creature hath, A sure and certaine meanes among our selues, To propagate the issue of our kinde: As it were comfort to our sole estate, So were it ease vnto thy working hand. Each Fish that swimmeth in the floating sea, Each winged fowle that soareth in the ayre, And euery beast that feedeth on the ground, Haue mates of pleasure to vpholde their broode: But thy Utopians, poore and simple men, As yet bewaile their want of female sex. A sleepe? why see how her alluring eyes, VVith open lookes do glaunce on euery side. O then to blesse the loue of Iphicles, VVhose heart dooth hold thee deerer then himselfe ▪ Do but behold me with a louing looke, And I will leade thee in our sollemne daunce, Teaching thee tunes, and pleasant layes of loue. She winkes and frownes. Now Cyprian Queene, guider of louing thoughts, Helpe Iphicles. How long shall death, incroching by delayes, A bridge the course of my decaying life: Before Pandora loue poore Iphicles? Some gentle words sweet loue. They drawe me on, and yet they put me back. VVhether thou drawe me on, or put me back, I must admyre thy beauties wildernesse. And why not Iphicles as well as you? Nay then tis time to snatch, the head is mine. O sweete Pandora deigne our humble suites. And I by our Dianes holy head. Come let vs goe and weepe our want els where Stesias hath got Pandora from vs all. Exeunt. Vnkind Pandora to delude me thus. To thee Learchus and to Melos false, In this complaint Ile ioyne with thee, let vs go. O foule Pandora, cursed Stesias. Loue is deafe, blinde, and incredulous, I neuer hung about Pandoraes neck, She neuer termd me fayre and thee black swaine. Can I heare this? can I view her? O no. Pale be my lookes to witnesse my amisse. And so is Iphicles. O bury all thy anger in this kisse, And mate me not with vttering my offence. VVhen shall I meet thee, tell me my bright loue. Farewell Pandora, Ile to Stesias. VVherefore did Iupiter create the day? Sweete is the night when euery creature sleepes, Come night, come gentle night, for thee I stay.
