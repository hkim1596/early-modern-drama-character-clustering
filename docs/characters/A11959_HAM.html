Plot summary

Plot — Hamlet, Prince of Denmark
Less than two months. before the start of the action, King Hamlet died suddenly. Since then, his widow, Gertrude, has remarried his brother Claudius, who has become King of Denmark. The kingdom is urgently preparing for war over territory annexed thirty years ago when old Hamlet defeated and killed old Fortinbras, the King of Norway; now Prince Fortinbras is preparing to seize it back Claudius sends ambassadors to Norway to acquaint its King with young Fortinbras’s plans. Laertes is given permission to leave court for France, but Claudius denies Prince Hamlet’s request to return to his studies at Wittenberg, and names him the heir apparent. Laertes takes his leave of his family, and warns his sister Ophelia against Hamlet; their father, Polonius, goes further and forbids her even to speak to the Prince. Hamlet learns that his father’s ghost has been seen on the battlements on three consecutive nights, and joins the sentinels on the fourth night to see for himself. The ghost tells him that King Hamlet was poisoned by Claudius and enjoins him to avenge the murder. Hamlet begins to behave oddly, and Claudius summons his old schoolfellows Rosencrantz and Guildenstern to court to investigate. Polonius believes that he has been driven mad by Ophelia’s rejection, but Hamlet's less than loving behaviour when left alone with her seems to disprove that theory. Claudius is persuaded that his nephew may be dangerous, and arranges for him to be sent to England under escort on a diplomatic mission to demand the resumption of England’s tributary payments to Denmark. But first there is a play to attend: uncertain that the ghost is all that it seems, Hamlet has arranged for a court performance of The Murder of Gonzago, with stage business reproducing the ghost’s description of King Hamlet’s death.
