I'me glad your Highnesse is so pleasant. Madam, by this light I vow my selfe your seruant; onely yours, inespecially yours: time, like a turne-coat, may order and disorder the outward fashions of our bodies, but shall neuer inforce a change on the constancy of my minde, sweet Colona, faire Colona, young and sprightfull Lady, doe not let me in the best of my youth, languish in my earnest affections. That I confesse too; I am euery way so vnworthy of the first fruits of thy em braces, so farbeneath the riches of thy merit, that it can be no honor to thy fame, to rank me in the number of thy seruants, yet proue me how true, how firme I will stand to thy plea sures, to thy command; and as time shall serue be euer thine Now prethe deere Colona. But what? doe not pitty thy owne gentlenesse, Louely Colona; shall I speake? shall I? say But I, and our wishes are made vp. You wil not faile to meet two houres hence, sweet. Plot — 478
Eteocles and Polynices are elected co-kings of Thebes, ruling in alternate years. At the end of the first year, Eteocles refuses to stand down and banishes Polynices. Six champions join Polynices to besiege Thebes, but, after heavy losses, Polynices offers to settle the case by single combat. He and Eteocles kill one another, and Creon takes the crown.
