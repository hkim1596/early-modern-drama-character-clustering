Alas good Brothers, how came this mischance? He would not be so monstrous I am sure. Alas what course is left for vs to liue by then? Shall I be left then like a common road, That euery beast that can but pay his tole May trauel ouer, and like to Cammomil, Flourish the better being trodden on. Did he do so. Why then I see there is no hope of him. Some husbandes are respectles of their wiues, During the time that they are yssulesse, But none with Infants blest, can nourish hate, But loue the mother for the childrens sake. I know not what course to take me to, Honesty faine would liue: What shall I do? Iest not at her, whose burden is too greuous, But rather lend a meanes how to releeue vs. And I am not Sir so vncharitable, To hate the man that loues me. I can, for I loue al the world, but am in loue with none. May I beleeue you? Sir the commendations I haue receiued from Butler of your byrth and worth, together with the Iudgement of mine owne eie, bids me beleeue and loue you. As doth befit your seruant and your wife, That owe you loue and duty al my life. Not till you tel me Sir, Euen to my blood, Whats mine is yours, to gaine your peace or good. Sir I wil shew you al the wealth I haue, Of Coyne, of Iewels, or Possessions, The land I can endow you with, is my Loue, The riches I possesse for you is loue, A Treasure greater then is Land or Gold, It cannot be forfeited, and it shal neare be sold. O me, most miserable. O greefe, that pouerty should ha that power to tear: Men from themselues, tho they wed, bed, and sweare. Vndone, vndone. My husband has forsooke me. Has taine my Iewels, and my Bracelets from me. Left me distrest, and thurst mee forth a doores. Ile sit me downe, sigh loude in stead of wordes, And wound my selfe with griefe as they with swords. Plot summary

Plot — The Miseries of Enforced Marriage
The ward William Scarborow betroths himself to Clare Harcop, but on his return to London he is forced to marry Katherine, the niece of his guardian, Lord Falconbridge. He is rewarded with control of his own’ and his younger brothers’ property. He writes to tell Clare the news of his unwelcome marriage; upon receiving the letter, she dies (possibly by suicide). ===== PAGE 337 ===== 1606 Scarborow takes her death badly: he blames his wife, and becomes prodigal. Ilford fakes his own arrest for debt in order to induce Scarborow to guarantee the supposed loan, thereby defrauding him of his money. This still leaves him with the patrimony of his younger brothers, Thomas and John; but he spends it, and drunkenly assaults his friends and kinsmen. The brothers and the honest family servant Butler are forced into theft to keep themselves from starvation. They steal £300 from Harcop, Clare’s father, which they use to make their sister appear to be a rich woman. Butler tricks Ilford into marrying her, but on learning that she is penniless her new husband throws her out; Butler offers to support her out of his meagre wages. Thomas confronts and fights with his elder brother, but saves his life when they are attacked by Ilford. Now penitent, Thomas declares his intention to confess to Harcop that he and John stole the money, even though it will mean hanging for them both. John falls out with him over this, but they are reconciled by Butler. Dr Baxter, who married Scarborow to Katherine, talks him into repentance. On his deathbed, Lord Falconbridge also repents and leaves the Scarborow brothers double their original wealth in restitution, along with a dowry for their sister.
